# Persona: Domain Expert

## Profile
- **Name:** Professor Elizabeth Warren
- **Age:** 56
- **Role:** Professor of Organizational Behavior & Change Management
- **Institution:** Leading UK Business School
- **Experience:** 30 years in research, teaching, and consulting
- **Location:** Oxford, UK

## Background
Elizabeth is an academic and practitioner expert in organizational change, process design, and project management methodologies. She publishes research, teaches MBA students, and consults with organizations. She evaluates companies based on theoretical soundness and empirical evidence. She's sought after for expert opinions.

## Goals & Motivations
- Advance knowledge in her field
- Ensure practitioners use sound methodologies
- Identify best practices and innovations
- Educate future business leaders
- Consult on complex organizational challenges
- Publish influential research

## Pain Points & Challenges
- Practitioners who misuse or misunderstand theory
- Companies making unsubstantiated claims
- Lack of rigor in applying methodologies
- Fads and buzzwords without substance
- Difficulty distinguishing genuine expertise from marketing
- Limited time to evaluate all vendors and approaches

## What They Look For in a Website
- **Methodology:** Clear explanation of approaches and frameworks
- **Theoretical Foundation:** Evidence of sound theoretical basis
- **Research Base:** Citations, research, evidence-based practices
- **Expertise Credentials:** Team qualifications, education, certifications
- **Thought Leadership:** Original insights and contributions to field
- **Publications:** White papers, articles, research contributions
- **Case Studies:** Detailed examples showing methodology application
- **Industry Recognition:** Speaking engagements, teaching, advisory roles
- **Professional Language:** Appropriate terminology and concepts
- **Intellectual Honesty:** Acknowledging limitations and complexity
- **Continuous Learning:** Evidence of staying current with research
- **Academic Partnerships:** Connections to universities or research institutions

## How They Evaluate Personas
- **Knowledge Sophistication:** What level of expertise does each persona have?
- **Methodology Fit:** Which approaches suit which personas?
- **Learning Orientation:** How interested are personas in understanding theory?
- **Evidence Requirements:** Which personas need research backing?
- **Complexity Tolerance:** Can personas handle complex concepts?
- **Best Practice Adoption:** Which personas follow established best practices?
- **Innovation Openness:** Which personas are open to new approaches?
- **Expertise Appreciation:** Do personas value deep expertise?

## Technical Proficiency

- Advanced: Data analysis, consulting, teaching, framework development
- Intermediate: Business practices, industry knowledge
- Tools: Academic databases, research tools, statistical software, teaching platforms

## Preferred Communication Channels
- Academic journals and publications
- Research papers and studies

- Professional books and thought leadership

## Key Quotes

## Red Flags for This Persona
- Buzzwords without substance
- Methodologies without theoretical basis
- Misusing or misunderstanding academic concepts
- No evidence of continuous learning
- Team without relevant credentials or expertise
- No citations or research backing
- Oversimplifying complex concepts
- Ignoring established best practices
- Fads presented as innovation
- No thought leadership or original insights
