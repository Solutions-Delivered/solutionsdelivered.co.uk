# Persona: Lost / Churned Customer

## Profile
- **Name:** Martin Hughes
- **Age:** 51
- **Role:** Managing Director
- **Company:** Professional services firm
- **Former Customer:** 18 months, left 6 months ago
- **Location:** Cardiff, UK

## Background
Martin was a customer but left due to changing needs, budget constraints, or dissatisfaction with some aspect of service. He might consider returning if circumstances change or if he sees evidence of improvements. He may still speak about the company to others.

## Goals & Motivations
- Find solutions that better meet current needs
- Avoid making the same mistake twice
- Ensure any new provider addresses past issues
- Stay informed about market options
- Justify decisions to stakeholders

## Pain Points & Challenges
- Experienced issues or unmet expectations previously
- May have had better pricing elsewhere
- Services may not have scaled with needs
- Communication or relationship breakdowns
- Changed business circumstances
- Budget constraints that forced change
- Finding replacement services that work better

## What They Look For in a Website
- **Evidence of Change:** Updates, improvements, new offerings
- **Transparency:** Honest acknowledgment of common issues and solutions
- **Customer Success Stories:** Focus on retention and long-term relationships
- **Flexible Options:** Range of service levels and pricing
- **Communication:** Better support and account management
- **Relevance:** Services that address their evolved needs
- **Win-Back Offers:** Incentives for returning customers
- **Testimonials:** Stories from customers who came back
- **Clear Improvements:** What's different now vs. when they left
- **Low-Friction Reconnection:** Easy to reach out again

## How They Evaluate Personas
- **Churn Reasons:** Why do different personas leave?
- **Win-Back Potential:** Which churned personas might return?
- **Feedback Patterns:** What common complaints exist?
- **Competitive Losses:** Who are you losing to and why?
- **Timing:** When might they reconsider?
- **Unmet Needs:** What needs weren't addressed?
- **Relationship Quality:** Was there a relationship breakdown?
- **Value Perception:** Did they feel they got value?
- **Improvements Needed:** What would bring them back?

## Technical Proficiency
- Similar to existing/potential customers
- Industry-specific knowledge and tools
- Experience with competitor solutions
- Comparative mindset from shopping around

## Preferred Communication Channels
- Email (may be wary of pushy outreach)
- LinkedIn (professional, arms-length contact)
- Website content (passive research)
- Industry events (neutral setting)
- Referral from trusted source

## Key Quotes
> "I left for a reason. Show me that reason no longer exists."

> "I'm watching to see if you've actually improved or if it's just new marketing."

> "If you'd fixed [specific issue] when I asked, I wouldn't have left."

## Red Flags for This Persona
- No evidence of improvements or innovation
- Same issues appear to persist
- Ignoring past feedback or complaints
- Pushing same approach that didn't work before
- No acknowledgment of why customers leave
- Aggressive win-back tactics
- No flexibility in offerings
- Poor online reviews or complaints visible
- Stagnant case studies or examples
- No competitive improvements

## Website Evaluation Checklist
- [ ] Is there evidence of continuous improvement?
- [ ] Are new services or capabilities highlighted?
- [ ] Do testimonials address overcoming challenges?
- [ ] Is there flexibility in service offerings?
- [ ] Are support and communication emphasized?
- [ ] Can former customers easily reconnect?
- [ ] Are common issues proactively addressed?
- [ ] Is there transparency about improvements?
- [ ] Are customer success stories prominent?
- [ ] Is there value in staying informed about updates?
- [ ] Does content acknowledge past shortcomings?
- [ ] Are there incentives to reconsider?
