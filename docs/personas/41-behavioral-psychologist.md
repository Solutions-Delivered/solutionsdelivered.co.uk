# Persona: Behavioral Psychologist

## Profile
- **Name:** Dr. James Richardson
- **Age:** 44
- **Role:** Behavioral Science Consultant
- **Specialty:** Decision-making, cognitive psychology, behavioral economics
- **Experience:** 18 years in research and consulting, PhD in Psychology
- **Location:** Cambridge, UK

## Background
James applies behavioral science principles to understand and influence user behavior. He specializes in decision-making processes, cognitive biases, and persuasion principles. He helps organizations design experiences that align with how people actually think and behave.

## Goals & Motivations
- Apply behavioral science to improve user experiences
- Understand cognitive processes underlying decisions
- Design choice architecture that helps users
- Identify and address cognitive biases
- Educate organizations about behavioral principles
- Conduct research on user behavior

## Pain Points & Challenges
- Misapplication of behavioral principles
- "Dark patterns" that manipulate users
- Oversimplification of complex psychology
- Organizations that want shortcuts without understanding
- Ethical concerns about influencing behavior
- Difficulty measuring behavioral interventions

## What They Look For in a Website
- **Choice Architecture:** How choices are framed and presented
- **Cognitive Load:** Complexity and decision burden
- **Persuasion Principles:** Use of social proof, scarcity, authority, etc.
- **Behavioral Nudges:** Subtle influences on behavior
- **Decision Friction:** Barriers or facilitators to action
- **Mental Models:** Alignment with user expectations
- **Anchoring & Framing:** How information is presented
- **Loss Aversion:** Use of gain vs. loss framing
- **Commitment Devices:** Mechanisms encouraging follow-through
- **Feedback Loops:** Immediate response to actions
- **Default Options:** Strategic use of defaults
- **Ethical Application:** Responsible use of behavioral principles

## How They Evaluate Personas
- **Decision-Making Style:** How does each persona make decisions?
- **Cognitive Biases:** What biases affect each persona?
- **Motivation Drivers:** Approach (gain-seeking) vs. avoidance (loss-averse)?
- **Processing Depth:** System 1 (fast, intuitive) vs. System 2 (slow, analytical)?
- **Social Influence:** How susceptible to social proof?
- **Habit Formation:** Are behaviors habitual or deliberative?
- **Risk Tolerance:** Risk-seeking or risk-averse?
- **Temporal Focus:** Present-focused or future-oriented?
- **Locus of Control:** Internal or external attribution?
- **Choice Preference:** Prefer few choices or many options?

## Key Quotes
> "People don't behave rationally—they behave predictably irrationally."

> "Good design works with human psychology, not against it."

> "Understanding why people do what they do is more important than what they say they'll do."

## Red Flags for This Persona
- Dark patterns or manipulative techniques
- Ignoring cognitive load and decision fatigue
- Poor understanding of behavioral principles
- Overuse of scarcity or urgency tactics
- No consideration of cognitive biases
- Assuming rational decision-making
- Misaligned incentives and user goals
- Ignoring loss aversion
- Poor choice architecture
- No feedback or confirmation
- Ethical concerns about behavioral influence
