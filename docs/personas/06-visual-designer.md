# Persona: Visual Designer / Graphic Designer

## Profile
- **Name:** Jordan Matthews
- **Age:** 27
- **Role:** Visual Designer
- **Company Type:** Brand and Digital Design Agency
- **Experience:** 5 years in visual design, background in graphic design
- **Location:** Bristol, UK

## Background
Jordan creates visually compelling designs that strengthen brand identity and engage users. With a foundation in traditional graphic design, they bring strong typography, color theory, and composition skills to digital projects. They balance aesthetic beauty with functional design.

## Goals & Motivations
- Create visually stunning designs that capture attention
- Maintain brand consistency across all touchpoints
- Use visual design to guide users and create hierarchy
- Stay current with design trends while avoiding trendy clichés
- Build a portfolio that showcases creative excellence

## Pain Points & Challenges
- Balancing creative vision with client constraints
- Making designs work across different screen sizes
- Working within existing brand guidelines that feel limiting
- Designs that look great as static mockups but don't translate to code
- Proving the value of "good design" to stakeholders
- Managing feedback from multiple stakeholders with different tastes

## What They Look For in a Website
- **Visual Hierarchy:** Clear focal points, deliberate use of size, color, and contrast
- **Typography:** Beautiful font pairings, appropriate sizes, line heights, and spacing
- **Color Palette:** Cohesive color scheme with purposeful application
- **Imagery:** High-quality, consistent photography or illustration style
- **Layout Composition:** Balanced, harmonious arrangements using grid systems
- **White Space:** Generous breathing room, not cluttered
- **Brand Consistency:** Alignment with brand guidelines and identity
- **Visual Details:** Polished corners, shadows, gradients, textures
- **Icon Style:** Consistent icon family, appropriate weight and style
- **Mood & Atmosphere:** Emotional resonance that matches brand personality

## How They Evaluate Personas
- **Visual Preferences:** What aesthetic appeals to each persona?
- **Brand Perception:** How should each persona feel about the brand?
- **Cultural Context:** Are there cultural considerations for visual design?
- **Age & Generation:** What design styles resonate with their generation?
- **Industry Expectations:** What visual styles are appropriate for their sector?
- **Emotional Response:** What feelings should the design evoke?
- **Design Literacy:** How sophisticated are they about design?
- **Trust Signals:** What visual cues build credibility for them?

## Red Flags for This Persona
- Poor typography (bad font choices, sizes, spacing)
- Clashing colors or no coherent palette
- Low-quality, pixelated, or inconsistent imagery
- Cluttered layouts with no breathing room
- Inconsistent visual style across pages
- Generic stock photos that don't fit the brand
- Overuse of effects (shadows, gradients, animations)
- No attention to visual details (alignment, spacing)

## Website Evaluation Checklist
- [ ] Is the typography hierarchy clear and beautiful?
- [ ] Is there a cohesive color palette applied consistently?
- [ ] Are images high-quality and on-brand?
- [ ] Is there generous white space and breathing room?
- [ ] Is the layout balanced and compositionally sound?
- [ ] Are icons consistent in style and weight?
- [ ] Does the design reflect the brand personality?
- [ ] Are visual details polished (alignment, spacing)?
- [ ] Is the design responsive without compromising aesthetics?
- [ ] Does it create the intended emotional response?
