# Persona: Sales Manager

## Profile
- **Name:** James Mitchell
- **Age:** 43
- **Role:** VP of Sales
- **Company Type:** B2B Professional Services
- **Experience:** 18 years in sales, 8 years in sales leadership
- **Location:** Birmingham, UK

## Background
James leads a sales team focused on consultative B2B sales with longer sales cycles. He's responsible for pipeline management, revenue targets, and sales enablement. He views the website as a critical tool for lead qualification and sales support, not just marketing.

## Goals & Motivations
- Generate high-quality, sales-qualified leads
- Reduce sales cycle length
- Improve lead-to-customer conversion rates
- Equip sales team with effective resources
- Hit quarterly and annual revenue targets
- Build predictable, repeatable sales processes

## Pain Points & Challenges
- Low-quality leads that waste sales team time
- Prospects who aren't properly qualified before contact
- Lack of information about prospect needs and pain points
- Difficulty demonstrating value and ROI to prospects
- Long sales cycles with multiple stakeholders
- Competitive pressure and price objections
- Sales and marketing misalignment

## What They Look For in a Website
- **Lead Qualification:** Forms that capture qualifying information
- **Clear Value Proposition:** Compelling reasons to buy immediately apparent
- **Objection Handling:** Content that addresses common concerns
- **Proof Points:** Case studies, ROI calculators, testimonials, data
- **Sales Enablement:** Resources sales team can share with prospects
- **Pricing Transparency:** Clear pricing or "request quote" options
- **Contact Options:** Phone numbers, chat, demo requests, meeting schedulers
- **Personalization:** Industry-specific messaging or solutions
- **Competitive Differentiation:** Clear positioning against competitors
- **Trust Signals:** Client logos, awards, certifications, security badges
- **CRM Integration:** Leads flowing directly into sales CRM
- **Response Time:** Fast lead notification and routing

## How They Evaluate Personas
- **Buying Power:** Do they have budget authority?
- **Decision-Making Role:** Economic buyer, influencer, or end user?
- **Purchase Intent:** How ready are they to buy?
- **Pain Severity:** How urgent is their need?
- **Budget Range:** What's their likely spend level?
- **Company Size:** Do they fit our ideal customer profile?
- **Sales Cycle:** How long will it take to close them?
- **Objections:** What concerns will they raise?
- **Competitors:** Who else are they considering?
- **Sales Approach:** What's the best way to engage them?
- **Lifetime Value:** What's their revenue potential?

## Technical Proficiency
- Expert: Sales process, negotiation, relationship building, consultative selling
- Advanced: CRM systems (Salesforce, HubSpot), sales enablement tools, pipeline management
- Intermediate: Basic marketing concepts, lead scoring, email automation
- Tools: Salesforce, LinkedIn Sales Navigator, Calendly, Zoom, sales engagement platforms

## Preferred Communication Channels
- Sales pipeline dashboards and reports
- Lead quality and conversion metrics
- Competitive battlecards
- Case studies and proof points
- ROI calculators and proposals

## Key Quotes
> "Marketing brings me leads. I need qualified opportunities."

> "Our website should pre-sell prospects before my team even talks to them."

> "If prospects can't quickly see why we're different, we'll compete on price—and nobody wins that game."

## Red Flags for This Persona
- Generic contact forms with no qualifying questions
- No clear value proposition or differentiation
- Missing case studies or proof points
- No pricing information or guidance
- Difficult to find contact information
- No clear next steps or CTAs
- Long, complex forms that discourage submission
- No CRM integration for lead capture
- Missing trust signals and credibility markers
- Content that doesn't address buyer objections

## Website Evaluation Checklist
- [ ] Do forms capture qualifying information?
- [ ] Is the value proposition immediately clear?
- [ ] Are case studies and proof points prominent?
- [ ] Is there pricing transparency or clear next steps?
- [ ] Are multiple contact options available?
- [ ] Do trust signals build credibility?
- [ ] Is competitive differentiation clear?
- [ ] Does content address common objections?
- [ ] Are CTAs strategic and compelling?
- [ ] Does the site integrate with CRM?
- [ ] Can prospects self-qualify and self-educate?
- [ ] Are there resources for the sales team to use?
- [ ] Is the sales process clear and logical?
- [ ] Are there industry-specific examples?
- [ ] Can prospects easily book meetings or demos?
