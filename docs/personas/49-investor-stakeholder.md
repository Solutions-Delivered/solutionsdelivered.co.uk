# Persona: Investor / Stakeholder

## Profile
- **Name:** Richard Blackwell
- **Age:** 54
- **Role:** Managing Partner, Private Equity Firm
- **Investment Focus:** Professional services and B2B companies
- **Experience:** 28 years in private equity and corporate finance
- **Location:** London, UK

## Background
Richard evaluates companies for investment, acquisition, or board participation. He assesses market position, growth potential, management quality, and business fundamentals. The website is his first impression of the company's professionalism, positioning, and potential.

## Goals & Motivations
- Identify promising investment opportunities
- Assess company quality and potential
- Evaluate management team and capabilities
- Understand market position and differentiation
- Gauge growth trajectory and scalability
- Minimize investment risk

## Pain Points & Challenges
- Limited transparency in private companies
- Difficulty assessing true market position
- Evaluating intangible factors like culture and brand
- Time constraints for evaluating opportunities
- Separating marketing claims from reality
- Understanding competitive dynamics
- Assessing management team quality remotely

## What They Look For in a Website
- **Company Information:** History, size, growth, leadership team
- **Leadership Bios:** Backgrounds and track records of executives
- **Market Position:** Clear positioning and competitive differentiation
- **Growth Indicators:** Expansion, new services, market traction
- **Client Portfolio:** Quality and diversity of client base
- **Financial Signals:** Revenue indicators, employee growth, office locations
- **Media Coverage:** Press releases, news, industry recognition
- **Awards & Recognition:** Industry accolades and validation
- **Thought Leadership:** Executive visibility and expertise
- **Professional Presentation:** Credibility and professionalism
- **Scalability Indicators:** Repeatable processes and systems
- **Strategic Direction:** Clear vision and market opportunity

## How They Evaluate Personas
- **Market Size:** How large are the addressable persona segments?
- **Growth Potential:** Which personas represent growth opportunities?
- **Profitability:** Which personas drive the best margins?
- **Competitive Position:** How strong is position with each persona?
- **Retention:** Which personas have best retention rates?
- **Scalability:** Can the company scale to serve more of each persona?
- **Diversification:** Is revenue diversified across personas?
- **Market Trends:** Are persona segments growing or declining?
- **Competitive Risk:** How vulnerable is each persona segment?

## Technical Proficiency
- Expert: Financial analysis, business valuation, market assessment, due diligence
- Advanced: Strategic analysis, industry knowledge, risk assessment
- Intermediate: Market research, competitive analysis
- Tools: Financial modeling tools, research databases, industry reports

## Preferred Communication Channels
- Annual reports and financial statements
- Investment memorandums
- Executive presentations
- Market analysis reports
- Due diligence materials

## Key Quotes
> "Your website is my first due diligence. If it looks amateurish, I'm moving on."

> "I'm looking for signals of growth, professionalism, and market leadership—not marketing fluff."

> "The management team is often more important than the business model. Show me who's running this."

## Red Flags for This Persona
- Unprofessional or amateurish presentation
- Missing or vague company information
- No leadership team information
- Generic or commodity positioning
- No evidence of growth or traction
- Missing client or market validation
- No media coverage or recognition
- Inconsistent or outdated information
- Signs of stagnation
- Unclear competitive positioning
- Poor financial signals (single location, small team)
