# Persona: UX Researcher

## Profile
- **Name:** Dr. Marcus Thompson
- **Age:** 38
- **Role:** Lead UX Researcher
- **Company Type:** Tech Company (B2B SaaS)
- **Experience:** 10 years in user research, PhD in Human-Computer Interaction
- **Location:** Manchester, UK

## Background
Marcus transitioned from academic research to industry UX research. He specializes in both qualitative and quantitative research methods, from user interviews and usability testing to large-scale surveys and analytics analysis. He advocates for evidence-based design decisions.

## Goals & Motivations
- Uncover real user needs through rigorous research
- Influence product decisions with data and insights
- Build empathy for users across the organization
- Establish research as a core part of the design process
- Validate (or invalidate) assumptions before development

## Pain Points & Challenges
- Lack of time or budget for proper research
- Stakeholders who make decisions based on opinions, not data
- Difficulty recruiting representative participants
- Research findings that get ignored or deprioritized
- Balancing speed with research quality
- Translating research insights into actionable recommendations

## What They Look For in a Website
- **User Testing Evidence:** Clear signs that real users were involved in the design process
- **Behavioral Patterns:** Design that accommodates how people actually behave, not how we wish they would
- **Problem-Solution Fit:** Content and features that address genuine user pain points
- **Mental Models:** Navigation and structure that matches user expectations
- **Accessibility:** Inclusive design that works for diverse users
- **Data Collection:** Analytics setup, A/B testing capability, feedback mechanisms
- **User Journeys:** Clear paths that align with different user goals
- **Edge Cases:** Consideration of unusual scenarios and diverse user needs

## How They Evaluate Personas
- **Research Foundation:** Are personas based on actual user research or assumptions?
- **Sample Size:** How many users were interviewed or surveyed?
- **Research Methods:** What methods were used (interviews, surveys, observation, analytics)?
- **Behavioral Focus:** Do they emphasize behaviors and goals over demographics?
- **Data Points:** Are specific quotes, statistics, and examples included?
- **Validation:** Have personas been validated with real users?
- **Negative Personas:** Are excluded or edge-case personas documented?
- **Update Frequency:** How often are personas refreshed with new research?
- **Segmentation Logic:** Is the segmentation meaningful and actionable?

## Red Flags for This Persona
- Generic personas based on stereotypes or assumptions
- No evidence of user testing or research
- Design decisions that contradict known user behavior
- Missing consideration of diverse user needs
- No feedback mechanisms or data collection
- Ignoring analytics or user complaints
- Over-reliance on stakeholder opinions

## Website Evaluation Checklist
- [ ] Is there evidence of user research in the design?
- [ ] Do user flows match real user mental models?
- [ ] Are there clear personas documented somewhere?
- [ ] Does the site track meaningful user behavior metrics?
- [ ] Are there mechanisms for user feedback?
- [ ] Do error messages reflect actual user problems?
- [ ] Is the navigation structure user-tested?
- [ ] Are accessibility needs considered?
- [ ] Do CTAs align with user goals?
- [ ] Is there a plan for ongoing research and iteration?
