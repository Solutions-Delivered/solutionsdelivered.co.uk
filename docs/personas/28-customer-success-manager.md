# Persona: Customer Success Manager

## Profile
- **Name:** Laura Bennett
- **Age:** 32
- **Role:** Customer Success Manager
- **Company Type:** B2B Professional Services
- **Experience:** 6 years in customer success and account management
- **Location:** London, UK

## Background
Laura manages relationships with existing customers, ensuring they achieve their goals and get value from services. She's responsible for retention, expansion, and customer satisfaction. She needs resources to support customers and demonstrate ongoing value.

## Goals & Motivations
- Ensure customer success and satisfaction
- Reduce churn and increase retention
- Identify expansion and upsell opportunities
- Proactively address issues before escalation
- Demonstrate value to justify renewals
- Build strong customer relationships

## Pain Points & Challenges
- Limited resources to support all customers equally
- Difficulty proving ROI to customer stakeholders
- Reactive rather than proactive customer support
- Competing priorities between customers
- Lack of visibility into customer health signals
- Customers not adopting all available services
- Renewal negotiations and price objections

## What They Look For in a Website
- **Customer Resources:** Knowledge base, best practices, templates
- **Success Stories:** Case studies to share with customers
- **Service Documentation:** Clear descriptions of all offerings
- **Expansion Opportunities:** Additional services customers might need
- **Self-Service Portal:** Customer access to information and support
- **Training Materials:** Resources to help customers succeed
- **ROI Tools:** Calculators or frameworks to demonstrate value
- **Support Resources:** Internal knowledge base for CSM team
- **Customer Communication:** Templates, guides, and talking points
- **Product Updates:** Information about new features or services
- **Community:** Customer forums or networking opportunities
- **Metrics Dashboard:** Tools to track customer health

## How They Evaluate Personas
- **Success Metrics:** What does success look like for each persona?
- **Adoption Barriers:** What prevents them from getting full value?
- **Expansion Potential:** Which personas are likely to expand?
- **Churn Risk:** What early warning signs indicate risk?
- **Support Needs:** What level of hand-holding do they need?
- **Relationship Preference:** High-touch or low-touch engagement?
- **Value Drivers:** What matters most to each persona?
- **Stakeholder Complexity:** Who else influences their satisfaction?
- **Usage Patterns:** How actively do they engage with services?

## Technical Proficiency
- Expert: Customer success best practices, relationship management, account planning
- Advanced: CRM systems (Salesforce, HubSpot), customer health scoring
- Intermediate: Basic business analytics, presentation skills
- Tools: CRM, email, presentation software, customer success platforms

## Preferred Communication Channels
- Customer health dashboards
- Account plans and success plans
- Customer journey maps
- Case studies and success stories
- Internal knowledge base

## Key Quotes
> "My job is to make customers so successful they can't imagine working without us."

> "Churn happens long before the customer tells us they're leaving. I need early warning signals."

> "I need resources that help customers self-serve, so I can focus on strategic relationships."

## Red Flags for This Persona
- No customer resources or knowledge base
- Missing success stories to share with customers
- No clear information about additional services
- Difficult for customers to get support
- No tools to demonstrate ROI
- Missing documentation or service descriptions
- No customer community or engagement opportunities
- Lack of training materials
- No self-service options for customers
- Poor customer portal experience

## Website Evaluation Checklist
- [ ] Is there a comprehensive customer resource center?
- [ ] Are success stories and case studies prominent?
- [ ] Is all service information clearly documented?
- [ ] Can customers easily access support?
- [ ] Are there tools to demonstrate value/ROI?
- [ ] Are training materials available?
- [ ] Is there a customer community or forum?
- [ ] Can customers self-serve common needs?
- [ ] Is there documentation for expansion services?
- [ ] Are product/service updates communicated?
- [ ] Can CSMs find internal resources easily?
- [ ] Is there a good customer portal experience?
