# Persona: Content Strategist

## Profile
- **Name:** Rachel Green
- **Age:** 36
- **Role:** Head of Content Strategy
- **Company Type:** Content Marketing Agency
- **Experience:** 10 years in content strategy and planning
- **Location:** Bristol, UK

## Background
Rachel develops comprehensive content strategies that align with business goals and user needs. She oversees content planning, governance, editorial calendars, and content audits. She believes content should be strategic, purposeful, and measurable—not just filling space.

## Goals & Motivations
- Create content that drives business results
- Align content with user needs and search intent
- Establish content governance and quality standards
- Build sustainable, scalable content systems
- Measure and demonstrate content ROI
- Create cohesive content experiences across channels

## Pain Points & Challenges
- Stakeholders who want content without strategy
- Limited resources for content creation
- Difficulty measuring content effectiveness
- Content silos across departments
- Outdated or duplicate content
- Lack of content governance or maintenance
- Balancing SEO with user experience

## What They Look For in a Website
- **Content Architecture:** Logical structure and information hierarchy
- **Content Inventory:** Complete understanding of all content
- **Content Gaps:** Identifying missing content for user needs
- **Content Lifecycle:** Clear creation, review, and retirement processes
- **Editorial Calendar:** Planned, consistent content publication
- **Content Guidelines:** Style guide, tone of voice, quality standards
- **Content Types:** Appropriate mix of formats (articles, videos, infographics)
- **User Intent Mapping:** Content aligned with user questions and needs
- **Content Measurement:** Analytics on performance and engagement
- **Content Reusability:** Modular content that can be repurposed
- **SEO Integration:** Content optimized for search and discovery
- **Content Governance:** Roles, workflows, and approval processes

## How They Evaluate Personas
- **Content Needs:** What information does each persona seek?
- **Content Format Preferences:** Articles, videos, podcasts, infographics?
- **Reading Level:** What literacy level and language complexity?
- **Content Depth:** Quick answers or comprehensive deep dives?
- **Content Journey:** What content do they need at each stage?
- **Topics of Interest:** What subjects matter to each persona?
- **Questions and Pain Points:** What questions does content need to answer?
- **Content Triggers:** What prompts them to seek content?
- **Sharing Behavior:** Which personas amplify and share content?
- **Content Frequency:** How often do they consume content?

## Technical Proficiency

## Key Quotes
> "Content without strategy is just stuff. Strategy without content is just talk."

> "Good content answers questions users are actually asking, not questions we wish they'd ask."

> "If you can't maintain it, don't create it. Content debt is real."

## Red Flags for This Persona
- Content without clear purpose or audience
- Duplicate or conflicting content
- Outdated content that hasn't been reviewed
- No content governance or ownership
- Missing content for key user needs
- Content that doesn't align with business goals
- No measurement of content effectiveness
- Inconsistent tone or quality
- Content silos across departments
- Publishing for the sake of publishing
