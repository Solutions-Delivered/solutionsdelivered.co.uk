# Persona: Service Designer

## Profile
- **Name:** Priya Sharma
- **Age:** 36
- **Role:** Service Design Lead
- **Company Type:** Service Design Consultancy
- **Experience:** 9 years in service design, previously in business strategy
- **Location:** Birmingham, UK

## Background
Priya takes a holistic view of user experiences, mapping entire service ecosystems across digital and physical touchpoints. She works with organizations to design end-to-end experiences that span websites, apps, call centers, retail spaces, and back-office processes.

## Goals & Motivations
- Map complete user journeys across all touchpoints
- Identify pain points and opportunities throughout the service
- Design seamless transitions between channels
- Align front-stage (customer-facing) and back-stage (operational) processes
- Create service blueprints that guide implementation

## Pain Points & Challenges
- Siloed teams that don't communicate across touchpoints
- Lack of visibility into back-end processes
- Inconsistent experiences across channels
- Difficulty getting all stakeholders aligned
- Limited budget for cross-functional workshops
- Measuring impact across the full service journey

## What They Look For in a Website
- **Journey Continuity:** How the website fits into the broader service ecosystem
- **Channel Integration:** Smooth handoffs to other channels (phone, email, in-person)
- **Touchpoint Consistency:** Aligned messaging, branding, and experience across channels
- **Service Recovery:** Clear paths when things go wrong
- **Support Access:** Easy ways to get help (chatbots, contact forms, phone numbers)
- **Progress Tracking:** Ability to see status of requests or orders
- **Account Management:** Self-service for common tasks
- **Omnichannel Experience:** Start on one device/channel, finish on another
- **Emotional Journey:** Design that acknowledges user feelings at each stage
- **Ecosystem Thinking:** Integration with third-party services and partners

## How They Evaluate Personas
- **Journey Stages:** What are the persona's stages (awareness, consideration, purchase, usage, advocacy)?
- **Touchpoint Preferences:** Which channels do they prefer at each stage?
- **Emotional States:** How do they feel at different points in the journey?
- **Service Expectations:** What do they expect from the overall service, not just the website?
- **Pain Points:** Where do they experience friction across touchpoints?
- **Support Needs:** When and how do they seek help?
- **Cross-Channel Behavior:** Do they use multiple channels in their journey?
- **Relationships:** What's their relationship with the brand over time?
- **Contextual Factors:** What external factors influence their journey?

## Red Flags for This Persona
- Website feels disconnected from other channels
- No clear path to human support when needed
- Inconsistent information across touchpoints
- Starting a process online but can't complete it
- No ability to track progress or status
- Poor handoffs between digital and physical
- Ignoring emotional states in critical moments
- No recovery options when things go wrong

## Website Evaluation Checklist
- [ ] How does the website connect to other service touchpoints?
- [ ] Can users easily transition to other channels?
- [ ] Is messaging consistent with other touchpoints?
- [ ] Are support options clearly available?
- [ ] Can users track progress of their requests?
- [ ] Does the experience acknowledge emotional states?
- [ ] Are there clear recovery paths for errors?
- [ ] Can users complete tasks fully online?
- [ ] Is there continuity across devices?
- [ ] Does the website set appropriate service expectations?
