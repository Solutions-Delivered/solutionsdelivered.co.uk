# Persona: Business Owner / Seller (M&A Target)

## Profile
- **Name:** Graham Peterson
- **Age:** 58
- **Role:** Owner & Managing Director
- **Company:** Established consulting firm (25 employees, £2.5M revenue)
- **Situation:** Considering exit options / succession planning
- **Location:** Nottingham, UK

## Background
Graham built his business over 20 years and is now exploring exit options. He's considering retirement, pursuing other opportunities, or simply wants to take chips off the table. He's evaluating potential acquirers based on cultural fit, treatment of employees, valuation, and legacy preservation. He's cautious and wants to ensure his life's work goes to the right buyer.

## Goals & Motivations
- Achieve fair valuation for life's work
- Ensure employees are taken care of
- Preserve company legacy and reputation
- Find cultural fit with acquiring company
- Smooth transition and exit
- Retirement security or capital for next venture
- Maintain client relationships and quality

## Pain Points & Challenges
- Emotional attachment to the business
- Concerns about cultural fit with acquirer
- Worry about employee treatment post-acquisition
- Uncertainty about business valuation
- Fear of making wrong decision
- Limited knowledge of M&A process
- Concerns about client continuity
- Legacy and reputation protection
- Deal structure complexity (earn-outs, etc.)

## What They Look For in a Website
- **Company Stability:** Evidence of established, stable company
- **Cultural Values:** Shared values and approach to business
- **Growth Trajectory:** Acquiring company is growing, not struggling
- **Employee Treatment:** How they treat their people
- **Client Relationships:** Approach to client service and relationships
- **Acquisition Experience:** History of acquisitions and integration
- **Leadership Team:** Who they'll be working with or handing over to
- **Market Reputation:** Industry standing and reputation
- **Geographic Reach:** Where they operate (expansion opportunity)
- **Service Alignment:** Complementary or similar services
- **Financial Health:** Signs of financial stability and backing
- **Strategic Vision:** Where the company is headed
- **Professional Presentation:** Credibility and professionalism
- **Contact for M&A:** Clear business development or M&A contact

## How They Evaluate Personas
- **Client Continuity:** Will the acquirer serve my clients well?
- **Employee Fit:** Will my employees thrive in the new organization?
- **Market Position:** Does this enhance or diminish market position?
- **Service Alignment:** Do our customer bases align?
- **Cultural Match:** Will our clients and employees fit their culture?
- **Growth Potential:** Can they grow the business I built?
- **Integration Ease:** How smooth will the transition be?
- **Value Preservation:** Will they maintain what made us successful?

## Technical Proficiency
- Expert: Industry knowledge, business operations, client management
- Advanced: Business strategy, financial management, P&L management
- Intermediate: M&A basics (learning), legal and tax implications
- Tools: Business financial software, industry systems, advisors

## Preferred Communication Channels
- Direct, confidential conversations
- Introduction through trusted advisors or brokers
- Professional M&A materials
- LinkedIn professional outreach
- Industry events and connections

## Key Quotes
> "I've spent 20 years building this business. I need to know it's going to the right home."

> "My employees are like family. I need assurance they'll be treated well."

> "Valuation matters, but it's not everything. Cultural fit and legacy matter more than an extra 10%."

## Red Flags for This Persona
- Unprofessional or aggressive acquisition approach
- No evidence of successful past integrations
- Cultural misalignment (cutthroat vs. relationship-focused)
- Poor employee treatment or high turnover
- Financial instability or struggling business
- No clear strategic rationale for acquisition
- Reputation concerns in the industry
- Pushy or high-pressure tactics
- No understanding of the business being acquired
- Missing acquisition track record
- Poor client relationship approach

## Acquisition Decision Factors (Priority Order)

1. **Cultural Fit** - Do our values and approaches align?
2. **Employee Treatment** - Will my people be taken care of?
3. **Client Continuity** - Will clients get same or better service?
4. **Legacy Preservation** - Will they honor what I've built?
5. **Valuation** - Is the price fair and structured well?
6. **Leadership Trust** - Can I trust the acquiring leadership?
7. **Integration Approach** - How smooth will the transition be?
8. **Strategic Fit** - Does this make strategic sense?
9. **Financial Security** - Is the deal structured to minimize risk?
10. **Post-Acquisition Role** - What's expected of me (if anything)?

## Key M&A Website Needs

**Essential Information:**
- Clear statement about acquisition interest or M&A contact
- Evidence of past successful acquisitions and integrations
- Employee testimonials from acquired companies
- Client retention stats post-acquisition
- Cultural values and employee treatment philosophy
- Leadership bios showing experience and character
- Financial stability indicators
- Strategic vision and growth plans

**Trust-Building Elements:**
- Case studies of successful integrations
- Employee and client retention after acquisitions
- Awards and industry recognition
- Long-term client relationships
- Stable leadership team
- Professional, established presentation
- Thought leadership and industry respect
