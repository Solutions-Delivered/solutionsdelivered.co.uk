# Persona: Inclusive Design Specialist

## Profile
- **Name:** Aisha Patel
- **Age:** 34
- **Role:** Inclusive Design Lead
- **Company Type:** Global Tech Company
- **Experience:** 8 years in inclusive design, background in cognitive psychology
- **Location:** London, UK

## Background
Aisha champions inclusive design practices that go beyond minimum accessibility compliance. She focuses on designing for cognitive diversity, neurodivergent users, low-literacy audiences, and situational disabilities. She believes that inclusive design benefits everyone, not just people with permanent disabilities.

## Goals & Motivations
- Design for the widest possible range of human diversity
- Address cognitive and neurodivergent needs, not just physical disabilities
- Create products that reduce cognitive load for all users
- Challenge assumptions about "normal" users
- Shift from compliance mindset to empathy-driven design

## Pain Points & Challenges
- Accessibility conversations that only focus on screen readers
- Lack of research with neurodivergent users
- Designers who don't understand cognitive disabilities
- Testing that doesn't include diverse participants
- Budget constraints for inclusive research
- Difficulty quantifying the business value of inclusive design

## What They Look For in a Website
- **Simple Language:** Clear, concise content at appropriate reading level
- **Predictable Patterns:** Consistent layouts and familiar conventions
- **Cognitive Load Reduction:** Not too much information at once, clear priorities
- **Error Prevention:** Design that prevents mistakes rather than just catching them
- **Chunked Content:** Information broken into digestible sections
- **Visual Clarity:** Clear distinction between elements, not visually overwhelming
- **Focus Support:** Clear current location, one thing at a time
- **Flexible Timing:** No time limits or easily extended timeouts
- **Multiple Formats:** Information presented in text, images, and video
- **Neurodivergent Support:** Reduced distractions, ability to customize display
- **Cultural Sensitivity:** Inclusive of different cultures, languages, and norms
- **Literacy Levels:** Support for low-literacy users, symbol support

## How They Evaluate Personas
- **Diversity Representation:** Do personas represent diverse abilities, not just "typical" users?
- **Cognitive Differences:** Are neurodivergent personas included (ADHD, autism, dyslexia)?
- **Temporary Conditions:** Situational disabilities (distraction, stress, injury)?
- **Cultural Diversity:** Different cultural backgrounds and contexts?
- **Age Range:** Children, young adults, middle-aged, elderly?
- **Literacy Levels:** Different education levels and language proficiency?
- **Technology Access:** Users with older devices, slow connections, limited data?
- **Lived Experience:** Were diverse users consulted in creating personas?
- **Intersectionality:** Do personas reflect overlapping identities and challenges?

## Red Flags for This Persona
- Complex language and jargon without explanation
- Overwhelming amount of information on one page
- Unpredictable navigation or layout changes
- Time-based interactions without warnings or extensions
- Flashing or strobing content
- Distracting animations or auto-playing content
- Unclear error messages or consequences
- Assuming users understand cultural references
- Forms that don't allow for diverse answers
- No consideration for cognitive load

## Website Evaluation Checklist
- [ ] Is content written in plain, simple language?
- [ ] Is the layout predictable and consistent?
- [ ] Is information chunked into digestible sections?
- [ ] Are there multiple ways to consume content?
- [ ] Can users control timing and pacing?
- [ ] Is there clear visual hierarchy and focus?
- [ ] Are errors prevented where possible?
- [ ] Is the design not visually overwhelming?
- [ ] Can users customize their experience?
- [ ] Is content culturally sensitive and inclusive?
- [ ] Does design support different literacy levels?
- [ ] Were neurodivergent users included in testing?
