# Persona: Competitor

## Profile
- **Name:** Helen Carter
- **Age:** 39
- **Role:** Founder & CEO
- **Company Type:** Competing Professional Services Firm
- **Service Focus:** Business transformation and change management
- **Location:** Birmingham, UK

## Background
Helen runs a competing firm and regularly monitors competitors to understand market positioning, service offerings, pricing strategies, and differentiation. She uses competitor websites to identify threats, opportunities, and market trends. She may also evaluate them as acquisition targets or partnership opportunities.

## Goals & Motivations
- Monitor competitive threats and positioning
- Identify market opportunities competitors are missing
- Understand competitor pricing and offerings
- Spot competitive differentiators to counter or adopt
- Evaluate potential acquisition targets
- Track market trends through competitor activity
- Protect and grow market share

## Pain Points & Challenges
- Competitors who are vague about offerings (making comparison difficult)
- New entrants disrupting the market
- Competitors with better marketing or positioning
- Difficulty accessing accurate competitive intelligence
- Price pressure from competitors
- Losing deals to competitors
- Keeping up with competitor innovations

## What They Look For in a Website
- **Service Offerings:** Detailed understanding of what they offer
- **Pricing Signals:** Any indication of pricing strategy
- **Positioning & Messaging:** How they differentiate themselves
- **Target Market:** Who they're going after
- **Case Studies:** Types of clients and projects
- **Team Size & Capabilities:** Organizational capacity
- **Geographic Reach:** Where they operate
- **Innovation:** New services or approaches
- **Marketing Strategy:** Channels and messages they use
- **Strengths & Weaknesses:** What they do well and poorly
- **Growth Signals:** Hiring, expansion, new offerings
- **Partnership Alliances:** Strategic relationships

## How They Evaluate Personas
- **Competitive Overlap:** Which personas do we both target?
- **Market Share:** Who's winning which personas?
- **Differentiation:** How do we position differently to each persona?
- **Unserved Needs:** Which persona needs are competitors missing?
- **Win/Loss Analysis:** Which personas do we win vs. lose?
- **Pricing Competition:** How do we compare on value for each persona?
- **Capability Gaps:** Where do competitors have advantages?
- **Market Whitespace:** Which personas are underserved?
- **Competitive Response:** How should we respond to competitor moves?

## Technical Proficiency
- Expert: Competitive strategy, market positioning, business strategy
- Advanced: Market analysis, pricing strategy, service development
- Intermediate: Digital marketing, sales processes
- Tools: Competitive intelligence tools, industry databases, web monitoring

## Preferred Communication Channels
- Competitive analysis reports
- Market intelligence updates
- Industry conferences and events
- Sales team feedback
- Win/loss analysis

## Key Quotes
> "I study competitors to find what they're doing well—and where they're weak."

> "Their website tells me their strategy, whether they realize it or not."

> "The best competitive intelligence comes from understanding their positioning and target market."

## Red Flags for This Persona
- Strong, clear differentiation (competitive threat)
- Innovative service offerings
- Superior positioning or messaging
- Strong client portfolio and case studies
- Evidence of rapid growth
- Better online presence or marketing
- Thought leadership and market visibility
- Lower pricing or better value proposition
- Strategic partnerships or alliances
- Award recognition and industry validation
