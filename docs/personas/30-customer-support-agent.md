# Persona: Customer Support Agent

## Profile
- **Name:** Emily Carter
- **Age:** 26
- **Role:** Customer Support Specialist
- **Company Type:** Professional Services Company
- **Experience:** 3 years in customer support
- **Location:** Manchester, UK

## Background
Emily handles day-to-day customer questions, issues, and requests. She's the first point of contact for most customer interactions. She needs clear information, documentation, and resources to help customers quickly and accurately.

## Goals & Motivations
- Resolve customer issues quickly and effectively
- Provide excellent customer experience
- Reduce customer frustration
- Meet response time and resolution metrics
- Escalate complex issues appropriately
- Learn about products/services to help better

## Pain Points & Challenges
- Incomplete or outdated documentation
- Complex issues beyond her expertise
- Angry or frustrated customers
- Lack of clear escalation paths
- Information scattered across multiple systems
- Pressure to resolve issues quickly
- Not knowing about product updates or changes
- Difficult-to-navigate internal resources

## What They Look For in a Website
- **Knowledge Base:** Comprehensive, searchable help articles
- **FAQs:** Answers to common questions
- **Service Documentation:** Clear descriptions of all offerings
- **Internal Resources:** Support team access to detailed information
- **Troubleshooting Guides:** Step-by-step problem resolution
- **Contact Escalation:** Clear paths to specialists or management
- **Customer Portal:** Self-service options to reduce support load
- **Updates Log:** Changes and new features documented
- **Support Templates:** Canned responses for common issues
- **Video Tutorials:** Visual guides for complex topics
- **Status Page:** Known issues and outages communicated
- **Feedback Mechanism:** Way to improve documentation based on support trends

## How They Evaluate Personas
- **Support Needs:** What questions does each persona commonly ask?
- **Issue Frequency:** Which personas require more support?
- **Issue Complexity:** Are issues simple or requiring escalation?
- **Self-Service Potential:** Can they find answers independently?
- **Communication Preference:** Phone, email, chat, or self-service?
- **Technical Proficiency:** How much hand-holding do they need?
- **Response Time Expectations:** How urgent are their needs?
- **Common Problems:** What are the recurring issues?
- **Escalation Patterns:** When do issues need escalation?

## Technical Proficiency
- Expert: Customer service skills, communication, problem-solving
- Advanced: Support ticket systems, basic troubleshooting, product knowledge
- Intermediate: Documentation tools, knowledge base systems
- Tools: Zendesk, Freshdesk, internal knowledge bases, CRM systems

## Preferred Communication Channels
- Internal knowledge base and documentation
- Support ticket system
- Team communication (Slack, Teams)
- Training materials and guides
- Support team meetings

## Key Quotes
> "If I can't find the answer in our knowledge base in 30 seconds, the customer certainly can't."

> "The best support interaction is the one that doesn't need to happen because the customer found their answer."

> "I learn more from the questions customers ask than from any training manual."

## Red Flags for This Persona
- Missing or incomplete knowledge base
- Outdated support documentation
- No searchable help articles
- Complex navigation to find information
- No self-service options for customers
- Missing troubleshooting guides
- Unclear escalation procedures
- No way to track known issues
- Information scattered across multiple places
- No feedback loop to improve docs
