# Persona: Legal / Compliance Officer

## Profile
- **Name:** Robert Harrison
- **Age:** 48
- **Role:** Digital Compliance Manager
- **Company Type:** Large Financial Services Company
- **Experience:** 15 years in legal compliance, specialized in digital accessibility law
- **Location:** London, UK

## Background
Robert ensures the organization's digital properties comply with legal requirements including the Equality Act 2010, Public Sector Bodies Accessibility Regulations 2018, and EU Accessibility Act. He works closely with legal counsel to mitigate risk of discrimination lawsuits and regulatory penalties.

## Goals & Motivations
- Protect the organization from legal liability
- Ensure compliance with accessibility regulations
- Document due diligence for audits
- Create clear policies and procedures
- Balance legal requirements with business needs

## Pain Points & Challenges
- Evolving regulations across jurisdictions
- Proving compliance without proper documentation
- Getting executive buy-in for accessibility investment
- Coordinating across siloed teams (legal, IT, design)
- Managing third-party vendor compliance
- Responding to accessibility complaints
- Limited budget for compliance activities

## What They Look For in a Website
- **WCAG 2.2 Level AA Compliance:** Meeting minimum legal standards
- **Accessibility Statement:** Clear, honest statement of compliance status
- **Documentation:** Evidence of accessibility testing and remediation efforts
- **Audit Trail:** Records of when issues were identified and fixed
- **Third-Party Compliance:** Ensuring plugins, widgets, and integrations are accessible
- **Privacy Compliance:** GDPR, cookies, data protection requirements
- **Terms of Service:** Clear legal disclaimers and user agreements
- **Accessibility Feedback Mechanism:** Way for users to report accessibility issues
- **Regular Testing:** Scheduled audits and testing procedures
- **Training Records:** Evidence that team members are trained in accessibility
- **Remediation Plans:** Documented plans to fix known issues
- **Alternative Access:** Options for users who can't access standard interface

## How They Evaluate Personas
- **Legal Risk:** Which personas might face barriers that violate anti-discrimination laws?
- **Protected Classes:** Are personas with disabilities (protected class) represented?
- **Jurisdictional Requirements:** Do personas reflect users in regulated jurisdictions?
- **Complaint Risk:** Which personas are most likely to file complaints or lawsuits?
- **Business Impact:** Which personas represent the largest legal/financial risk?
- **Regulatory Focus:** Are personas aligned with regulator priorities?
- **Documentation Value:** Can personas support compliance documentation?
- **Evidence-Based:** Are personas based on actual user groups, not assumptions?

## Red Flags for This Persona
- No accessibility statement or vague claims
- Claims of "fully accessible" without evidence
- Using accessibility overlay widgets as sole solution
- No process for handling accessibility complaints
- Third-party content with no accessibility consideration
- No documentation of testing or remediation
- Missing privacy policy or cookie consent
- Auto-playing videos or content
- No alternative formats available
- Ignoring accessibility complaints or requests

## Website Evaluation Checklist
- [ ] Is there a clear, honest accessibility statement?
- [ ] Does the site meet WCAG 2.2 Level AA standards?
- [ ] Is there documentation of accessibility testing?
- [ ] Is there a feedback mechanism for accessibility issues?
- [ ] Are third-party integrations assessed for accessibility?
- [ ] Is there GDPR/privacy compliance?
- [ ] Are there terms of service and legal disclaimers?
- [ ] Is there a remediation plan for known issues?
- [ ] Are accessibility processes documented?
- [ ] Is staff trained in accessibility requirements?
- [ ] Are there alternative access options if needed?
- [ ] Is compliance reviewed regularly?

## Relevant UK Regulations
- **Equality Act 2010:** Prohibits discrimination based on disability
- **Public Sector Bodies (Websites and Mobile Applications) Accessibility Regulations 2018:** Requires public sector sites to meet WCAG 2.1 AA
- **Reasonable Adjustments:** Duty to make adjustments for disabled users
- **EU Accessibility Act:** Products and services requirements (applies until UK replaces it)

## Potential Legal Consequences
- Discrimination lawsuits under Equality Act
- Regulatory enforcement actions
- Reputational damage
- Exclusion from public sector contracts
- Financial penalties
- Requirement for costly remediation
