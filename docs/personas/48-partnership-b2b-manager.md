# Persona: Partnership / B2B Manager

## Profile
- **Name:** Catherine Walsh
- **Age:** 45
- **Role:** Head of Strategic Partnerships
- **Company Type:** B2B Software Company
- **Experience:** 18 years in business development and partnerships
- **Location:** Edinburgh, UK

## Background
Catherine identifies, develops, and manages strategic business partnerships. She evaluates potential partners based on strategic fit, capabilities, and trustworthiness. She needs to understand what partners do, how they work, and whether they're reliable enough for joint customer relationships.

## Goals & Motivations
- Identify complementary partnership opportunities
- Build mutually beneficial relationships
- Create new revenue channels through partnerships
- Enhance product/service offerings via partnerships
- Vet potential partners thoroughly
- Maintain partner ecosystem

## Pain Points & Challenges
- Difficulty assessing partner quality and reliability
- Unclear value propositions from potential partners
- Partnership agreements that don't deliver value
- Managing complex partner relationships
- Ensuring partner brand alignment
- Limited resources for partner management
- Risk of partnering with wrong organizations

## What They Look For in a Website
- **Partnership Information:** Dedicated partner program or partnership pages
- **Capabilities Overview:** Clear understanding of what they offer
- **Target Market:** Who they serve and how they serve them
- **Company Information:** Size, stability, track record, reputation
- **Integration Options:** Technical integration capabilities
- **Partner Portal:** Resources and support for partners
- **Case Studies:** Evidence of successful client work
- **Complementary Services:** Non-competing offerings
- **Contact for Partnerships:** Clear business development contact
- **Partner Testimonials:** References from existing partners
- **Value Proposition:** What value they bring to partnerships
- **Professional Credentials:** Awards, certifications, recognition

## How They Evaluate Personas
- **Partnership Fit:** Which personas would benefit from combined offerings?
- **Complementary Needs:** What needs do personas have that partners can fill?
- **Joint Value:** Can partnerships create better outcomes for personas?
- **Market Overlap:** Do partners serve the same personas?
- **Competitive Concerns:** Are there competitive conflicts with personas?
- **Partnership Models:** Referral, reseller, integration, co-marketing?
- **Decision Influence:** Do personas influence partnership decisions?
- **Risk Assessment:** What risks exist with persona overlap?

## Technical Proficiency
- Expert: Business development, partnership strategy, negotiation, relationship management
- Advanced: Market analysis, contract management, partner ecosystem development
- Intermediate: Product knowledge, integration basics, channel management
- Tools: CRM, partner management platforms, contract management systems

## Preferred Communication Channels
- Partnership program documentation
- Business development meetings
- Partner case studies and references
- Integration documentation
- Strategic partnership proposals

## Key Quotes
> "I need to know you're stable, professional, and won't damage relationships with our mutual clients."

> "Partnerships work when there's clear, complementary value—not when we're competing for the same dollar."

> "Your website tells me if you're serious about partnerships or just collecting logos."

## Red Flags for This Persona
- No partnership information or contact
- Vague or unclear service offerings
- Reputation concerns or poor client references
- Competitive overlap without differentiation
- No partner portal or resources
- Unclear company stability or size
- Poor professionalism or brand presentation
- No integration capabilities
- Missing case studies or proof of capability
- Difficult to contact for business development
