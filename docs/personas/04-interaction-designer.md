## Background
Alex focuses on the dynamic, interactive aspects of digital products. Coming from a motion design background, they excel at creating smooth transitions, meaningful animations, and delightful micro-interactions that enhance usability while adding personality to interfaces.

## Goals & Motivations
- Create interactions that feel natural and intuitive
- Use motion to guide attention and provide feedback
- Reduce cognitive load through clear affordances
- Add delight without sacrificing usability
- Prototype and test interaction patterns before development

## Pain Points & Challenges
- Developers who implement interactions differently than designed
- Performance issues with complex animations
- Balancing delight with accessibility (motion sensitivity)
- Articulating interaction patterns without showing them
- Limited prototyping tool capabilities
- Interactions that work on desktop but fail on mobile

## What They Look For in a Website
- **Affordances:** Clear visual indicators of interactive elements
- **Feedback:** Immediate response to user actions (clicks, hovers, focus)
- **Transitions:** Smooth, purposeful animations between states
- **Loading States:** Skeletons, spinners, or progress indicators
- **Hover Effects:** Subtle changes that indicate interactivity
- **Focus States:** Clear keyboard navigation indicators
- **Error Handling:** Helpful, contextual error messages
- **Form Interactions:** Real-time validation, clear completion states
- **Scroll Behavior:** Smooth scrolling, scroll-triggered animations
- **Button States:** Default, hover, active, disabled, loading states
- **Gesture Support:** Swipe, pinch, drag interactions on touch devices
- **Motion Reduction:** Respect prefers-reduced-motion settings

## How They Evaluate Personas
- **Interaction Preferences:** How does each persona prefer to interact (mouse, touch, keyboard, voice)?
- **Device Context:** What devices do they primarily use?
- **Technical Proficiency:** Are they tech-savvy or need extra guidance?
- **Speed vs. Delight:** Do they prioritize efficiency or enjoy exploratory interactions?
- **Accessibility Needs:** Do they use assistive technologies or have motion sensitivities?
- **Learning Curve:** How much time will they invest in learning interactions?
- **Task Frequency:** Are they first-time or repeat users?
- **Attention Span:** Do they need immediate feedback or can they wait?

## Red Flags for This Persona
- No hover or focus states on interactive elements
- Buttons that don't look clickable
- Instant state changes with no transitions
- Loading without indicators
- Animations that slow down the experience
- No consideration for reduced motion preferences
- Interactions that don't work on touch devices
- Form validation that only appears after submission

## Website Evaluation Checklist
- [ ] Are all interactive elements clearly identifiable?
- [ ] Do buttons and links have hover/focus states?
- [ ] Are transitions smooth and purposeful?
- [ ] Is there feedback for all user actions?
- [ ] Do loading states clearly indicate progress?
- [ ] Are animations respectful of motion preferences?
- [ ] Do forms provide real-time feedback?
- [ ] Are error states helpful and clear?
- [ ] Do interactions feel responsive (< 100ms)?
- [ ] Do gestures work appropriately on touch devices?
