## Background
Jennifer specializes in web accessibility audits, WCAG compliance consulting, and accessibility training. She works with organizations to ensure their digital products are usable by people with disabilities. She's passionate about inclusive design and advocates for accessibility being built in from the start, not bolted on at the end.

- "Accessibility overlay" solutions that create false compliance
- Developers who don't understand WCAG requirements
- Budgets that don't account for proper accessibility work
- Automated tools that give false positives/negatives
- Resistance from stakeholders who see accessibility as expensive

## What They Look For in a Website
- **WCAG 2.2 Level AA Compliance:** Meeting all success criteria
- **Keyboard Navigation:** Full functionality without a mouse
- **Screen Reader Support:** Proper semantic HTML, ARIA labels, landmarks
- **Color Contrast:** Minimum 4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text and UI components
- **Text Alternatives:** Alt text for images, captions for videos, transcripts for audio
- **Focus Indicators:** Clear, visible focus states for all interactive elements
- **Form Labels:** Proper association between labels and form controls
- **Heading Structure:** Logical hierarchy (h1, h2, h3) without skipping levels
- **Error Identification:** Clear, descriptive error messages linked to form fields
- **Responsive Design:** Works at 200% zoom without horizontal scrolling
- **Animations:** Respects prefers-reduced-motion for vestibular disorders
- **Link Purpose:** Clear link text (not "click here" or "read more")

## How They Evaluate Personas
- **Disability Representation:** Are personas with disabilities included?
- **Assistive Technology Use:** Which tools do personas rely on (screen readers, magnifiers, keyboard-only)?
- **Specific Disabilities:** Vision (blind, low vision, color blind), hearing (deaf, hard of hearing), motor (limited dexterity, tremor), cognitive (dyslexia, ADHD, autism)
- **Temporary vs. Permanent:** Are situational/temporary disabilities considered?
- **Age-Related Changes:** Are older users with declining abilities represented?
- **Technology Access:** Do personas include users with older browsers or devices?
- **Real User Input:** Were people with disabilities consulted in persona creation?
- **Barriers Documented:** What specific barriers does each persona face?

## Red Flags for This Persona
- No keyboard navigation support
- Missing alt text on images
- Insufficient color contrast
- No focus indicators or poor visibility
- Empty links or buttons
- Missing form labels
- Improper heading hierarchy
- Auto-playing content without controls
- CAPTCHAs without alternatives
- Accessibility overlay widgets (AccessiBe, UserWay, etc.)
- PDF content that's not accessible

## Website Evaluation Checklist
- [ ] Can all functionality be accessed with keyboard only?
- [ ] Do all images have appropriate alt text?
- [ ] Is color contrast sufficient (4.5:1 minimum)?
- [ ] Are focus indicators clearly visible?
- [ ] Do form fields have associated labels?
- [ ] Is heading structure logical and sequential?
- [ ] Are error messages descriptive and linked to fields?
- [ ] Does content work at 200% zoom?
- [ ] Are animations respectful of motion preferences?
- [ ] Has the site been tested with actual screen readers?
- [ ] Is there a valid accessibility statement?
- [ ] Are videos captioned and audio transcribed?
