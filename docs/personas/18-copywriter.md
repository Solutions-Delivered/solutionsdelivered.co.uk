# Persona: Copywriter

## Profile
- **Name:** Tom Hughes
- **Age:** 29
- **Role:** Senior Copywriter
- **Company Type:** Creative Agency
- **Experience:** 7 years writing for web and marketing
- **Location:** Manchester, UK

## Background
Tom crafts compelling copy that engages users and drives action. He specializes in web copy, landing pages, and marketing materials. He understands the psychology of persuasion and knows how to balance creativity with conversion-focused writing.

## Goals & Motivations
- Write compelling copy that drives action
- Craft messages that resonate emotionally
- Balance creativity with conversion goals
- Develop unique brand voice and tone
- Improve conversion rates through better copy
- Create clear, scannable web content

## Pain Points & Challenges
- Stakeholders who want to "say everything"
- Copy that gets buried in committee feedback
- Balancing SEO requirements with good writing
- Limited space for copy (character limits, mobile)
- Lack of clear brief or direction
- Clients who don't value good copywriting
- Writing for industries they don't understand

## What They Look For in a Website
- **Clear Hierarchy:** Headlines, subheads, body copy logically structured
- **Compelling Headlines:** Attention-grabbing, benefit-focused headlines
- **Benefit-Focused Copy:** Emphasis on "what's in it for the user"
- **Scannability:** Short paragraphs, bullet points, clear formatting
- **Active Voice:** Direct, engaging language
- **Strong CTAs:** Clear, action-oriented calls to action
- **Tone Consistency:** Unified brand voice throughout
- **White Space:** Copy has room to breathe, not cramped
- **Appropriate Length:** Right amount of copy for each context
- **Persuasive Elements:** Social proof, urgency, authority
- **Clarity:** No jargon, complex sentences, or confusion
- **Mobile-Friendly:** Copy works on small screens

## How They Evaluate Personas
- **Language Preferences:** What vocabulary and tone resonates?
- **Pain Points:** What problems can copy address?
- **Motivations:** What drives them to take action?
- **Objections:** What concerns need to be overcome?
- **Benefits Sought:** What outcomes do they want?
- **Emotional Triggers:** What emotions drive decision-making?
- **Reading Habits:** How do they consume content (scanning vs. deep reading)?
- **Literacy Level:** What reading level is appropriate?
- **Formality:** Professional, casual, or somewhere in between?
- **Decision Factors:** What information do they need to decide?

## Technical Proficiency
- Expert: Persuasive writing, tone of voice, headline writing, editing
- Advanced: SEO copywriting, content marketing, brand messaging
- Intermediate: Basic HTML, analytics, conversion optimization
- Tools: Google Docs, Grammarly, Hemingway Editor, word processors

## Preferred Communication Channels
- Copy decks and messaging documents
- Style guides and tone of voice guidelines
- Brand messaging frameworks
- Conversion-focused copy examples
- A/B test results for copy variations

## Key Quotes
> "People don't read, they scan. Write accordingly."

> "Features tell. Benefits sell."

> "Every word should earn its place on the page."

## Red Flags for This Persona
- Walls of text without breaks
- Jargon-heavy, complex language
- Weak or generic headlines
- No clear CTAs or multiple conflicting CTAs
- Burying the lead or key message
- Inconsistent tone across pages
- Passive voice and unclear language
- Copy that talks about "we" instead of "you"
- Too much copy cramped into small spaces
- Generic, templated copy

## Website Evaluation Checklist
- [ ] Are headlines clear and compelling?
- [ ] Is copy benefit-focused, not feature-focused?
- [ ] Is the language clear and jargon-free?
- [ ] Are paragraphs short and scannable?
- [ ] Are CTAs clear and action-oriented?
- [ ] Is tone consistent throughout?
- [ ] Does copy use active voice?
- [ ] Is there appropriate white space?
- [ ] Does copy address user pain points?
- [ ] Are objections preemptively addressed?
- [ ] Is copy mobile-friendly?
- [ ] Does copy create emotional connection?
- [ ] Is there social proof and credibility?
- [ ] Does every word earn its place?
- [ ] Is the value proposition immediately clear?
