# Persona: Recruitment Manager / HR Professional

## Profile
- **Name:** Karen Thompson
- **Age:** 42
- **Role:** Head of People & Talent
- **Company Type:** Growing Professional Services Firm
- **Experience:** 15 years in HR and talent acquisition
- **Location:** Leeds, UK

## Background
Karen is responsible for attracting, hiring, and retaining top talent. She views the website as a critical recruitment tool—the first impression for potential candidates. She needs the site to accurately represent the company and attract the right people.

## Goals & Motivations
- Attract high-quality candidates
- Build strong employer brand
- Reduce cost per hire and time to hire
- Improve candidate experience
- Retain top talent
- Support company growth through hiring

## Pain Points & Challenges
- Competitive talent market
- Candidates who don't fit company culture
- High cost of poor hiring decisions
- Lengthy hiring processes that lose candidates
- Difficulty conveying company culture online
- Generic job descriptions that don't attract right people
- Missing diversity in candidate pool
- Website that doesn't support recruitment goals

## What They Look For in a Website
- **Compelling Careers Page:** Attractive, informative careers section
- **Job Listings:** ATS integration, current openings, easy application
- **Employer Branding:** Clear EVP (Employee Value Proposition)
- **Culture Content:** Videos, photos, stories showing real culture
- **Employee Testimonials:** Authentic voices from current employees
- **Benefits & Perks:** Comprehensive information about compensation
- **DEI Content:** Commitment to diversity, equity, and inclusion
- **Career Paths:** Growth and development opportunities
- **Easy Application:** Simple, mobile-friendly application process
- **Recruitment Marketing:** Content that attracts passive candidates
- **Analytics:** Tracking of career page visits and application sources
- **Social Proof:** Awards, "best place to work" recognitions

## How They Evaluate Personas
- **Talent Segments:** What types of talent are we trying to attract?
- **Candidate Motivations:** What drives each talent segment?
- **Recruitment Channels:** Where does each segment look for jobs?
- **Decision Criteria:** What factors influence their choice?
- **Experience Level:** Entry, mid-level, senior, executive?
- **Skills Needed:** What competencies are we hiring for?
- **Culture Fit:** What personality and values match our culture?
- **Diversity Goals:** Are we attracting diverse candidates?
- **Retention Risk:** Which personas are likely to stay long-term?
- **Referral Sources:** Who refers the best candidates?

## Technical Proficiency
- Expert: Recruitment, employer branding, candidate experience, HR systems
- Advanced: ATS (Applicant Tracking Systems), LinkedIn Recruiter, recruitment marketing
- Intermediate: Analytics, social media recruiting, job boards
- Tools: ATS systems, LinkedIn, job boards, recruitment marketing platforms

## Preferred Communication Channels
- Recruitment marketing materials
- Employer brand guidelines
- Candidate feedback and surveys
- Recruitment metrics and dashboards
- Job board performance data

## Key Quotes
> "Your careers page is your first interview. Make it count."

> "Generic 'we're a great place to work' claims don't work. Show, don't tell."

> "Every friction point in the application process loses good candidates to competitors."

## Red Flags for This Persona
- No dedicated careers page or section
- Outdated or generic job descriptions
- Complicated, lengthy application process
- No information about culture or benefits
- Stock photos instead of real employees
- No mobile-friendly application
- Missing diversity and inclusion content
- No employee testimonials or stories
- Vague or cliché employer branding
- No clear path to apply or contact recruitment
- Missing "best place to work" awards or recognition

## Website Evaluation Checklist
- [ ] Is the careers page compelling and informative?
- [ ] Is the application process simple and mobile-friendly?
- [ ] Are job listings current with clear descriptions?
- [ ] Is company culture authentically represented?
- [ ] Are real employee stories and testimonials featured?
- [ ] Is benefits information comprehensive?
- [ ] Is DEI commitment clearly communicated?
- [ ] Are career growth paths described?
- [ ] Is the EVP (Employee Value Proposition) clear?
- [ ] Are there videos or visual culture content?
- [ ] Can passive candidates be nurtured?
- [ ] Is there ATS integration?
- [ ] Are awards and recognition displayed?
- [ ] Can recruitment track sources and conversions?
- [ ] Does the site attract the right talent?
