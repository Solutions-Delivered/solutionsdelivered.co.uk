# Persona: Industry Professional

## Profile
- **Name:** Simon Harris
- **Age:** 47
- **Role:** Director of Operations, Manufacturing Sector
- **Experience:** 22 years in operations and supply chain management
- **Location:** Midlands, UK

## Background
Simon is a respected professional in his industry. He's not actively looking for a job but is aware of companies in his sector. He may encounter the company at industry events, through his network, or while researching solutions. His opinion of the company influences its reputation.

## Goals & Motivations
- Stay informed about industry trends and players
- Build and maintain professional network
- Evaluate potential partners or vendors
- Keep career options open (passive candidate)
- Share knowledge and insights in his field
- Maintain professional reputation

## Pain Points & Challenges
- Too many vendors claiming to be experts
- Difficult to assess genuine expertise from marketing
- Limited time to research all options
- Concerned about reputation if recommending poor vendors
- Skeptical of overpromising and underdelivering
- Values substance over flash

## What They Look For in a Website
- **Industry Expertise:** Evidence of deep sector knowledge
- **Thought Leadership:** Insightful content, not just marketing
- **Case Studies:** Relevant examples from their industry
- **Team Credentials:** Backgrounds and expertise of key people
- **Speaking & Events:** Industry presence and participation
- **Publications:** White papers, research, articles
- **Client List:** Recognizable companies in the sector
- **Awards & Recognition:** Industry-specific accolades
- **Authenticity:** Honest representation, not overselling
- **Professional Network:** Connections to other industry leaders
- **Industry Knowledge:** Understanding of sector-specific challenges
- **Reputation Signals:** What others in the industry say

## How They Evaluate Personas
- **Industry Affiliation:** Which sectors do personas operate in?
- **Professional Level:** Junior, mid-level, senior, executive?
- **Influence:** Do personas have industry influence?
- **Network Strength:** Are they well-connected in industry?
- **Expertise Areas:** What are their specialized knowledge areas?
- **Reputation Sensitivity:** How much do they value professional reputation?
- **Partnership Potential:** Could they become partners, clients, or referrers?
- **Content Consumers:** Do they engage with thought leadership?

## Technical Proficiency
- Expert: Industry-specific knowledge and practices
- Advanced: Professional networking, industry research
- Intermediate: General business and technology awareness
- Tools: LinkedIn, industry publications, trade associations, conferences

## Preferred Communication Channels
- Industry publications and journals
- LinkedIn professional updates
- Industry conferences and events
- Professional associations
- Peer recommendations

## Key Quotes
> "I can spot a poser from a mile away. Show me you understand my industry's real challenges."

> "Your website tells me if you're serious players or just marketing well."

> "I don't have time for generic content. Give me insights I can actually use."

## Red Flags for This Persona
- Generic claims without industry-specific evidence
- No recognizable clients or projects
- Missing thought leadership or insights
- Stock photos and corporate speak
- No team credentials or expertise shown
- Absence from industry events or publications
- Overpromising without substance
- No case studies from relevant sectors
- Poor reputation or unknown in industry
- Website that looks like every other consultant

## Website Evaluation Checklist
- [ ] Is industry expertise clearly demonstrated?
- [ ] Is thought leadership substantive and insightful?
- [ ] Are there relevant industry case studies?
- [ ] Are team credentials and backgrounds visible?
- [ ] Is there evidence of industry participation?
- [ ] Are there valuable publications or resources?
- [ ] Are recognizable industry clients featured?
- [ ] Are industry-specific awards displayed?
- [ ] Does content reflect deep industry understanding?
- [ ] Is the company known and respected in industry?
- [ ] Are there connections to industry leaders?
- [ ] Does the site build professional credibility?
- [ ] Would they recommend the company to peers?
- [ ] Does it feel authentic and substantive?
- [ ] Would they be proud to be associated with it?
