# Persona: Mobile UX Specialist

## Profile
- **Name:** Maya Patel
- **Age:** 29
- **Role:** Mobile UX Designer
- **Company Type:** Mobile-First Digital Agency
- **Experience:** 6 years specializing in mobile user experience
- **Location:** London, UK

## Background
Maya specializes in designing exceptional mobile experiences. She understands the unique constraints and opportunities of mobile devices, including touch interfaces, small screens, varying connection speeds, and on-the-go contexts. She advocates for mobile-first thinking, not just responsive afterthoughts.

## Goals & Motivations
- Create seamless mobile experiences
- Advocate for mobile-first design approach
- Optimize for touch interfaces and gestures
- Design for varying connection speeds
- Ensure mobile conversion optimization
- Stay current with mobile UX best practices

## Pain Points & Challenges
- Desktop designs that don't translate well to mobile
- Designers who treat mobile as an afterthought
- Performance issues on mobile networks
- Touch target sizing and gesture conflicts
- Limited screen real estate
- Varying device capabilities and screen sizes
- Desktop metrics applied to mobile without context

## What They Look For in a Website
- **Mobile-First Design:** Designed for mobile, not just responsive
- **Touch-Optimized:** Appropriate touch target sizes (44x44px minimum)
- **Performance:** Fast loading on mobile networks (3G, 4G)
- **Thumb-Friendly:** Important actions within thumb reach
- **Simplified Navigation:** Mobile-appropriate menu patterns
- **Reduced Content:** Prioritized content for small screens
- **Form Optimization:** Mobile keyboard considerations, autofill support
- **Gesture Support:** Swipe, pinch, long-press where appropriate
- **Progressive Disclosure:** Showing information progressively
- **Mobile CTAs:** Large, easy-to-tap buttons
- **Orientation Support:** Works in both portrait and landscape
- **Mobile Testing:** Evidence of real device testing

## How They Evaluate Personas
- **Mobile Usage:** What percentage use mobile devices?
- **Context of Use:** Where and when do they access on mobile?
- **Task Urgency:** Are mobile tasks time-sensitive?
- **Connection Quality:** What network conditions do they experience?
- **Device Types:** Which devices and screen sizes?
- **App vs. Web:** Do they prefer native apps or mobile web?
- **Mobile Behaviors:** How do they interact on mobile differently?
- **One-Handed Use:** Do they often use device one-handed?
- **Interruption Tolerance:** How easily are they interrupted?

## Red Flags for This Persona
- Desktop-first design shrunk to fit mobile
- Touch targets too small (under 44x44px)
- Slow mobile performance
- Hamburger menus requiring multiple taps
- Hover-dependent interactions
- Non-mobile-optimized forms
- Tiny text requiring zoom
- Content not prioritized for mobile
- No consideration for one-handed use
- Missing mobile-specific testing
- Ignoring mobile conversion funnel
