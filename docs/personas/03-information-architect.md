# Persona: Information Architect

## Profile
- **Name:** Rebecca Patterson
- **Age:** 41
- **Role:** Senior Information Architect
- **Company Type:** Enterprise Consultancy
- **Experience:** 15 years in IA and content strategy
- **Location:** Edinburgh, UK

## Background
Rebecca specializes in organizing complex information systems. She's worked on large-scale website restructures, navigation redesigns, and taxonomy development. She believes that good IA is invisible—users should find what they need without thinking about the structure.

## Goals & Motivations
- Create logical, intuitive information structures
- Reduce cognitive load through clear organization
- Make complex systems easy to navigate
- Improve findability and discoverability
- Balance business goals with user needs in site structure

## Pain Points & Challenges
- Stakeholders who want everything in the navigation
- Conflicting organizational models (user vs. business logic)
- Legacy content that doesn't fit new structures
- Lack of card sorting or tree testing budget
- Political battles over naming and hierarchy
- Maintaining IA documentation and governance

## What They Look For in a Website
- **Clear Hierarchy:** Logical structure with appropriate depth (usually 3-4 levels max)
- **Consistent Labeling:** Navigation terms that match user language
- **Breadcrumbs:** Clear wayfinding showing current location
- **Search Functionality:** Robust search with filters for complex sites
- **Cross-Linking:** Related content connections, contextual links
- **Taxonomy:** Consistent categorization and tagging system
- **Metadata:** Proper page titles, descriptions, structured data
- **Scalability:** Structure that can grow without breaking
- **Mobile Navigation:** Thoughtful mobile menu organization
- **Sitemap:** XML sitemap for search engines, HTML sitemap for users

## How They Evaluate Personas
- **Information Needs:** What information is each persona seeking?
- **Search Behavior:** How do they prefer to find information (browse vs. search)?
- **Mental Models:** How do they expect information to be organized?
- **Content Priorities:** What content matters most to each persona?
- **Task Scenarios:** What are the common tasks for each persona?
- **Entry Points:** Where do different personas typically enter the site?
- **Navigation Preferences:** Do they prefer categories, filters, or search?
- **Vocabulary:** What terms do they use to describe concepts?
- **Context of Use:** Where and when are they accessing information?

## Red Flags for This Persona
- Mega-menus trying to show everything
- Inconsistent naming across navigation
- More than 4 levels of hierarchy
- Navigation that matches org chart, not user needs
- Orphaned pages with no clear path
- Broken or circular navigation
- No search functionality on content-heavy sites
- Missing breadcrumbs or wayfinding

## Website Evaluation Checklist
- [ ] Can users predict what they'll find in each section?
- [ ] Is the navigation consistent across pages?
- [ ] Are similar items grouped logically?
- [ ] Is the hierarchy appropriate (not too deep/flat)?
- [ ] Do labels match user vocabulary (not jargon)?
- [ ] Are there multiple ways to find key content?
- [ ] Is search available and functional?
- [ ] Are breadcrumbs present and accurate?
- [ ] Can the structure scale without breaking?
- [ ] Is there a clear site map available?
