# Persona: Potential Customer

## Profile
- **Name:** Sarah Jenkins
- **Age:** 38
- **Role:** Head of Transformation
- **Company:** Growing services company (200 employees)
- **Challenge:** Struggling with scaling operations and change management
- **Location:** Leeds, UK

## Background
Sarah is researching solutions for organizational change and process optimization. She's comparing multiple vendors and trying to understand which approach fits her needs. She's cautious about making the wrong choice and needs to justify the decision to executives.

## Goals & Motivations
- Find the right partner to solve current challenges
- Understand capabilities and approach
- Evaluate value for money
- Build confidence in the provider
- Get executive buy-in for the investment
- Minimize risk of choosing wrong provider

## Pain Points & Challenges
- Overwhelmed by options and vendor claims
- Difficulty comparing providers
- Unclear pricing and what's included
- Concerns about implementation complexity
- Need to justify cost to stakeholders
- Limited time to research thoroughly
- Uncertainty about cultural fit
- Fear of committing to wrong solution

## What They Look For in a Website
- **Clear Value Proposition:** Immediately understand what you do and for whom
- **Services Overview:** Detailed information about offerings
- **Case Studies:** Relevant examples from similar companies or industries
- **Pricing Transparency:** At least ballpark pricing or starting points
- **Methodology:** Understanding of approach and process
- **Team Expertise:** Credentials, experience, qualifications
- **Client Testimonials:** Social proof from satisfied customers
- **Clear CTAs:** Easy path to next step (consultation, demo, quote)
- **Trust Signals:** Awards, certifications, industry recognition
- **Comparison Content:** How you differ from alternatives
- **Resources:** Educational content that demonstrates expertise
- **Contact Options:** Multiple ways to reach out (form, phone, email)

## How They Evaluate Personas
- **Decision Journey:** What information does each persona need at each stage?
- **Buying Triggers:** What prompts them to seek solutions?
- **Evaluation Criteria:** What factors drive their decision?
- **Objections:** What concerns do they have?
- **Information Needs:** What questions must be answered?
- **Stakeholder Dynamics:** Who else influences their decision?
- **Competition:** What alternatives are they considering?
- **Timeline:** How urgent is their need?
- **Budget:** What's their likely investment range?

## Technical Proficiency
- Advanced: Business strategy, change management, project management
- Intermediate: Business software, research and evaluation
- Basic: General web browsing, professional tools
- Tools: LinkedIn, Google Search, comparison sites, CRM systems

## Preferred Communication Channels
- Website content and resources
- Email nurture campaigns
- LinkedIn connections and content
- Consultative sales calls
- Webinars and events

## Key Quotes
> "I need to see examples of companies like mine that you've helped successfully."

> "Don't make me dig for pricing. If it's 'contact for quote,' I'm moving on."

> "I'm comparing three providers. Give me reasons to choose you, not generic marketing speak."

## Red Flags for This Persona
- Vague or unclear service descriptions
- No case studies or customer examples
- Missing pricing information entirely
- Generic stock photos and content
- No clear differentiation from competitors
- Difficult to find contact information
- Long forms with too many fields
- No social proof or credibility signals
- Pushy sales tactics or gated content
- No clear methodology or process
- Missing team credentials or expertise

## Website Evaluation Checklist
- [ ] Is the value proposition immediately clear?
- [ ] Are services well-described with detail?
- [ ] Are there relevant case studies?
- [ ] Is pricing information available?
- [ ] Is the approach/methodology explained?
- [ ] Is team expertise demonstrated?
- [ ] Are there client testimonials?
- [ ] Are CTAs clear and compelling?
- [ ] Are trust signals prominent?
- [ ] Is differentiation clear?
- [ ] Are educational resources available?
- [ ] Are contact options easy to find?
- [ ] Does content address common objections?
- [ ] Is the site mobile-friendly?
- [ ] Can I quickly understand if you're right for me?
