# Persona: UI/UX Designer

## Profile
- **Name:** Sarah Chen
- **Age:** 32
- **Role:** Senior UI/UX Designer
- **Company Type:** Digital Agency
- **Experience:** 8 years in digital design
- **Location:** London, UK

## Background
Sarah has worked on dozens of client websites, from startups to enterprise organizations. She's passionate about creating intuitive, beautiful interfaces that serve both user needs and business goals. She stays current with design trends, accessibility standards, and emerging technologies.

## Goals & Motivations
- Create user-centered designs that improve conversion and engagement
- Ensure visual consistency across all touchpoints
- Balance aesthetics with usability and accessibility
- Demonstrate measurable impact of design decisions
- Build a portfolio of work that showcases best practices

## Pain Points & Challenges
- Stakeholders who prioritize aesthetics over usability
- Inconsistent design systems across products
- Lack of user research budget or time
- Difficulty communicating design value to non-designers
- Balancing multiple projects with tight deadlines

## What They Look For in a Website
- **Visual Hierarchy:** Clear information architecture and logical flow
- **Consistency:** Coherent design system, reusable components, brand alignment
- **Responsive Design:** Seamless experience across devices and screen sizes
- **User Flow:** Intuitive navigation, minimal cognitive load, clear CTAs
- **Accessibility:** WCAG compliance, keyboard navigation, screen reader support
- **Performance:** Fast load times, smooth interactions, optimized assets
- **White Space:** Proper spacing, breathing room, uncluttered layouts
- **Typography:** Readable fonts, appropriate hierarchy, good line height
- **Color Usage:** Purposeful palette, sufficient contrast, emotional resonance
- **Micro-interactions:** Delightful details, loading states, hover effects

## How They Evaluate Personas
- **User-Centered:** Do personas reflect real user needs and behaviors?
- **Research-Backed:** Are they based on data, interviews, and observation?
- **Actionable:** Can designers use them to make concrete design decisions?
- **Specific:** Do they include details about context, goals, and pain points?
- **Visual Representation:** Are they presented in a clear, memorable format?
- **Journey Mapping:** Do they connect to user flows and touchpoints?
- **Diverse:** Do they represent the full range of users, including edge cases?

## Red Flags for This Persona
- Cluttered layouts with poor visual hierarchy
- Inconsistent spacing, typography, or color usage
- Missing mobile or accessibility considerations
- Stock photos that don't represent real users
- Design that prioritizes trends over usability
- No clear user journey or conversion path
- Slow performance or janky animations

## Website Evaluation Checklist
- [ ] Is the visual hierarchy clear and logical?
- [ ] Are interactive elements obviously clickable?
- [ ] Is the design system consistent across pages?
- [ ] Does it work well on mobile, tablet, and desktop?
- [ ] Are loading and error states well-designed?
- [ ] Is there sufficient color contrast for readability?
- [ ] Are forms easy to complete with clear validation?
- [ ] Does the design support the content, not overpower it?
- [ ] Are there delightful micro-interactions?
- [ ] Does it reflect modern design best practices?
