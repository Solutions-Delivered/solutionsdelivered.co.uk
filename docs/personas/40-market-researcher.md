# Persona: Market Researcher

## Profile
- **Name:** Samantha Brooks
- **Age:** 36
- **Role:** Market Research Manager
- **Company Type:** B2B Services Company
- **Experience:** 11 years in market research and consumer insights
- **Location:** London, UK

## Background
Samantha designs and conducts research studies to understand target markets, customer needs, and competitive dynamics. She uses quantitative and qualitative methods including surveys, interviews, and focus groups. She helps inform strategy, product development, and marketing decisions.

## Goals & Motivations
- Understand target market characteristics and needs
- Identify market opportunities and threats
- Validate business assumptions with research
- Segment markets effectively
- Inform strategic decisions with insights
- Measure brand perception and awareness

## Pain Points & Challenges
- Limited budget for comprehensive research
- Difficulty recruiting representative participants
- Stakeholders who make decisions without research
- Small sample sizes affecting confidence
- Quickly changing market conditions
- Research that sits unused
- Balancing speed with research quality

## What They Look For in a Website
- **Target Market Evidence:** Clear understanding of who is being targeted
- **Persona Documentation:** Research-backed personas and segments
- **Value Proposition Testing:** Evidence that messaging has been validated
- **Customer Insights:** Demonstration of understanding customer needs
- **Market Positioning:** Clear positioning based on market understanding
- **Competitive Awareness:** Understanding of competitive landscape
- **Surveys & Feedback:** Mechanisms to gather user input
- **Testimonials & Quotes:** Real customer voices and experiences
- **Market Size Data:** Understanding of total addressable market
- **Trend Awareness:** Recognition of market trends and shifts
- **Segmentation:** Different messages for different segments
- **Research Publications:** Market research or insights shared

## How They Evaluate Personas
- **Research Foundation:** What research methods were used to create personas?
- **Sample Representation:** Are personas based on adequate sample sizes?
- **Demographic Detail:** Age, location, role, company size, industry?
- **Psychographic Depth:** Attitudes, values, motivations, behaviors?
- **Needs & Pain Points:** Clearly documented problems and desires?
- **Decision Journey:** Understanding of how personas make decisions?
- **Segment Sizing:** How large is each persona segment?
- **Validation:** Have personas been tested and refined?
- **Differentiation:** How do personas differ from each other?
- **Actionability:** Can personas guide real business decisions?

## Technical Proficiency
- Expert: Research methodology, survey design, qualitative research, analysis
- Advanced: Statistical analysis, data interpretation, segmentation
- Intermediate: Research software, data visualization, reporting
- Tools: Qualtrics, SurveyMonkey, SPSS, NVivo, research panels

## Preferred Communication Channels
- Research reports with methodology
- Survey results and analysis
- Focus group findings
- Market sizing and segmentation studies
- Competitive landscape analysis

## Key Quotes
> "Personas without research are just educated guesses—and often wrong guesses."

> "The best market research challenges assumptions, not confirms them."

> "If you haven't talked to your target market, you don't really know what they need."

## Red Flags for This Persona
- Personas that seem assumption-based
- No evidence of customer research
- Generic market understanding
- Positioning that doesn't reflect market reality
- No segmentation or targeting strategy
- Ignoring market trends or competitive threats
- Messages that don't resonate with target market
- No feedback mechanisms or customer voice
- Over-reliance on internal perspectives
- Market claims without data backing
