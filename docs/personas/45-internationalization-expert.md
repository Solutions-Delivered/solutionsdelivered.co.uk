# Persona: Internationalization Expert

## Profile
- **Name:** Marie Dubois
- **Age:** 40
- **Role:** Localization and Internationalization Manager
- **Company Type:** Global Software Company
- **Experience:** 14 years in internationalization (i18n) and localization (l10n)
- **Location:** Brussels, Belgium (works globally)

## Background
Marie specializes in making websites work for global audiences. She understands cultural differences, language considerations, technical requirements for multilingual sites, and regional preferences. She ensures websites are culturally appropriate and technically ready for international markets.

## Goals & Motivations
- Enable seamless global expansion
- Ensure cultural appropriateness across markets
- Implement proper technical i18n infrastructure
- Avoid cultural missteps and offensive content
- Optimize for local user preferences
- Make localization scalable and maintainable

## Pain Points & Challenges
- Hard-coded English text throughout codebase
- Designs that don't accommodate text expansion
- Cultural assumptions baked into UX
- Lack of right-to-left (RTL) language support
- Date, time, and number format inconsistencies
- Images or content that don't translate culturally
- Limited budget for proper localization

## What They Look For in a Website
- **Language Support:** Multi-language capability with proper switching
- **Character Encoding:** UTF-8 support for all languages
- **Text Direction:** RTL support for Arabic, Hebrew, etc.
- **Text Expansion:** Design accommodating 30-40% text expansion
- **Date/Time Formats:** Locale-appropriate formatting
- **Number Formats:** Proper currency, decimals, thousands separators
- **Cultural Sensitivity:** Images, colors, symbols appropriate for markets
- **Local Payment Methods:** Region-specific payment options
- **Legal Compliance:** GDPR, regional regulations
- **SEO Hreflang:** Proper hreflang tags for international SEO
- **Translation Infrastructure:** CMS or system supporting translations
- **Content Strategy:** Consideration for translated content needs

## How They Evaluate Personas
- **Geographic Distribution:** Where are personas located globally?
- **Language Preferences:** What languages do they speak?
- **Cultural Context:** What cultural norms apply?
- **Local Regulations:** What legal requirements exist per region?
- **Market Maturity:** Are markets developed or emerging?
- **Device Preferences:** What devices are popular in each region?
- **Payment Preferences:** What payment methods do they use?
- **Content Expectations:** What content resonates in each culture?
- **Local Competition:** Who competes in each market?

## Technical Proficiency

- Intermediate: CMS systems, translation tools, regional compliance

## Key Quotes
> "Internationalization isn't translation—it's making your product technically and culturally ready for global markets."

> "If your design can't handle German text expansion or Arabic RTL, it's not ready for international."

## Red Flags for This Persona
- Hard-coded English text throughout
- No language switching capability
- Designs that break with long text
- No RTL language support
- English-only date/time/number formats
- Culturally insensitive imagery or content
- Only one currency or payment method
- Missing hreflang tags
- No consideration for text expansion
- Assumptions about English-speaking users
- Missing regional legal compliance
- Poor or machine-only translation
