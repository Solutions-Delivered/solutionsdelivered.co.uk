# Industry Professional Website Review
**Persona:** Simon Harris, Director of Operations
**Date:** 20 November 2025
**Reviewer Role:** Industry Professional (22 years experience, Manufacturing Sector)

---

## Executive Summary

As someone who's been in operations and supply chain management for over two decades, I've seen countless consultancies come and go. I've learned to spot the difference between genuine expertise and marketing fluff within minutes. Unfortunately, Solutions Delivered's website reads like every other generic consultancy trying to break into the market.

The site is professionally designed and technically competent, but it fundamentally fails to answer the critical question every industry professional asks: "Why should I trust you?" There's no evidence of deep expertise, no proof of successful delivery, no recognizable clients, and no thought leadership that demonstrates understanding of real business challenges.

If a colleague asked me about this company, I'd have nothing substantive to share. If a supplier or partner asked for recommendations, I couldn't confidently endorse them based on what's presented here. The website is all promise, no proof.

**Overall Rating: 3.5/10**

---

## Strengths

### 1. Professional Presentation
The website is well-designed, accessible (WCAG 2.2 compliance mentioned), and technically sound. The visual design is modern and the user experience is smooth. This shows attention to basic quality standards.

### 2. Clear Service Structure
The four core services (Web Development, Service Management, Project Management, Business Change) are clearly defined and easy to navigate. The "How We Work" section provides a transparent process overview.

### 3. Collaboration Emphasis
The focus on direct collaboration and working alongside client teams rather than just delivering from a distance is commendable. This approach resonates with how successful engagements actually work.

### 4. Honest Positioning
Phrases like "no bloat philosophy" and "quality over speed" suggest a realistic understanding of project delivery. The acknowledgment that they value "quality over arbitrary deadlines" shows maturity.

### 5. Accessibility Commitment
The genuine commitment to WCAG 2.2 compliance demonstrates attention to inclusive design, which is increasingly important in modern business.

---

## Weaknesses

### 1. Zero Evidence of Expertise
This is the most critical failure. There are no case studies, no client testimonials, no project examples, no success metrics. Every claim about expertise is unsupported by evidence. As someone who evaluates vendors regularly, I need proof, not promises.

### 2. No Team Credentials
Who are these people? What's their background? What industries have they worked in? What qualifications do they hold? The complete absence of team information makes it impossible to assess whether they have relevant expertise for any specific sector.

### 3. Missing Industry Presence
There's no evidence of:
- Speaking engagements at industry events
- Published articles or whitepapers
- Participation in professional associations
- Conference presentations
- Industry awards or recognition
- Thought leadership content

If they were genuine players in any industry, there would be traces of their participation.

### 4. Generic Content Throughout
Every description could apply to any consultancy in any sector. There's no industry-specific language, no demonstration of understanding sector challenges, no references to specific methodologies or frameworks beyond a passing mention of "ITIL-aligned" (which is table stakes, not a differentiator).

### 5. No Client Validation
Not a single recognizable client name. Not one logo. Not one testimonial. Not one quote from a satisfied customer. In professional services, reputation and references are everything. Their absence is deafening.

### 6. Lack of Substantive Thought Leadership
The content reads like marketing copy, not insights from experienced professionals. Where are the blog posts analyzing industry trends? Where are the deep dives into complex problems? Where's the evidence they understand the challenges they claim to solve?

### 7. Vague Value Propositions
"Tailored solutions," "trusted delivery," "collaborative approach" - these are meaningless platitudes. Every consultancy says this. What specifically makes them different? What's their methodology? What frameworks do they use? What tools do they employ?

### 8. Missing Credentials and Certifications
No mention of professional certifications (PRINCE2, PMP, ITIL Expert, etc.), industry memberships, or quality standards beyond web accessibility. In professional services, credentials matter.

---

## Specific Issues

### Critical Issues

1. **No Proof Points**: The entire website makes claims without substantiation. "Experienced team" - prove it. "Proven experience" - show me. "Track record of delivering results" - where are they?

2. **Zero Industry Context**: No mention of specific industries served, specific problems solved, or specific outcomes achieved. It's impossible to determine if they have relevant experience for my sector.

3. **No Thought Leadership**: The absence of any substantive content (blogs, whitepapers, case studies) suggests either they're very new or they don't have insights worth sharing. Either way, that's problematic.

4. **Missing Trust Signals**: No client logos, no testimonials, no awards, no media mentions, no industry affiliations. Everything that would build credibility is absent.

### Moderate Issues

5. **Careers Page Disconnect**: The careers page talks about what they offer employees but provides no insight into the team's actual expertise or the company's market position.

6. **Generic Process Description**: The four-step engagement process (Initial Discussion, Proposal & Planning, Collaborative Delivery, Handover & Support) is standard for any professional services firm. Nothing distinctive or innovative.

7. **Buzzword Overload**: Terms like "transformation," "sustainable growth," "operational excellence" appear without context or evidence of what they actually mean in practice.

8. **No Pricing Indicators**: While specific pricing isn't necessary, some indication of scale (SME-focused vs. enterprise, project sizes, typical engagement lengths) would help assess fit.

### Minor Issues

9. **About Page Too Generic**: The "About Us" page reads like a template. It doesn't tell me anything about the company's history, founding story, market position, or unique approach.

10. **No Contact Information Beyond Form**: No phone number, no email address, no LinkedIn profiles. This reduces transparency and makes it harder to research the company through normal professional channels.

---

## Recommendations

### Immediate Priorities (Essential)

1. **Add Case Studies** (Critical)
   - Minimum 3-5 detailed case studies showing:
     - Client context (anonymize if necessary, but give details)
     - Specific challenges faced
     - Approach taken and why
     - Measurable outcomes achieved
     - Lessons learned
   - Include different industries and service types
   - Use real data and specific examples

2. **Introduce the Team** (Critical)
   - Create team pages with:
     - Professional backgrounds and experience
     - Industry sectors worked in
     - Specific expertise areas
     - Relevant qualifications and certifications
     - LinkedIn profiles for validation
   - Even a small team needs to demonstrate credibility

3. **Develop Thought Leadership Content** (Critical)
   - Start a blog with substantive articles on:
     - Industry-specific challenges and solutions
     - Technical deep-dives on methodologies
     - Lessons learned from projects
     - Analysis of industry trends
   - Aim for insight, not marketing
   - Demonstrate expertise through shared knowledge

4. **Add Client Testimonials and References** (Critical)
   - Even if clients can't be named, quotes add credibility
   - Video testimonials are even more powerful
   - Specific examples of what clients valued
   - Permission to contact references (even on NDA)

### Short-term Improvements (Important)

5. **Showcase Industry Participation**
   - List speaking engagements and presentations
   - Share published articles or guest posts
   - Highlight professional association memberships
   - Display any industry awards or recognition
   - Show conference attendance or sponsorships

6. **Add Credentials and Certifications**
   - List team certifications (PRINCE2, PMP, ITIL, etc.)
   - Show company quality standards (ISO, etc.)
   - Highlight specialized training or expertise
   - Demonstrate continuous professional development

7. **Create Industry-Specific Content**
   - Develop pages or sections for key industry sectors
   - Demonstrate understanding of sector-specific challenges
   - Use industry terminology and reference relevant regulations
   - Show familiarity with sector trends and dynamics

8. **Improve "About" Narrative**
   - Tell the company's story: Why founded? What gap does it fill?
   - Share the vision and what makes the approach different
   - Explain market positioning and focus areas
   - Provide more context about the business philosophy

### Long-term Enhancements (Beneficial)

9. **Build Partner and Client Ecosystems**
   - Show technology partnerships (relevant vendors/platforms)
   - Display client logos (with permission)
   - Highlight collaboration with other consultancies
   - Demonstrate network and relationships

10. **Create Resource Library**
    - Templates and frameworks used
    - Industry guides and whitepapers
    - Webinars and video content
    - Tools and methodologies explained

11. **Add Social Proof**
    - Industry media mentions
    - Recognition in trade publications
    - Third-party reviews and ratings
    - Social media presence with engaged community

12. **Develop ROI and Metrics Content**
    - Explain how success is measured
    - Share typical return on investment
    - Provide frameworks for evaluating outcomes
    - Show data on project success rates

### Why This Matters

As a Director of Operations with 22 years in the field, I evaluate vendors constantly. This website would not make my shortlist for any engagement because:

1. **No validation of capability** - I can't assess if they can actually do what they claim

### The Bottom Line

This website might attract small businesses looking for basic services, but it will not impress industry professionals or decision-makers at established companies. To play in the professional services market where reputation matters, Solutions Delivered needs to fundamentally rethink their content strategy.

They've built a beautiful frame with nothing inside. Until they fill it with evidence of expertise, proof of delivery, and thought leadership that demonstrates genuine understanding, they'll struggle to win the trust of experienced professionals who've seen it all before.

I can spot a poser from a mile away, and right now, this looks like marketing without substance. Prove me wrong with case studies, team credentials, and thought leadership, and I'll reconsider.

---

## Final Thoughts

I don't write this review to be harsh - I write it because the gap between what's presented and what's needed is significant. The good news is the foundation is solid: the design is professional, the technical execution is sound, and the messaging around collaboration is right.

But in professional services, particularly in B2B consulting, reputation and proof of expertise are everything. You can't just claim to be experienced; you must demonstrate it. You can't just say you deliver results; you must show them.

My advice: Stop treating the website like a marketing brochure and start treating it like your professional reputation. Because that's exactly what it is.

If Solutions Delivered addresses the critical gaps outlined above - particularly case studies, team credentials, and thought leadership - they could transform this from a 3.5/10 to an 8/10. The infrastructure is there. Now they need to fill it with substance.

Until then, I'll keep looking elsewhere for partners I can trust and recommend with confidence.
