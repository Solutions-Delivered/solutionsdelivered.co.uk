# M&A Website Review: Business Owner / Seller Perspective

**Reviewer:** Graham Peterson
**Role:** Owner & Managing Director
**Company:** Established consulting firm (25 employees, £2.5M revenue)
**Review Date:** 2025-11-20
**Website:** Solutions Delivered (solutionsdelivered.co.uk)

---

## Executive Summary

I've spent 20 years building my consulting business from the ground up, and I'm at a stage where I'm exploring exit options. When I evaluate potential acquirers, I'm looking for stability, cultural alignment, employee care, and most importantly—trust. I need to know my life's work will go to the right home.

After thoroughly reviewing the Solutions Delivered website, I have to be honest: this doesn't look like a potential acquirer. In fact, it appears to be a small consultancy—possibly even a solo operation or very small team—rather than an organization with the scale, resources, or track record to acquire my 25-person, £2.5M business.

**Bottom Line:** This is not a viable acquisition opportunity. There's a fundamental mismatch in scale, and the website provides none of the information I need to evaluate them as a potential buyer.

---

## Strengths

Despite my concerns about fit, I'll acknowledge what they're doing well:

### 1. **Professional Presentation**
The website is clean, modern, and professionally designed. It's WCAG 2.2 compliant, which shows they care about quality and accessibility. The design is thoughtful and the messaging is clear.

### 2. **Collaborative Values**
Their emphasis on "Direct Team Collaboration," "No-Bloat Philosophy," and working alongside clients rather than just for them resonates with how I've built my business. We share similar values around partnership and transparency.

### 3. **Service Alignment**
Their services (Service Management, Project Management, Business Change, and Web Development) are complementary to what we do. There's clear strategic fit in terms of service offerings.

### 4. **Quality Focus**
Their no-bloat philosophy, focus on bespoke solutions, and emphasis on quality over quick fixes aligns with my approach. They're not just selling templates or cookie-cutter solutions.

### 5. **Clear Communication Style**
The website uses straightforward language, transparent pricing mentions, and realistic expectations. This is refreshing and suggests they operate with integrity.

---

## Weaknesses

Here's where my serious concerns begin:

### 1. **No Evidence of Scale**
There's absolutely no indication of company size, employee count, revenue, or growth trajectory. For an M&A evaluation, this is critical information that's completely missing. I don't even know if they have 5 employees or 50.

### 2. **No Leadership Information**
Zero information about the leadership team. No bios, no photos, no credentials. I have no idea who I'd be working with or handing my business over to. This is a dealbreaker for establishing trust.

### 3. **No Financial Stability Indicators**
No information about:
- Years in business
- Revenue or profitability
- Funding or backing
- Client base size
- Growth metrics
- Office locations or geographic reach

### 4. **Zero M&A Track Record**
This is perhaps the biggest red flag. There's no mention of:
- Previous acquisitions
- Integration experience
- Employee retention post-acquisition
- Client retention through transitions
- M&A strategy or appetite

### 5. **No Social Proof**
Not a single:
- Client testimonial
- Case study
- Industry award
- Professional certification
- Client logo or reference
- Success metric

### 6. **No Evidence of Team Size**
The careers page talks about joining "our team" but provides no indication of current team size. The "How We Work" page mentions "direct access" and "no account managers," which actually suggests a very small operation—possibly just one or two people.

### 7. **No M&A Contact or Business Development Information**
There's no dedicated contact for strategic partnerships, acquisitions, or business development. Just a generic "Get Started" contact form designed for service inquiries.

---

## Specific Issues

### Scale Mismatch
My company has:
- 25 employees
- £2.5M annual revenue
- Established client base
- 20 years of history
- Office presence in Nottingham

Solutions Delivered appears to be:
- Possibly solo or very small team (2-5 people)
- Remote-first with no office mentioned
- Unclear revenue scale
- Unclear history
- Positioning themselves as a boutique consultancy, not a growth-focused acquirer

**This is fundamentally incompatible.** Even if they wanted to acquire my business, I see no evidence they have the financial resources, infrastructure, or experience to do so successfully.

### Cultural Concerns
While their stated values align with mine, I have concerns:
- "Remote-first, UK-based" - My team has worked from our Nottingham office for years. Would they maintain that?
- "Direct collaboration" sounds great for small projects, but can they manage 25 people?
- No evidence of how they treat employees or handle HR at scale

### Client Continuity Risk
Without any case studies or client testimonials, I have no confidence that:
- They can handle our client base and relationships
- They have the capacity to service multiple large accounts
- They understand the complexities of enterprise client management

### Employee Integration Concerns
Critical missing information:
- What happens to my 25 employees?
- What's their HR infrastructure?
- Do they have benefits, professional development programs?
- What's their retention rate?
- How do they handle performance management at scale?

### No Strategic Vision for Growth Through Acquisition
There's nothing on this website suggesting they're:
- Looking to grow through acquisition
- Building a larger organization
- Expanding their service offerings
- Seeking to acquire complementary businesses

---

## Recommendations

If Solutions Delivered were serious about pursuing acquisitions (which I'm not convinced they are or should be), they would need to completely overhaul their website and positioning:

### 1. **For M&A Credibility:**
- Add a dedicated "Strategic Partnerships" or "M&A" page
- Provide detailed company information (size, revenue range, years in business)
- Showcase acquisition track record and integration success stories
- Display leadership team with full bios and credentials
- Share growth trajectory and strategic vision

### 2. **For Seller Confidence:**
- Employee testimonials from acquired companies
- Client retention statistics post-acquisition
- Case studies of successful integrations
- Clear information about employee care and benefits
- Financial stability indicators

### 3. **For Trust Building:**
- Multiple client case studies with results
- Industry certifications and awards
- Long-term client relationship examples
- Professional references
- Office locations and infrastructure details

### 4. **For Cultural Fit Assessment:**
- Company values with real examples
- Employee culture and work environment
- Professional development programs
- Career progression paths
- Community involvement

## Acquisition Decision: **NO - Not a Viable Acquirer**

I'll be blunt: Solutions Delivered is not a potential acquirer for my business, and I don't believe they're positioned to be one. This appears to be a small consultancy—possibly even a solo practitioner or very small team—doing good work for their clients, but they're operating in a completely different league than what I need.

### Why I Would Not Pursue This Further:

1. **Fundamental Scale Mismatch**
   - They appear to be a micro-consultancy (possibly 1-5 people)
   - My business has 25 employees and £2.5M in revenue
   - There's no way they could acquire, integrate, and run my business
   - The financial resources required would be enormous relative to their apparent size

2. **Zero M&A Infrastructure or Experience**
   - No acquisition track record
   - No evidence of managing acquired businesses
   - No M&A contact or process
   - Not positioned as a growth-through-acquisition company

3. **Insufficient Information to Build Trust**
   - No leadership team information
   - No financial stability indicators
   - No proof of scale or capability
   - No social proof or track record

4. **Capacity Concerns**
   - No evidence they could handle 25 employees
   - Unclear if they have HR infrastructure
   - No proof of managing large client accounts
   - Remote-first model may not work for my office-based team

5. **Missing Critical M&A Elements**
   - No integration methodology
   - No employee retention track record
   - No valuation framework or approach
   - No strategic acquirer positioning

### What This Website Is Actually Good For:

To be fair to Solutions Delivered, their website is well-designed for their actual purpose: **attracting clients for consulting services**. They're positioning themselves as a boutique, high-quality consultancy that does bespoke work with direct collaboration. That's a perfectly legitimate business model.

However, that's not what I need. I need an acquirer with:
- Proven acquisition track record
- Scale and resources to integrate my 25-person team
- Financial backing and stability
- Clear M&A strategy and vision
- Evidence of successful integrations
- Leadership I can trust with my legacy

### The Reality Check:

This exercise has helped me clarify what I'm actually looking for. A potential acquirer's website should immediately signal:
- **"We grow through strategic acquisitions"**
- **"We've successfully integrated X companies"**
- **"Our employees from acquired companies thrive here"**
- **"We preserve the legacy of businesses we acquire"**

I see none of that here. Instead, I see a small consultancy doing good work at their scale, but nowhere near ready to acquire a business like mine.

### My Honest Assessment:

If someone from Solutions Delivered reached out about acquiring my business, I'd be skeptical. My first questions would be:
1. "How many people work there?"
2. "What's your annual revenue?"
3. "Have you ever acquired a company before?"
4. "How would you finance this acquisition?"
5. "What's your plan for my 25 employees?"

I suspect the answers would confirm my concerns: they're not positioned to be an acquirer.

### Final Thoughts:

I've spent 20 years building my business, and I'm emotionally invested in ensuring it goes to the right home. My employees are like family. My clients trust us. My reputation is on the line.

Solutions Delivered might be a great consultancy for their clients, but they're not a viable acquirer for my business. The scale mismatch alone makes this a non-starter, and the complete absence of M&A positioning, leadership information, and financial indicators means I can't even begin to evaluate them seriously.

I need to find a buyer with proven acquisition experience, the financial resources to close the deal, the infrastructure to support my team, and the track record to give me confidence. This isn't it.

**Verdict: Not a suitable acquirer. Would not pursue further discussions.**

## For Solutions Delivered to Consider:

If you're reading this and thinking "We're not trying to acquire companies," then fair enough—this review isn't for you. But if you ever *do* want to grow through acquisition, you'll need to completely transform your positioning, add substantial company information, showcase your team and financials, and demonstrate acquisition capability.

Until then, focus on what you're good at: delivering quality consulting services to your clients at your current scale.
