## Executive Summary

As someone who has evaluated hundreds of professional services firms for acquisition and investment over 28 years, I approached the Solutions Delivered website with the same rigor I apply to any preliminary due diligence. This is my first impression of the company, and in private equity, first impressions matter enormously—they signal professionalism, transparency, and readiness for institutional partnership.

**Bottom Line:** This website provides virtually no information that an investor needs to make even a preliminary assessment. While the presentation is professional and the service offering is clear, there is a complete absence of the fundamental business intelligence required for investment evaluation: no leadership team, no company metrics, no growth indicators, no client validation, and no financial signals whatsoever.

I cannot determine if this is a £500k operation or a £5M operation. I cannot assess management quality. I cannot evaluate market position. I cannot gauge growth trajectory. In short, this company is invisible to the investment community.

**Investment Rating:** 2/10 (Not Investment Ready)

## 1. Strengths

### 1.1 Professional Market Positioning
The website clearly articulates a focused service offering across four complementary domains: Web Development (Laravel-specific), Service Management, Project Management, and Business Change. This is a coherent portfolio that could serve SMB clients well, particularly those in digital transformation.

The positioning is professional and avoids the breathless marketing hyperbole common in this space. The tone is measured and practical.

### 1.2 Accessibility Market Opportunity
The consistent emphasis on WCAG 2.2 compliance demonstrates awareness of a significant market opportunity. With UK Public Sector accessibility regulations and the incoming European Accessibility Act, there's a genuine growing market here.

**Investment Angle:** This could be a differentiator in a competitive market, though without evidence of delivery, it's merely positioning.

### 1.3 Technology Focus
The Laravel-specific approach shows technical clarity. Rather than claiming to be "platform agnostic" (which often means no deep expertise), they've chosen a specific, modern framework and built their offering around it.

**Investment Angle:** This focus could drive operational efficiency and quality consistency—both attractive to investors—but it also limits addressable market.

### 1.4 Process Transparency
The four-step engagement process (Initial Discussion, Proposal & Planning, Collaborative Delivery, Handover & Support) is clear and client-friendly. This suggests process maturity, though at what scale remains unknown.

### 1.5 Cost Structure Advantages
Remote-first operation suggests low overhead, which could translate to attractive margins. The "no-bloat philosophy" implies lean operations, though this requires validation.

**Investment Angle:** If genuine, this could indicate a capital-efficient operation with potentially attractive EBITDA margins.

### 7.2 Business Model: Scalability Questions

Service businesses scale in predictable ways:

**Linear Scaling (Lowest Value):**
- Revenue grows 1:1 with headcount
- Founder involvement required on every project
- Margins flat or declining with growth
- Valuation: 3-4x EBITDA

**Leveraged Scaling (Moderate Value):**
- Apprentice model with senior oversight
- Standardized methodologies
- Some recurring revenue components
- Valuation: 5-7x EBITDA

**Platform Scaling (Highest Value):**
- Technology-enabled delivery
- Productized service components
- Recurring revenue model
- Limited marginal cost per new client
- Valuation: 8-12x EBITDA

**Solutions Delivered Indicators:**
- "Direct collaboration" suggests linear scaling
- No evidence of leverage model
- No platform or technology discussed
- Likely 3-4x EBITDA valuation if profitable

**Investment Implication:** Even if this is a good business, it may not be a good investment due to limited scaling potential.
