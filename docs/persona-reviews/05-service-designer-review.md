# Service Design Review: Solutions Delivered Website
**Reviewer:** Priya Sharma, Service Design Lead
**Date:** November 20, 2025
**Review Type:** Service Ecosystem & Journey Continuity Analysis

---

## Executive Summary

As a Service Design Lead, I've conducted a comprehensive review of the Solutions Delivered website through the lens of the broader service ecosystem. While the website demonstrates strong visual design and clear messaging, it operates largely as an isolated digital touchpoint with limited integration into a holistic service journey. The experience feels very much like a brochure website with a contact form, rather than a connected node in a multi-channel service ecosystem.

The fundamental issue is that the website doesn't acknowledge or facilitate the reality of how people actually interact with business services - through multiple channels, at different stages of readiness, with varying needs for human contact, and with expectations of continuity across touchpoints.

**Overall Rating: 5.5/10**

---

## 1. Strengths

### Clear Journey Structure
The website establishes a logical progression from awareness (Home) → consideration (Solutions, About) → decision (How We Work) → action (Get Started). Each page builds on the previous one, creating narrative continuity that guides users through the sales funnel.

### Well-Designed Contact Touchpoint
The contact form on the Get Started page is thoughtfully designed with proper validation, accessibility features, and clear field labels. The form sets explicit expectations about response times (24 hours) and next steps, which is excellent for managing user anticipation.

### Consistent Messaging Across Pages
Throughout the website, the messaging around "Direct Team Collaboration," "No-Bloat Philosophy," and "WCAG 2.2 Compliant" remains consistent. This consistency would translate well if users encountered the brand through other channels, creating a coherent brand experience.

### Explicit Service Expectations
The "How We Work" page does an admirable job of setting clear expectations around communication, delivery, pricing, and working arrangements. This transparency helps align user expectations with service reality - a crucial element of service design.

### Process Transparency
The three-step process on the Get Started page (Review → Consultation → Proposal) gives users visibility into what happens after form submission, reducing anxiety about the unknown.

### Technical Foundation
The website's WCAG 2.2 compliance and accessible design patterns show consideration for diverse user needs, which is foundational for inclusive service delivery.

---

## 2. Weaknesses

### Single-Channel Dependency
The website exists as a standalone digital touchpoint with no visible integration to other channels. There's no phone number, no live chat, no social media links, and no indication of how the website connects to other ways of reaching the business. This creates a fragile service experience - if the contact form fails or if a user prefers phone contact, there's no alternative path.

### No Real-Time Support
For a consultancy that emphasizes "direct team collaboration," the absence of any synchronous communication channel is jarring. Users experiencing urgent issues or those who prefer real-time conversation have no recourse. The website assumes everyone is comfortable with asynchronous form-based communication.

### Lack of Progress Tracking
Once someone submits the contact form, they enter a black box. There's no confirmation email mentioned, no tracking number, no way to check the status of their inquiry. Users who submitted a form three days ago have no way to know if it was received, if it's being reviewed, or if they should follow up.

### No Service Recovery Mechanisms
What happens if the contact form fails? What if the email doesn't arrive? What if someone needs to update their request? The website provides no error recovery paths or alternative routes to resolution. The single success message doesn't account for the many things that could go wrong.

### Missing Channel Integration
The website doesn't acknowledge that people might want to:
- Start their inquiry online and finish it via phone
- Check their request status while on the go
- Escalate to a human quickly if needed
- Connect through their preferred social media channel
- Continue a conversation across devices

### No Account or Self-Service Capabilities
For a business consultancy, there's no way for existing clients to:
- Access project documentation
- Track project milestones
- Submit support requests
- View invoices or statements
- Manage their account

Everything requires initiating fresh contact, which doesn't scale and creates friction for returning clients.

### Absence of Emotional Journey Design
The website treats all visitors as if they're in the same emotional state - curious and optimistic. It doesn't acknowledge that someone might be:
- Frustrated with their current situation (needing reassurance)
- Anxious about making a wrong choice (needing social proof)
- Under time pressure (needing fast paths)
- Experiencing decision paralysis (needing simplification)

### Limited Trust-Building Mechanisms
While there are some trust indicators ("24 hour response time," "Free initial consultation"), there's a notable absence of:
- Client testimonials or case studies
- Industry certifications or partnerships
- Team profiles or photos
- Portfolio of past work
- Reviews or ratings

These elements would help users feel more confident in the service relationship.

## 3. Specific Issues

### Issue 1: No Human Contact Information
**Location:** Global (all pages)
**Severity:** High
**Description:** There is no phone number, physical address, or email address visible anywhere on the website. The only way to reach the business is through a web form. This creates a significant barrier for:
- Users who distrust web forms
- Users who need immediate answers
- Users with accessibility needs that make forms difficult
- Users experiencing technical issues with the form

**Service Impact:** This breaks the service journey for a significant segment of potential clients and creates no redundancy if the primary channel fails.

---

### Issue 2: No Post-Submission Confirmation
**Location:** Contact form (Get Started page)
**Severity:** High
**Description:** After form submission, users see a success message but receive no email confirmation. They have no proof of submission, no reference number, and no way to verify their message was received.

**Service Impact:** Users are left in limbo, unable to check status or confirm receipt. This creates anxiety and may lead to duplicate submissions or lost opportunities if users assume the form failed.

---

### Issue 3: No Status Tracking Mechanism
**Location:** Entire service journey
**Severity:** High
**Description:** There's no way for users to track where their inquiry is in the process. After the initial "24 hour response time" window, users have no visibility into progress.

**Service Impact:** Users cannot self-serve their status information, leading to increased anxiety and potential follow-up contacts that could be avoided with proactive status updates.

---

### Issue 4: No Channel Handoff Points
**Location:** All touchpoints
**Severity:** Medium
**Description:** The website doesn't provide clear paths to transition to other channels. There's no "Prefer to call? Here's our number" or "Want to chat live? Click here" options.

**Service Impact:** Users are locked into the web form channel regardless of their preference or situation, reducing flexibility and potentially losing clients who prefer different interaction modes.

---

### Issue 5: No Error Recovery Paths
**Location:** Contact form
**Severity:** Medium
**Description:** If form submission fails (network error, server error, validation error), there's no clear recovery path. The form validation helps prevent some errors, but there's no guidance for technical failures.

**Service Impact:** Users experiencing technical issues may abandon the process entirely, with no alternative path to reach the business.

---

### Issue 6: No Omnichannel Continuity
**Location:** Entire ecosystem
**Severity:** Medium
**Description:** There's no mechanism to start an interaction on desktop and continue on mobile, or start on web and continue via phone. Each interaction is isolated.

**Service Impact:** Modern users expect continuity across devices and channels. The lack of this creates friction and may force users to restart their journey multiple times.

---

### Issue 7: No Existing Client Path
**Location:** Navigation and homepage
**Severity:** Medium
**Description:** The website is entirely focused on acquiring new clients. There's no "Client Login," "Support Portal," or "Existing Clients" section for people who are already working with the business.

**Service Impact:** Existing clients must use the same contact form as prospects, creating inefficiency and making them feel less valued than new prospects.

---

### Issue 8: No Social Proof Integration
**Location:** All pages
**Severity:** Low-Medium
**Description:** While the website has strong content, there are no client testimonials, case studies, reviews, or third-party validation visible.

**Service Impact:** Users at the consideration stage have no social proof to validate their decision, potentially extending the decision-making process or causing drop-off.

---

## 4. Recommendations

### Immediate Priorities (High Impact, Quick Wins)

#### 1. Add Multi-Channel Contact Options
**Action:** Add phone number, email address, and physical address to the footer of every page.
**Rationale:** Provides channel redundancy and respects user preferences for different communication methods.
**Implementation:** Simple content addition to footer partial.

#### 2. Implement Email Confirmation
**Action:** Send automated confirmation email immediately after form submission with:
- Confirmation/reference number
- Copy of submitted information
- Timeline expectations
- Alternative contact methods

**Rationale:** Provides users with proof of submission and peace of mind.
**Implementation:** Add to ContactFormSubmitted mail class with auto-reply.

#### 3. Create Service Status Page
**Action:** Build a simple status-checking page where users can enter their email and/or reference number to see their inquiry status.
**Rationale:** Enables self-service status checks, reducing anxiety and support burden.
**Implementation:** New route with database query for submission status.

#### 4. Add Live Chat or Chatbot
**Action:** Implement a live chat widget (Intercom, Crisp, or similar) for real-time queries.
**Rationale:** Provides synchronous support for users who need immediate answers.
**Implementation:** Third-party integration with existing tools.

---

### Medium-Term Enhancements (Strategic Improvements)

#### 5. Build Client Portal
**Action:** Create a dedicated portal for existing clients to:
- View project status
- Access documentation
- Submit support requests
- View invoices

**Rationale:** Separates existing client journey from prospect journey, improving service for both groups.
**Implementation:** New authenticated section with project management integration.

#### 6. Add Social Proof Elements
**Action:** Create case study pages, add client testimonials, and display third-party certifications.
**Rationale:** Builds trust and helps users validate their decision.
**Implementation:** New content pages and components for testimonials.

#### 7. Implement Service Journey Mapping
**Action:** Create visual journey maps showing:
- Current stage in the process
- What happens next
- Estimated timelines
- Who is responsible

**Rationale:** Gives users visibility into the service process and reduces anxiety about unknowns.
**Implementation:** New journey tracking system with visual progress indicators.

#### 8. Design Error Recovery Paths
**Action:** Create explicit "Having trouble?" sections with:
- Alternative contact methods
- FAQ for common issues
- Troubleshooting guides
- Escalation paths

**Rationale:** Ensures users always have a way forward when things go wrong.
**Implementation:** New help section and improved error messaging.

---

### Long-Term Strategic Initiatives

#### 9. Build Omnichannel Continuity
**Action:** Implement systems that allow users to:
- Start on web, continue via phone with context preserved
- Receive SMS/email updates about their inquiry
- Access their information across devices
- Resume conversations across channels

**Rationale:** Meets modern user expectations for seamless cross-channel experiences.
**Implementation:** CRM integration with channel coordination.

#### 10. Develop Emotional Journey Touchpoints
**Action:** Design different paths for different emotional states:
- "Urgent Help Needed" fast track
- "Just Exploring" low-commitment path
- "Ready to Start" streamlined process

**Rationale:** Acknowledges that users arrive with different needs and emotional states.
**Implementation:** Personalized journeys with appropriate content and CTAs.

#### 11. Create Service Ecosystem Map
**Action:** Document all touchpoints (website, phone, email, in-person, social media) and design handoffs between them.
**Rationale:** Ensures consistent experience regardless of how users enter or move through the service.
**Implementation:** Service blueprint workshop and implementation roadmap.

#### 12. Implement Proactive Communication
**Action:** Set up automated touchpoints:
- Day 1: Confirmation email
- Day 2: "We're reviewing your request" update
- Day 7: Status update if still in progress
- Monthly: Newsletter for prospects

**Rationale:** Keeps users informed without requiring them to check status manually.
**Implementation:** Marketing automation platform integration.

## 6. Overall Rating: 5.5/10

### Rating Breakdown

**Journey Structure (7/10):** The linear path from awareness to contact is clear and logical, but it's too rigid with no accommodation for different user journeys.

**Channel Integration (2/10):** Severely limited. The website is an isolated touchpoint with no visible connection to other channels.

**Touchpoint Consistency (5/10):** Cannot fully assess as other touchpoints aren't visible, but internal consistency within the website is good.

**Support Access (4/10):** Contact form is well-designed, but lack of alternative channels and real-time support is a significant gap.

**Progress Tracking (1/10):** Essentially non-existent. No visibility into status or progress.

**Emotional Design (3/10):** Some trust-building elements present, but no acknowledgment of different emotional states or varying levels of urgency.

**Service Recovery (2/10):** Minimal error handling with no clear recovery paths.

**Self-Service (3/10):** Very limited. Users cannot complete any tasks without human intervention.

**Omnichannel Experience (1/10):** Not present. Each interaction is isolated with no cross-channel or cross-device continuity.

**Ecosystem Integration (2/10):** No visible integration with other services, CRMs, or partner systems.

---

## Final Thoughts

From a service design perspective, the Solutions Delivered website is a well-executed single touchpoint that unfortunately doesn't yet function as part of a broader service ecosystem. It's like having a beautiful front door to a building with no clear indication of what's behind it, how to get help if the door is locked, or how to continue your journey once you've knocked.

The good news is that the foundation is solid - the website is accessible, well-designed, and communicates clearly. The challenge is extending this quality into a multi-channel service experience that meets users where they are, acknowledges their emotional states, and provides continuity throughout their entire journey.

> "A website is just one touchpoint in a much larger service journey."

This website needs to embrace that philosophy by connecting to other touchpoints, providing multiple paths to value, and designing for the full spectrum of user needs and preferences.

For a consultancy that specializes in "process design" and "business change," demonstrating these principles in their own service delivery would be a powerful proof point for potential clients.
