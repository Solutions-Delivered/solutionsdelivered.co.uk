# Churned Customer Win-Back Assessment: Solutions Delivered Website
**Reviewer:** Martin Hughes, Managing Director
**Former Customer:** 18 months (left 6 months ago)
**Review Date:** November 20, 2025
**Overall Rating:** 4/10

---

## Executive Summary

I'm reviewing this website as someone who was once a customer of Solutions Delivered. I worked with them for 18 months before leaving 6 months ago. As I revisit their website today, I'm looking for answers to one critical question: **Has anything changed that would address the reasons I left?**

From what I see, the website has clearly been refreshed—it's modern, accessible, and professionally designed. But here's the problem: there's no evidence that the underlying service delivery, communication issues, or flexibility concerns that caused me to leave have actually been addressed. The website tells me what they *say* they do, but gives me no reason to believe they've actually *improved* since I was a customer.

**Key Finding:** This is a missed opportunity for win-back. The website focuses on acquiring new customers but completely ignores former customers who might reconsider if shown evidence of genuine change. There's no acknowledgment of past shortcomings, no evidence of improvements, no testimonials from long-term retained customers, and no incentive for me to give them a second chance.

As someone who left for specific reasons, I need more than a fresh coat of paint. I need proof that those reasons no longer exist.

---

## Strengths

### 1. Modern, Professional Web Presence
The website is a significant improvement from what I remember. It's clean, accessible (WCAG 2.2 compliant), fast, and works well on mobile. This suggests they've invested in their digital presence, which is encouraging.

If poor digital presence was why someone left, this would address it. For me, it wasn't, so this is nice but not sufficient.

### 2. Clear Communication Commitments
The "How We Work" page now explicitly promises:
- 24-hour response during working days
- Direct access without account managers
- Regular progress updates
- Clear, jargon-free explanations

**This addresses one of my concerns.** Communication was inconsistent when I was a customer. If they're now committing to specific SLAs and direct access, that's a genuine improvement—but I'd need to verify they're actually delivering on these promises before believing it.

### 3. Transparent Process and Expectations
The four-step engagement process (Initial Discussion → Proposal & Planning → Collaborative Delivery → Handover & Support) is clearly outlined. This transparency is better than what I experienced, where expectations weren't always clearly set upfront.

The "What to Expect" section is particularly good—it sets realistic expectations about quality over speed, which suggests they've learned not to overpromise.

### 4. Flexible Pricing Models Mentioned
The site mentions both fixed-price and day-rate options, plus "flexible payment terms for ongoing engagements."

**This partially addresses another concern I had.** Their pricing wasn't flexible when I needed it, which contributed to budget pressure that led me to explore alternatives. If they've genuinely become more flexible, that's progress.

### 5. Values Alignment
The emphasis on "quality, accessibility, and long-term maintainability over quick fixes" and "collaborative partnerships over transactional vendor relationships" resonates with what I was looking for originally.

If they actually practice this consistently now (a big if), it would address my concerns about short-term thinking and lack of partnership mentality.

### 6. Easy Reconnection Path
The contact form is simple and low-friction. There's no barrier to reaching out again, which is good. I could easily send a message saying "I'm a former customer interested in discussing whether things have changed."

### 7. Remote-First Working Mentioned
The "Working Arrangements" section acknowledges they're remote-first with flexible scheduling and on-site visits available when needed. This is more flexible than their approach when I was a customer.

---

## Weaknesses

### 1. Zero Acknowledgment of Why Customers Leave
**Critical Issue:** The website acts as if customer churn doesn't exist. There's no recognition that some customers may not be satisfied, no acknowledgment of common complaints, and no section addressing "Why Previous Customers Left and Why They Came Back."

As a former customer, this feels like they're either unaware they have retention issues, or they're deliberately avoiding the topic. Neither inspires confidence that they've learned from past mistakes.

I want to see:
- Honest acknowledgment of common issues they've experienced
- What they've done to fix these issues
- Evidence that other churned customers have returned successfully

### 2. No Evidence of Improvements or Innovation
**Critical Issue:** The website tells me what they offer, but gives no indication of what's new, improved, or different from 6-12 months ago.

Missing:
- "What's New" section highlighting recent improvements
- Timeline showing evolution of services or approach
- Blog posts or updates demonstrating continuous improvement
- New service offerings that didn't exist when I was a customer
- Process improvements or methodology changes
- New team members bringing fresh expertise

Without this, I have to assume everything is exactly the same as when I left. **Why would I expect different results?**

### 3. Missing Customer Success and Retention Stories
**Critical Issue:** There are no testimonials, case studies, or customer success stories anywhere on the site.

What I need to see:
- **Long-term customer testimonials** - "We've worked with Solutions Delivered for 3+ years because..."
- **Retention stories** - "We considered leaving but stayed because..."
- **Comeback stories** - "We left but came back when they..."
- **Relationship quality evidence** - Testimonials that speak to partnership, communication, and adaptation

The absence of this content suggests either:
- They don't have many long-term retained customers (red flag)
- They haven't thought about showcasing retention (oversight)
- Customer satisfaction isn't strong enough to generate testimonials (major red flag)

For a churned customer, this is the most important missing element. I need social proof that other customers are happy and staying.

### 4. No Win-Back Offers or Incentives
**Critical Issue:** There's nothing specifically designed to encourage former customers to reconsider.

Missing opportunities:
- "Welcome Back" offer or incentive for returning customers
- Special terms for former clients who want to reconnect
- Complimentary review or assessment for previous customers
- "We've Changed" campaign highlighting specific improvements
- Acknowledgment that relationships can be repaired

As a former customer, I need something more than "fill out this form." I need recognition of our past relationship and an incentive to give them another chance.

### 5. No Transparency About Past Issues
The website promises transparency going forward but shows no transparency about past challenges, lessons learned, or improvements made.

I want to see:
- Honest discussion of common pain points they've addressed
- "How We've Improved" section
- Acknowledgment of feedback that drove changes
- Specific process or policy improvements implemented

Without this, "transparency" feels like marketing speak rather than genuine openness.

### 6. Generic "What We Look For" in Customers
The "When We're a Good Fit" section describes ideal customers but doesn't help me assess if the issues I experienced would be handled differently now.

It should address:
- What happens when budget constraints emerge mid-project
- How they handle changing requirements or scope
- What their escalation process is when communication breaks down
- How they adapt when client needs evolve

### 7. No Service Level Guarantees or Commitment Backing
They promise 24-hour responses and regular updates, but there's:
- No explanation of what happens if they miss these commitments
- No service level agreement details
- No recourse if expectations aren't met
- No satisfaction guarantee or trial period for returning customers

**When you've been burned before, promises without backing feel empty.**

### 8. Missing Flexibility Evidence
While they mention "flexible pricing" and "flexible scheduling," there's no detail about:
- How flexible they really are in practice
- Examples of how they've adapted to client needs
- Range of engagement models available
- Options for scaling up or down
- How they handle budget adjustments

As someone who left partly due to inflexibility, I need concrete evidence they've changed, not just marketing claims.

### 9. No Customer Feedback or Review System
There's no independent verification of quality:
- No Google Reviews integration
- No Trustpilot or similar ratings
- No LinkedIn recommendations displayed
- No mechanism for current/past customers to leave feedback
- No public accountability

Without this, I can't verify their claims through independent sources.

### 10. No Content Demonstrating Expertise Evolution
There's no blog, insights, thought leadership, or content showing they're actively improving their expertise and staying current.

Missing:
- Articles about lessons learned
- Updates on methodology improvements
- Industry insights and expertise
- Evidence they're learning and evolving
- Content addressing current business challenges

A stagnant consultancy is a concerning consultancy, especially for someone who already left once.

### 11. Team Information Completely Absent
I have no idea:
- Who I'd be working with if I returned
- Whether the same people are still there
- If they've added expertise in areas where they were weak
- What qualifications or experience the team has
- If there's continuity or if it's entirely different people

**Team changes matter.** If the person I had issues with is gone, that's relevant. If they've added senior expertise, that's relevant. But I have no visibility.

### 12. No Discussion of How Relationships are Managed
The site promises "direct collaboration" but doesn't explain:
- How account management works
- What their escalation process is if issues arise
- How they handle difficult conversations
- Their approach to conflict resolution
- How they maintain long-term relationships

As someone who experienced relationship breakdown, this information would help me assess if things would be different.

## Specific Issues

### Issue 1: The Website Doesn't Speak to My Situation
**Context:** I'm not a new prospect evaluating consultancies for the first time. I'm a former customer who already knows Solutions Delivered, already experienced both their strengths and weaknesses, and left for specific reasons.

**Problem:** The website is designed entirely for new customer acquisition. There's no content, no messaging, and no offers targeted at:
- Former customers considering a return
- Customers who are currently dissatisfied
- Long-term customers evaluating whether to stay
- Anyone with existing knowledge of the company

**Impact:** I feel invisible. The website doesn't acknowledge I exist or care whether I come back.

**What I Need:** A dedicated section like:
- "Thinking of Giving Us Another Try?"
- "What's Changed Since You Worked With Us"
- "Why Former Customers Come Back"
- "We've Listened and Improved"

### Issue 2: No Evidence They've Learned from Churn
**Context:** Every consultancy loses customers. The question is whether they understand *why* and whether they've adapted.

**Problem:** There's no indication that customer feedback, churn analysis, or lessons learned have driven any improvements. This suggests they might not even know why customers leave or might not take it seriously.

**Impact:** If they don't understand why I left (or why others like me left), how can they prevent it from happening again? Why would I risk the same outcome?

**What I Need:**
- Clear evidence they track and analyze customer satisfaction
- Examples of changes made based on customer feedback
- Acknowledgment of common retention challenges
- Demonstrated commitment to continuous improvement based on real customer experiences

### Issue 3: Missing Comparative Context
**Context:** I left Solutions Delivered and moved to a competitor. That competitor does some things better, some things worse.

**Problem:** The website doesn't help me compare what I'll get if I return versus what I have now. There's no clear differentiation, no compelling reason to switch back.

**Impact:** Inertia wins. It's easier to stay with my current provider (despite their flaws) than to switch back without clear evidence Solutions Delivered is now superior in the areas that matter to me.

**What I Need:**
- Clear differentiation versus typical competitors
- Specific advantages I'd gain by returning
- Honest acknowledgment of who they're best for (and who they're not)
- Compelling reason why now is the right time to reconsider

### Issue 4: No Risk Reduction for Returning Customers
**Context:** I already took a risk working with them once, and it didn't work out. Returning means taking that risk again—but this time with the added psychological burden of "going back" after leaving.

**Problem:** There's nothing to reduce the perceived risk of returning:
- No trial period or limited initial engagement to test the waters
- No satisfaction guarantee
- No special terms acknowledging the past relationship
- No exit flexibility if it doesn't work out again

**Impact:** The psychological and business risk of returning feels too high compared to staying with my current (imperfect but familiar) provider.

**What I Need:**
- Low-commitment way to re-engage (pilot project, assessment, trial period)
- Clear exit strategy if it doesn't work out
- Acknowledgment that trust needs to be rebuilt
- Incentive that demonstrates they value winning me back

### Issue 5: Testimonials Would Address Trust Gap
**Context:** I know my experience was real. But I don't know if it was typical, anomalous, or something they've since fixed.

**Problem:** Without testimonials from customers who:
- Stayed long-term and explain why
- Had issues that were resolved successfully
- Experienced the specific strengths they're claiming
- Can vouch for their reliability and partnership approach

...I have only my own negative experience to reference.

**Impact:** Negative experiences have stronger psychological weight than marketing promises. Without counterbalancing social proof, my memory of problems outweighs their claims of solutions.

**What I Need:**
- Diverse testimonials showing consistent positive experiences
- Specifically, testimonials addressing communication, flexibility, and relationship quality
- Evidence others have had better experiences than I did
- Ideally, testimonials from customers in similar situations to mine

### Issue 6: The "Collaborative Approach" Claims Need Proof
**Context:** The site heavily emphasizes "collaborative partnerships," "direct collaboration," and "working alongside you—not just for you."

**Problem:** These were claimed when I was a customer too, but the reality didn't always match. Nice words mean nothing without evidence of actual behavior.

**Impact:** I'm cynical about these claims because I've heard them before and experienced something different. Without proof, this messaging actually works *against* them because it reminds me of the gap between promise and reality.

**What I Need:**
- Specific examples of how collaboration works in practice
- Customer testimonials that speak to actual collaborative experiences
- Process details showing *how* they ensure collaboration
- Accountability mechanisms if collaboration breaks down

## Recommendations

### High Priority - Address These to Improve Win-Back Potential

#### 1. Create a "Why Customers Come Back" or "We've Improved" Section
**Action:** Add a dedicated page or prominent section acknowledging that some customers leave and explaining why some return.

Include:
- Honest acknowledgment of common reasons customers leave consultancies
- Specific improvements you've made in the last 12-18 months
- Changes in process, communication, or service delivery
- New capabilities or team members
- Customer stories of successful returns

**Why This Matters:** Shows self-awareness, demonstrates growth, and gives former customers a reason to reconsider.

#### 2. Add Long-Term Customer Testimonials and Case Studies
**Action:** Prioritize testimonials from customers who:
- Have worked with you 2+ years
- Can speak to relationship quality, communication, and flexibility
- Had challenges that were successfully resolved
- Chose to expand their engagement over time

Include metrics like:
- Average customer relationship length
- Customer retention rate
- Percentage of repeat/expanded engagements

**Why This Matters:** Proves that customers stay when satisfied and that relationships can be successful long-term.

#### 3. Implement a Win-Back Campaign/Offer
**Action:** Create a specific offer for former customers:
- "Welcome Back Assessment" - complimentary review of their current situation
- Special pricing for returning customers on first project
- Trial engagement or pilot project structure
- Dedicated account management for returning customers
- Clear exit terms if it doesn't work out

**Why This Matters:** Reduces risk and demonstrates you value winning back former customers.

#### 4. Add Transparency About Improvements and Changes
**Action:** Create a "What's New" or "How We've Improved" section showing:
- Timeline of major process improvements
- New service offerings
- Team additions and expertise expansion
- Client feedback-driven changes
- Methodology evolution

**Why This Matters:** Shows continuous improvement and gives former customers concrete evidence things are different now.

#### 5. Display Independent Reviews and Ratings
**Action:** Integrate:
- Google Reviews (with responses to negative reviews)
- Trustpilot or similar independent platform
- LinkedIn recommendations
- Industry certifications or awards

**Why This Matters:** Provides unbiased verification of quality and demonstrates accountability.

#### 6. Add Team Information and Expertise
**Action:** Create an "Our Team" page showing:
- Key team members and their backgrounds
- Relevant certifications and experience
- Areas of expertise
- How team structure supports client success

**Why This Matters:** Helps former customers assess if they'd be working with the same people and whether expertise gaps have been filled.

### Medium Priority - Strengthen Overall Value Proposition

#### 7. Develop Content Demonstrating Expertise Evolution
**Action:** Launch a blog or insights section with:
- Lessons learned from recent projects
- Industry trends and analysis
- Methodology improvements
- Common challenges and solutions
- Thought leadership

**Why This Matters:** Shows active learning, current expertise, and thought leadership.

#### 8. Add Detailed Flexibility Examples
**Action:** Expand the pricing and working arrangements sections with:
- Specific examples of flexible engagement models
- How you handle scope or budget changes
- Options for scaling up/down
- Case studies showing adaptation to client needs

**Why This Matters:** Proves flexibility claims with concrete examples.

#### 9. Create Service Level Agreement Details
**Action:** Provide specifics about:
- Response time commitments with escalation if missed
- Communication frequency and formats
- Deliverable quality standards
- Process for handling issues or complaints
- What happens if expectations aren't met

**Why This Matters:** Makes promises concrete and accountable.

#### 10. Develop "When We're Not a Good Fit" Content
**Action:** Be honest about:
- Types of clients or projects you don't take on
- When customers should choose competitors instead
- Red flags that indicate poor fit
- How you handle situations where fit is questionable

**Why This Matters:** Builds trust through honesty and helps right-fit clients self-identify.

### Lower Priority - Nice to Have

#### 11. Add Industry-Specific Content
Show expertise in specific sectors with relevant case studies and pain points.

#### 12. Create an FAQ Section
Address common questions including "What if we worked together before and it didn't work out?"

#### 13. Add a Newsletter or Content Subscription
Give former customers a low-commitment way to stay informed about improvements and changes.

## Final Thoughts

As a former customer who left 6 months ago, I approached this website with a specific question: **"Have the reasons I left been addressed?"**

Unfortunately, I can't answer that question based on this website. The site is professionally designed and makes good promises, but it provides no evidence that anything substantive has changed since I was a customer. It's entirely possible they've made significant improvements—better communication systems, more flexible processes, stronger quality control, improved project management—but none of this is visible or validated.

**The fundamental problem:** This website treats me like a stranger who knows nothing about Solutions Delivered, when in reality I know them quite well—and not all of it is positive. What I need is different from what a new prospect needs:

**New Prospect Needs:**
- What services do you offer?
- How do you work?
- What will it cost?
- Can I trust you?

**Former Customer Needs:**
- What's changed since I left?
- Have you addressed the issues I experienced?
- Are other customers happy and staying long-term?
- What's my incentive to take another risk with you?
- How can I verify you've genuinely improved?

This website answers the prospect questions but ignores the former customer questions entirely.

### Would I Reach Out?

**Honest answer: Probably not, based on this website alone.**

Here's why: Switching back to a former vendor is psychologically harder than switching to a new vendor. I'd need compelling evidence that:

1. The specific issues I experienced have been fixed
2. Other customers are successfully retained long-term
3. The improvements are real, not just marketing
4. There's lower risk this time (trial period, guarantees, etc.)
5. There's a tangible benefit to returning versus staying with current provider

I see none of this evidence. So while I might casually wonder "I wonder if they've improved," I wouldn't invest the time and energy to find out. The barrier to re-engagement is too high, and the potential upside isn't demonstrated.

### What Would Make Me Reconsider?

If I saw:

1. **A "Welcome Back" campaign** acknowledging former customers and offering a no-risk way to reconnect (free assessment, pilot project, special terms)

2. **Clear evidence of improvements** with specifics about what's changed in the last 6-12 months and why

3. **Long-term customer testimonials** proving others are happy and staying, especially testimonials addressing communication, flexibility, and partnership quality

4. **Independent verification** through Google Reviews, Trustpilot, or similar showing consistent positive experiences

5. **Honest acknowledgment** that some customers leave and transparent discussion of common issues and how they're addressed

6. **Team information** showing whether the people I worked with are still there or if new expertise has been added

7. **Service level commitments** with teeth—specific SLAs with consequences if not met

**Then** I'd consider reaching out.

### The Bottom Line

Solutions Delivered has done good work on their website design and messaging for new customer acquisition. But they've completely missed the opportunity to win back former customers who might reconsider if given evidence of genuine change.

**For former customers like me, this website raises a concerning question:** If they haven't thought about win-back and customer retention enough to address it on their website, have they thought about it enough to fix the underlying issues that cause churn?

I genuinely hope they've improved their service delivery and client relationships. But until they demonstrate it with evidence, I'll never know—and I'll never come back to find out.

**My advice to Solutions Delivered:** Don't let improved services hide behind an incomplete website. If you've genuinely addressed the issues that cause customers to leave, shout it from the rooftops. Show the receipts. Prove it. Because there are former customers like me who might give you another chance—but only if you give us a reason to believe things would be different this time.
