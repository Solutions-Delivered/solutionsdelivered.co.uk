# Customer Success Manager Website Review
**Reviewer:** Laura Bennett
**Role:** Customer Success Manager
**Date:** November 20, 2025
**Website:** Solutions Delivered (solutionsdelivered.co.uk)

---

## 1. Executive Summary

As a Customer Success Manager responsible for retention, expansion, and customer satisfaction, I find the Solutions Delivered website to be severely lacking in the resources and tools I need to support existing customers effectively. While the site does an adequate job of attracting new business with clear service descriptions and a professional presentation, it completely fails to address the needs of current customers or provide CSMs like me with the materials necessary to drive customer success and reduce churn.

**Overall Rating: 3/10**

The website is essentially a lead generation tool with no post-sale customer success infrastructure. This represents a critical gap that will make my job significantly harder and likely contribute to higher churn rates.

---

## 2. Strengths

Despite the significant weaknesses, there are a few positive aspects worth noting:

### 2.1 Clear Service Documentation
The Solutions page provides decent descriptions of the four core services (Web Development, Service Management, Project Management, Business Change). This gives me baseline material to reference when discussing expansion opportunities with customers, though it lacks the depth I'd need for meaningful value conversations.

### 2.2 Professional Process Overview
The "How We Work" page outlines a four-step engagement process with clear expectations around communication, delivery, pricing, and working arrangements. This is useful for setting customer expectations and could be referenced during onboarding, though it's clearly written for prospects rather than existing customers.

### 2.3 Transparent Company Values
The About page articulates the company's mission and values (Integrity, Collaboration, Excellence), which I can reference when reinforcing the partnership approach with customers. However, these feel generic and don't translate into tangible customer success metrics.

### 2.4 Accessible Design
The website's commitment to WCAG 2.2 compliance is mentioned prominently, which demonstrates the company takes accessibility seriously. This is a talking point I can use with customers who value inclusive design, though there's no detailed documentation about accessibility features that would help me educate customers.

### 2.5 Direct Communication Emphasis
The site emphasizes "direct team collaboration" and "no account managers or layers of bureaucracy," which aligns with a high-touch customer success model. This sets appropriate expectations, though it doesn't provide me with any tools to scale my efforts across multiple accounts.

---

## 3. Weaknesses

The weaknesses far outweigh the strengths. Here are the major deficiencies from a customer success perspective:

### 3.1 Complete Absence of Customer Resources
There is absolutely no knowledge base, resource center, or documentation library for existing customers. This means:
- Customers can't self-serve answers to common questions
- I have to respond to the same questions repeatedly
- No centralized location to direct customers for help
- Missing opportunity for customer education and adoption

### 3.2 No Success Stories or Social Proof
The website contains zero case studies, testimonials, customer success stories, or reviews. This is a massive gap because:
- I can't share relevant examples with customers facing similar challenges
- No proof points to demonstrate ROI during renewal conversations
- Missing content for customer marketing and advocacy programs
- No benchmark data to help customers understand success metrics

### 3.3 Lack of Expansion Path Documentation
While four services are described, there's no clear documentation of how services build on each other or natural expansion paths. I need:
- Service maturity models showing progression opportunities
- Complementary service combinations
- Pricing or packaging information for add-on services
- Use cases for expanding from one service to multiple

### 3.4 No Self-Service Portal
There's no customer portal, dashboard, or self-service area where customers can:
- Access their project information
- View documentation or deliverables
- Submit support requests
- Track their usage or engagement
- Access training materials

### 3.5 Missing ROI and Value Tools
I have no tools to demonstrate value or ROI to customer stakeholders:
- No calculators or frameworks to quantify business impact
- No templates for building business cases
- No metrics or KPIs to track customer success
- No value realization documentation

### 3.6 Absence of Training Materials
There are no resources to help customers maximize value from their services:
- No best practices guides
- No how-to documentation
- No training videos or webinars
- No certification programs
- No onboarding materials

### 3.7 No Community or Networking Opportunities
The website offers no way for customers to connect with each other:
- No customer forum or community
- No user groups or events
- No peer networking opportunities
- No customer advisory board information

### 3.8 Lack of Product/Service Updates Communication
There's no section for communicating new features, services, or improvements:
- No changelog or release notes
- No roadmap visibility
- No way to collect customer feedback
- No announcement system for updates

### 3.9 Missing Internal CSM Resources
I can't find any internal resources to help me do my job better:
- No CSM playbooks or guides
- No customer communication templates
- No talking points for common objections
- No renewal or upsell resources
- No customer health scoring frameworks

### 3.10 No Support Infrastructure
The website only offers a contact form with a promise of 24-hour response. There's no:
- Dedicated support portal
- FAQ section
- Troubleshooting guides
- Service level agreement (SLA) information
- Escalation process documentation

## 4. Specific Issues

### 4.1 Contact Form Is Only Entry Point
**Issue:** The only way to contact the company is through a single contact form on the "Get Started" page.

**Impact:**
- Existing customers have the same entry point as new prospects
- No differentiation between sales inquiries and support requests
- No way for customers to track their request status
- Creates confusion and potential delays in response

**Recommendation:** Create separate contact paths for prospects, customers, and partners with appropriate routing and tracking.

---

### 4.2 No Onboarding Materials
**Issue:** The website provides no resources for newly onboarded customers.

**Impact:**
- I have to manually create onboarding materials for each customer
- Inconsistent customer experience across accounts
- Longer time to value for new customers
- Higher risk of early-stage churn

**Recommendation:** Develop a comprehensive onboarding resource center with service-specific guides, video tutorials, and quick-start materials.

---

### 4.3 Missing Value Proposition for Existing Customers
**Issue:** All content is written for prospects, not existing customers. There's no "existing customer" section or value proposition for why they should stay or expand.

**Impact:**
- Customers don't see ongoing value communicated
- No reinforcement of their decision to work with us
- Missed opportunity to drive expansion
- Harder to justify renewals

**Recommendation:** Create a dedicated "Customer Success" or "Resources" section with content specifically for existing customers, highlighting ongoing value, new capabilities, and expansion opportunities.

---

### 4.4 No Metrics or Health Tracking
**Issue:** There are no tools or dashboards for tracking customer health, usage, or engagement.

**Impact:**
- I can't proactively identify at-risk customers
- No early warning signals for churn
- Difficult to prioritize my time across accounts
- Reactive rather than proactive support model

**Recommendation:** Implement a customer health scoring system with a dashboard showing key metrics like engagement, adoption, satisfaction, and renewal likelihood.

---

### 4.5 Lack of Customer Education Content
**Issue:** No blog, resources section, or educational content to help customers improve their operations or get more value from services.

**Impact:**
- Customers can't learn best practices
- No thought leadership to maintain engagement
- Missing opportunity for value-add beyond direct services
- Customers may not adopt all available features or capabilities

**Recommendation:** Launch a resources section with blog posts, whitepapers, webinars, and guides focused on customer success topics relevant to each service area.

---

### 4.6 No Evidence of Customer-Centricity
**Issue:** The entire website is about the company, not about customers and their success.

**Impact:**
- Feels transactional rather than partnership-oriented
- Doesn't reinforce the "collaborative approach" messaging
- Customers may question commitment to their success
- Misalignment between stated values and website experience

**Recommendation:** Reorient content to be customer-centric, highlighting customer outcomes, success stories, and the partnership experience.

---

### 4.7 Missing Renewal and Expansion Tools
**Issue:** There are no resources to support renewal conversations or expansion discussions.

**Impact:**
- I have to build every business case from scratch
- No standardized ROI frameworks or templates
- Difficult to demonstrate value quantitatively
- Longer sales cycles for expansion opportunities
- Higher risk of price objections at renewal

**Recommendation:** Create renewal and expansion toolkits with ROI calculators, case studies, benchmark data, and business case templates.

---

### 4.8 No Customer Feedback Mechanism
**Issue:** There's no visible way for customers to provide feedback, request features, or influence the roadmap.

**Impact:**
- Customers don't feel heard or valued
- Missing opportunity to collect actionable insights
- No transparency into how feedback is used
- Reduced customer engagement and loyalty

**Recommendation:** Implement a customer feedback portal with feature requests, voting, and roadmap visibility to demonstrate responsiveness to customer needs.

---

### 4.9 Absence of Customer Success Stories
**Issue:** No case studies, testimonials, or success metrics that I can share with customers or use in renewal discussions.

**Impact:**
- Can't demonstrate proven value to stakeholders
- No peer validation for customers to reference
- Missed opportunity for customer advocacy
- Harder to justify investment during renewals

**Recommendation:** Develop a robust library of customer success stories with quantified results, organized by industry, service type, and use case.

---

### 4.10 No Multi-Stakeholder Resources
**Issue:** All content assumes a single point of contact, with no resources tailored to different stakeholder roles within customer organizations.

**Impact:**
- I can't easily engage with economic buyers, champions, or end users separately
- No executive-level content for C-suite stakeholders
- Missing technical documentation for implementation teams
- One-size-fits-all approach doesn't address varied stakeholder needs

**Recommendation:** Create role-specific resources (executive summaries, technical documentation, user guides) to support multi-stakeholder engagement.

---

## 5. Recommendations

Based on my review, here are my prioritized recommendations for improving the website from a customer success perspective:

### Priority 1: Critical (Immediate Action Required)

1. **Launch a Customer Resource Center**
   - Create a dedicated section for existing customers
   - Include FAQs, best practices, how-to guides
   - Organize content by service type and customer journey stage
   - Make searchable and easy to navigate

2. **Develop Customer Success Stories and Case Studies**
   - Document at least 3-5 case studies across different service areas
   - Include quantified results and ROI metrics
   - Create customer testimonial videos
   - Make easily shareable and downloadable

3. **Implement a Customer Support Portal**
   - Separate support requests from sales inquiries
   - Provide ticket tracking and status updates
   - Include SLA information and escalation paths
   - Enable self-service for common issues

4. **Create ROI and Value Demonstration Tools**
   - Build ROI calculators for each service type
   - Develop value realization frameworks
   - Create business case templates
   - Provide benchmark data for customer comparisons

10. **Build a Customer Community**

## 8. Critical Concerns

As a Customer Success Manager, I have serious concerns about how this website impacts my ability to do my job effectively:

### Churn Risk
Without customer resources, self-service tools, or ongoing value communication, customers will struggle to see the continued value of our services. This directly increases churn risk, especially for customers who aren't getting high-touch support from me personally.

### Scalability Issues
I cannot scale my support across multiple customer accounts when there are no self-service resources. This means I'm constantly pulled into reactive support rather than proactive success planning, which limits the number of customers I can effectively manage.

### Renewal Challenges
The absence of ROI tools, case studies, and value demonstration resources makes every renewal conversation harder than it needs to be. I'm starting from scratch each time instead of leveraging proven frameworks and evidence.

### Expansion Friction
Without clear documentation of expansion paths, service combinations, or maturity models, I'm missing natural opportunities to drive growth within existing accounts. This represents significant lost revenue.

### Customer Perception
The website sends a message that the company is focused on acquiring new business, not supporting existing customers. This contradicts the "partnership" messaging and could undermine customer trust and satisfaction.

---

## 9. Key Recommendations Summary

If I could only implement three changes, they would be:

1. **Create a comprehensive customer resource center** with FAQs, best practices, documentation, and self-service tools. This would immediately reduce my reactive support burden and improve customer satisfaction.

2. **Develop 5-10 customer success stories** with quantified results that I can use in renewal and expansion conversations. This would provide the social proof and ROI evidence I desperately need.

3. **Build ROI calculators and value frameworks** for each service type to standardize how we demonstrate and measure customer success. This would make renewals smoother and create a consistent approach to value realization.

---

## 10. Final Thoughts

The Solutions Delivered website is professionally executed from a marketing and lead generation perspective, but it completely ignores the post-sale customer experience. For a company that emphasizes "collaboration" and "partnership," the lack of customer success infrastructure is glaring.

My job is to ensure customer success, reduce churn, and identify expansion opportunities. This website gives me almost nothing to work with. Every customer interaction requires manual effort, custom materials, and reinventing the wheel. This is unsustainable as the customer base grows.

The good news is that the foundation is solid—the website is well-designed, accessible, and professionally presented. Adding customer success features wouldn't require a rebuild, just strategic additions to serve existing customers as well as prospects.

Until these gaps are addressed, I'll continue to struggle with:
- High support burden from lack of self-service resources
- Difficulty demonstrating value at renewal time
- Limited ability to scale across multiple accounts
- Missed expansion opportunities
- Higher churn risk from lack of ongoing engagement

**Bottom line:** This website needs a customer success transformation to match the quality of its lead generation capabilities. The investment would pay dividends in improved retention, expansion revenue, and customer lifetime value.
