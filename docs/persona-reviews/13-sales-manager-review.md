# Sales Enablement Review: Solutions Delivered Website
**Reviewer:** James Mitchell, VP of Sales
**Review Date:** November 20, 2025
**Overall Rating:** 5/10

---

## Executive Summary

As VP of Sales with 18 years of experience in B2B consultative selling, I've reviewed the Solutions Delivered website through the lens of sales enablement and lead generation effectiveness. While the site has a solid foundation with clear messaging and good accessibility, it fundamentally lacks the tools and content needed to support an effective sales process.

The website presents a professional image and communicates services clearly, but it fails to qualify leads, demonstrate value through proof points, or equip a sales team with the resources needed to move prospects through the pipeline efficiently. In my experience, this type of site generates inquiries, but not necessarily qualified opportunities.

**Key Finding:** This website is marketing-focused rather than sales-focused. It tells prospects what you do, but doesn't convince them why they should choose you or help them self-qualify before reaching out.

---

## Strengths

### 1. Clear Value Proposition
The hero message "Delivering Solutions is in Our DNA" is memorable and reinforces the company name effectively. The tagline is immediately understandable and sets clear expectations.

### 2. Service Clarity
All four core services (Web Development, Service Management, Project Management, Business Change) are well-defined with clear descriptions. Prospects can quickly understand what you offer.

### 3. Process Transparency
The "How We Work" page demonstrates transparency with a clear four-step engagement process. This reduces uncertainty and helps set prospect expectations - something that can shorten sales cycles.

### 4. Response Time Commitment
The 24-hour response time commitment is excellent and should help with lead nurturing. This is a competitive advantage that addresses a common buyer concern.

### 5. Low-Friction Contact Process
The contact form is simple and not overly demanding. While this is good for conversion, it comes at the cost of qualification (see Weaknesses).

### 6. Trust Indicators Present
Basic trust signals are included: WCAG 2.2 compliance, direct collaboration model, no-bloat philosophy. These differentiate your approach from competitors.

### 7. Pricing Transparency Mentioned
The "How We Work" page mentions fixed-price and day-rate options, which is more transparent than many consultancies. This can help filter out prospects with unrealistic budget expectations.

---

## Weaknesses

### 1. No Lead Qualification Mechanism
**Critical Issue:** The contact form captures name, email, company (optional), and message. This provides zero qualifying information. I have no idea if this lead has:
- Budget authority
- Timeline urgency
- Project scope or budget range
- Decision-making authority
- Current pain severity
- Company size or industry

Result: My sales team wastes time on unqualified leads, and our lead-to-customer conversion rates suffer.

### 2. Missing Proof Points and Case Studies
**Critical Issue:** There are no case studies, client testimonials, success metrics, or ROI data anywhere on the site.

In consultative B2B sales, prospects need social proof before they'll engage. Without case studies showing:
- Similar clients you've helped
- Problems you've solved
- Measurable outcomes achieved
- ROI delivered

...prospects have no reason to believe your claims. This is probably costing you 40-50% of potential leads who need validation before contacting you.

### 3. No Competitive Differentiation
The site explains what you do, but not why you're different from the dozens of other UK consultancies offering similar services.

Missing elements:
- Unique methodology or framework
- Proprietary tools or approaches
- Specific industry expertise
- Team credentials and certifications
- Years of experience or project count
- Client retention rates

Without clear differentiation, prospects will default to price comparison - the worst possible position for a consultancy.

### 4. Missing Client Logos and Social Proof
No client logos, no industry affiliations, no certifications, no awards.

B2B buyers want to see that you've worked with companies like theirs. Even if you can't name clients, you should show:
- "Trusted by 50+ UK businesses"
- Industry sectors served
- Company size ranges you work with
- Professional certifications held

### 5. No Pricing Guidance
While you mention pricing models in "How We Work," there are no ballpark figures or pricing ranges. This makes it difficult for prospects to self-qualify on budget.

Recommendation: Add something like:
- "Projects typically range from £X,XXX to £XX,XXX"
- "Day rates start from £XXX"
- "Initial consulting engagements typically start at £X,XXX"

This filters out tire-kickers while attracting serious buyers.

### 6. Limited Objection Handling Content
The site doesn't proactively address common objections:
- Why not use an off-the-shelf solution?
- Why choose a small consultancy over a large firm?
- What if the project doesn't deliver expected results?
- How do you handle scope changes?
- What happens if we're not satisfied?

Sales cycles lengthen when prospects have unanswered concerns they're afraid to raise directly.

### 7. No Sales Support Resources
There's nothing for my sales team to use during the sales process:
- No downloadable service overviews or one-pagers
- No ROI calculator or business case templates
- No comparison guides
- No email nurture sequences for prospects not ready to buy
- No webinars or educational content
- No presentation decks

Modern sales teams need content to share at each stage of the buyer's journey.

### 8. Missing Contact Options
Only one contact method: a form. Missing:
- Phone number (even if just for callbacks)
- Live chat for immediate questions
- Meeting scheduler (Calendly/similar) for prospects ready to book
- LinkedIn profile links
- Office address (builds trust even if remote-first)

B2B decision-makers often prefer to call rather than fill forms. You're losing these leads.

### 9. No Lead Magnets or Content Offers
There's no mechanism to capture leads who aren't ready to engage yet:
- No whitepapers or guides to download
- No email newsletter signup
- No webinars or events
- No free assessments or audits

You're only capturing bottom-of-funnel leads (maybe 3% of visitors). The other 97% leave and likely forget about you.

### 10. No Industry Segmentation
The site treats all prospects the same. But a healthcare organization has different needs than a retail business.

Missing:
- Industry-specific landing pages
- Vertical-specific case studies
- Sector-specific pain points addressed

This generic approach reduces relevance and conversion rates.

### 11. Weak CTAs and Next Steps
While CTAs exist, they're all the same: "Contact Us" or "Get Started."

Better approach:
- "Schedule a Free Consultation"
- "Get Your Free Process Assessment"
- "Download Our Service Comparison Guide"
- "See Our Pricing Options"
- "View Case Studies"

Different prospects are at different stages. Offer multiple next steps.

### 12. No CRM Integration or Lead Routing
The contact form appears to send an email. There's no mention of:
- CRM integration for automatic lead capture
- Lead scoring based on form responses
- Automatic routing to the right person
- Lead notification systems

This means manual handling, slower response times, and leads falling through cracks.

## Specific Issues

### Contact Form (Get Started page)
**Issues:**
1. Doesn't ask about budget range
2. Doesn't ask about timeline/urgency
3. Doesn't ask about specific service interest
4. Doesn't ask about company size
5. Doesn't ask about decision-making authority
6. Company field is optional - should be required for B2B

**Impact:** Every inquiry requires discovery calls just to determine if it's qualified. Wastes sales time.

**Recommendation:** Add qualifying questions:
- Which service are you interested in? [dropdown]
- What's your timeline? [ASAP / 1-3 months / 3-6 months / Just exploring]
- What's your budget range? [Under £10k / £10k-£50k / £50k+ / Not sure yet]
- Company size? [1-10 / 11-50 / 51-200 / 200+]
- Your role? [Owner/Director / Manager / Other]

### Services Pages
**Issues:**
1. Features are listed, but benefits aren't clearly tied to business outcomes
2. No pricing indicators
3. No typical engagement length mentioned
4. No "Who this is for" section
5. No FAQs addressing common concerns

**Impact:** Prospects can't self-assess fit. More time spent on discovery.

**Recommendation:** Add outcomes-focused content:
- "Typical ROI: X% efficiency gain within 6 months"
- "Average engagement: 3-6 months"
- "Best suited for: organizations with 50+ employees"
- "Common results: reduced costs by X%, improved speed by Y%"

### About Page
**Issues:**
1. No team information or credentials
2. No certifications or professional affiliations
3. No company history or founding story
4. No client count or years in business
5. Generic statements without proof points

**Impact:** Doesn't build credibility or trust. Reads like every other consultancy.

**Recommendation:** Add credibility markers:
- Team bios with credentials and certifications
- "Founded in 20XX, completed 100+ projects"
- Professional memberships (BCS, APM, etc.)
- Client count and industries served
- Key achievements and milestones

### Homepage
**Issues:**
1. No social proof above the fold
2. No specific outcomes or metrics
3. No urgency or reason to act now
4. CTAs all lead to the same form
5. No secondary conversion opportunities for researchers

**Impact:** Fails to convert visitors in research mode. Only catches prospects already decided.

**Recommendation:**
- Add client testimonials or logos near hero section
- Include metrics: "Delivered 100+ projects, 95% client satisfaction"
- Add multiple CTAs for different stages: "Download Guide" vs "Book Consultation"
- Include trust badges/certifications in hero area

## Final Thoughts

Look, I've been in sales long enough to know the difference between a marketing website and a sales tool. This is a marketing website. It looks good, it explains what you do, but it doesn't help me close deals.

If I were running sales for Solutions Delivered, I'd be frustrated. Every lead that comes through requires extensive discovery just to figure out if they're worth pursuing. I have no case studies to send prospects during nurture sequences. I can't point them to pricing information when they ask "What's this going to cost?" I have no social proof to overcome skepticism.

The good news? The foundation is solid. You're not starting from zero. But you need to shift thinking from "Here's what we do" to "Here's why you should choose us and here's proof it works."

Priority one: Add qualifying questions to the form and create 3-5 case studies. Those two changes alone would bump this from a 5/10 to a 7/10.

This isn't just about the website - it's about enabling the entire sales process. Right now, the website creates work for sales. It should be reducing work and pre-selling prospects so they arrive already 60-70% convinced.

Marketing brings me leads. I need qualified opportunities. This website needs to help me differentiate wheat from chaff before I pick up the phone.
