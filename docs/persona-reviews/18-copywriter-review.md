# Copywriting Review: Solutions Delivered Website

**Reviewer:** Tom Hughes, Senior Copywriter
**Date:** 2025-11-20
**Review Type:** Comprehensive Copywriting Audit

---

## 1. Executive Summary

I've completed a thorough copywriting review of the Solutions Delivered website, analyzing headlines, body copy, CTAs, tone, scannability, and persuasive elements across all main pages.

**Overall Assessment:** The website has a solid foundation with clean, professional copy that's generally well-structured and accessible. However, there's significant room for improvement in making the copy more benefit-focused, compelling, and conversion-oriented. The site currently leans too heavily on describing what the company does rather than what the client gets. The copy feels a bit safe and corporate when it could be more engaging and persuasive.

**Quick Verdict:** 6.5/10 - Good bones, needs more muscle.

---

## 2. Strengths

### 2.1 Structure and Scannability
The website does many things right from a readability perspective:
- **Short paragraphs** throughout make the content easy to digest
- **Good use of white space** - the copy has room to breathe
- **Bullet points and lists** used effectively to break up information
- **Clear visual hierarchy** with consistent heading structure
- **Logical content flow** that follows a natural progression

### 2.2 Tone and Voice
- **Consistent tone** across all pages - professional but approachable
- **Active voice** used in most places, keeping copy direct
- **Jargon-free** for the most part (with a few exceptions noted below)
- **No fluff** - copy is concise and gets to the point

### 2.3 Trust Building
- **Trust indicators** effectively placed ("WCAG 2.2 Compliant", "Direct Team Collaboration", "No-Bloat Philosophy")
- **Transparency messaging** about response times and process
- **"No commitment" language** reduces friction effectively

### 2.4 Technical Execution
- **Mobile-friendly** copy lengths that work across devices
- **Accessibility considerations** evident throughout
- **Good heading structure** for SEO and navigation

## 3. Weaknesses

### 3.1 Headlines Lack Punch
The headlines are my biggest concern. They're functional but not compelling.

**Problem:** Headlines state facts rather than benefits or create curiosity.

**Examples:**
- "Our Core Services" - tells me nothing about value
- "What Sets Us Apart" - generic category label
- "Our Approach" - descriptive but dull
- "What We Look For" - inward-facing, not benefit-driven

**What's Missing:** Headlines should grab attention, create curiosity, or promise a benefit. Right now, they're just signposts.

### 3.2 Feature-Focused, Not Benefit-Focused
Throughout the site, copy talks about what you do rather than what I get.

**Problem Examples:**
- "We build bespoke web systems using Laravel" - features
- "Our Service Management solutions focus on optimizing your internal processes" - what you do
- "We work directly with your teams" - your process, not my outcome

**Better approach:** Lead with the benefit, support with features. "Save 40% on maintenance costs with lean, custom-built systems" tells me why I should care.

### 3.3 Generic, Safe Copy
The copy plays it safe and feels like it could apply to any consultancy.

**Generic phrases I spotted:**
- "Tailored solutions" (used repeatedly - becoming meaningless)
- "Drive real results"
- "Sustainable growth"
- "Operational excellence"
- "Transform your business"

These are consultancy buzzwords that don't differentiate you. Every competitor says the same thing.

### 3.4 Weak or Generic CTAs
The CTAs are clear but not compelling.

**Current CTAs:**
- "Get Started" (appears 15+ times)
- "Contact Us"
- "Learn More"
- "View Our Solutions"

**What's wrong:** They're generic and don't create urgency or excitement. "Get Started" doing what? Why now?

### 3.5 Missing Emotional Connection
The copy is rational and logical but lacks emotional triggers.

**What's missing:**
- Pain point articulation (you mention challenges but don't dig into the frustration)
- Aspiration (what does success feel like?)
- Story (no client stories, case studies, or concrete examples)
- Urgency (no reason to act now vs. next month)

### 3.6 "We" vs. "You" Balance Is Off
I did a quick count, and the copy uses "we" far more than "you". This is a classic B2B mistake.

**Examples where it's too "we" focused:**
- "We believe in building genuine partnerships" - your belief, not my benefit
- "We provide tailored business solutions" - what you do
- "We focus on three core areas" - your focus

**Better:** Start with "you" - address the reader's situation first, then explain how you help.

### 3.7 Value Proposition Isn't Immediately Clear
When I land on the homepage, I need to know within 3 seconds:
1. What do you do?
2. Who is it for?
3. Why should I care?

**Current hero copy:**
> "Delivering Solutions is in Our DNA"
> "Tailored IT solutions for service management, web development, and business transformation"

**Problems:**
- "Delivering Solutions is in Our DNA" - clever wordplay with company name but tells me nothing
- The subheading lists what you do but not who it's for or what outcome I get
- No clear differentiator

### 3.8 Missing Social Proof
I see zero testimonials, case studies, client logos, or specific results throughout the site.

**Why this matters:** B2B buyers need reassurance. They want to know "Has this worked for companies like mine?" Without social proof, you're asking me to take a leap of faith.

### 3.9 Some Technical Jargon Remains
While mostly jargon-free, some terms need translation:
- "WCAG 2.2 compliant" (most business owners don't know what this means)
- "ITIL-aligned service management frameworks"
- "Laravel-based systems" (why should I care about the tech stack?)
- "No-bloat philosophy" (what does this actually mean in practice?)

**Rule:** If you use a technical term, immediately follow with what it means for the reader.

## 5. Recommendations

### 5.1 Immediate Wins (High Impact, Low Effort)

#### A. Rewrite Key Headlines to Focus on Benefits
Transform section headers from labels to value propositions:

**Before:** "Our Core Services"
**After:** "Solutions That Solve Your Most Critical Business Challenges"

**Before:** "What Sets Us Apart"
**After:** "Why Companies Choose Direct Collaboration Over Corporate Agencies"

**Before:** "Our Approach"
**After:** "How We Deliver Results Without the Red Tape"

#### B. Fix the Hero Headline
The homepage hero is the most important copy on the site.

**Current:** "Delivering Solutions is in Our DNA"

**Better options:**
- "Stop Paying for Features You'll Never Use" (if no-bloat is your differentiator)
- "UK IT Consultancy That Delivers Results, Not Promises"
- "Accessible Web Systems and Business Solutions That Actually Work"

Follow with a subheading that clarifies who it's for.

#### C. Strengthen CTAs
Make every CTA specific and benefit-oriented:

**Before:** "Get Started"
**After:** "Book Your Free Consultation" or "See How We Can Help Your Business"

**Before:** "Learn More"
**After:** "See Our Approach in Action" or "Discover Your Solution"

**Before:** "Contact Us Today"
**After:** "Start Your Transformation" or "Get Your Custom Solution"

#### D. Add Outcome-Focused Bullet Points
Wherever you list what you do, add what the client gets:

**Before:** "WCAG 2.2 compliant"
**After:** "WCAG 2.2 compliant - reach every customer, avoid legal risks"

**Before:** "Direct team collaboration"
**After:** "Direct team collaboration - no account managers, faster decisions"

**Before:** "No-bloat philosophy"
**After:** "No-bloat philosophy - pay only for what you need, save 30-40% on typical project costs"

#### E. Replace Generic Language
Do a find-and-replace for overused terms:
- "Tailored solutions" → Be specific about what you customize
- "Drive results" → What kind of results?
- "Transform your business" → What changes specifically?
- "Sustainable growth" → What does growth look like?

### 5.2 Medium-Term Improvements (High Impact, More Effort)

#### A. Add Social Proof Throughout
Every major section should have proof:
- Client testimonials (even if anonymous: "IT Director, Healthcare")
- Specific results ("Reduced processing time by 60%")
- Client logos (if you can get permission)
- Project examples or mini case studies
- Certifications or credentials

#### B. Address Pain Points Explicitly
Add sections that dig into the problems you solve:

**Example for Web Development:**
> "Frustrated by bloated, overpriced systems that do everything except what you actually need? Us too. That's why we..."

Start with the pain, then position yourself as the aspirin.

#### C. Create a Clear Value Proposition
Develop a one-sentence value prop that's unique and specific:

**Framework:** "We help [specific audience] achieve [specific outcome] without [common pain point]."

**Example:** "We help UK businesses get accessible, custom web systems without the enterprise price tag or unnecessary features."

#### D. Flip the "We" Focus to "You"
Rewrite sections to start with the reader:

**Before:** "We work directly with your teams, fostering knowledge transfer..."
**After:** "You'll work directly with our team—no account managers to slow you down—while building internal knowledge that lasts..."

#### E. Add Personality and Voice
The copy is professional but a bit sterile. Consider:
- Adding more conversational moments
- Using contractions (you're, we're, don't)
- Addressing objections directly ("Worried about hidden costs? We get it.")
- Showing some personality in subheadings and asides

### 5.3 Long-Term Strategy (Highest Impact, Most Effort)

#### A. Develop Case Studies
Create 3-5 detailed case studies showing:
- The problem
- Your solution
- Specific, measurable results
- Client testimonial

These become your best sales tools.

#### B. Create Targeted Landing Pages
Instead of one "Solutions" page, create separate pages for:
- Each service type
- Each industry you serve
- Each buyer persona

This allows for hyper-relevant copy that converts better.

#### C. Add Video and Multimedia
Copy doesn't exist in a vacuum. Consider:
- Video testimonials
- Explainer videos for complex services
- Process animations
- Team introductions

#### D. Implement Conversion-Focused Copy Testing
Set up A/B tests for:
- Headlines
- CTA buttons
- Value propositions
- Form field copy

Use data to continuously improve.

#### E. Develop a Content Strategy
Create content that demonstrates expertise:
- Case study blog posts
- "How we solved X" articles
- Client success stories
- Behind-the-scenes process content

This builds trust and gives you proof points to reference in your main copy.

### Summary:
The Solutions Delivered website has professional, well-structured copy that won't turn anyone away. However, it's not doing the hard work of conversion-focused copywriting. It's playing it safe with generic consultant language when it should be bold, specific, and benefit-driven.

The good news? The foundation is solid. With some strategic rewrites focusing on benefits, stronger headlines, compelling CTAs, and social proof, this could easily jump to an 8.5-9/10.

**My advice:** Start with the homepage hero, rewrite your headlines to focus on benefits, and add some social proof. Those three changes alone would dramatically improve conversion potential.

## 8. Priority Action Items

If you can only do three things, do these:

1. **Rewrite the homepage hero headline and subheading** to clearly state who you serve, what problem you solve, and what makes you different. This is your first impression—make it count.

2. **Add social proof** somewhere on every major page. Even anonymous testimonials ("Working with Solutions Delivered cut our development time in half" - IT Director, Healthcare) are better than nothing.

3. **Transform your headlines** from labels to value propositions. "Our Core Services" becomes "Solutions That Solve Your Most Critical Business Challenges." This change alone will increase time on page and engagement.

These three changes require minimal development effort but will significantly impact how compelling your copy feels.

---

## Final Thoughts

Listen, you've got a clean, professional site with solid bones. The copy isn't bad—it's just not working hard enough for you. You're in a competitive space, and generic "tailored solutions that drive results" copy isn't going to cut through the noise.

The biggest opportunity I see is shifting from talking about what you do to showing what clients get. Less "we build bespoke systems," more "you'll save 40% on maintenance costs." Less "we provide project management," more "your projects will finish on time, on budget."

You've clearly got expertise and a solid offering. Now make sure your copy demonstrates that at every touchpoint. Show, don't tell. Be specific, not generic. Lead with benefits, not features.

Do that, and you'll turn tire-kickers into clients.
