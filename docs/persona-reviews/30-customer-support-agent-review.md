# Customer Support Review: Solutions Delivered Website
**Reviewer:** Emily Carter, Customer Support Specialist
**Review Date:** November 20, 2025
**Overall Rating:** 4/10

---

## Executive Summary

As a Customer Support Specialist with 3 years of experience handling customer inquiries and issues, I've reviewed the Solutions Delivered website from a support perspective. My focus was on evaluating the availability of help resources, self-service options, knowledge base content, and support access points that would enable both customers and support teams to resolve issues efficiently.

The website presents a professional consulting firm with clear service descriptions and accessible design. However, it fundamentally lacks the support infrastructure and self-service resources that modern customers expect. There's no knowledge base, no FAQs section, no troubleshooting guides, and limited contact options beyond a single form.

**Key Finding:** This website forces every question or concern to go through the contact form, creating unnecessary friction for customers who want quick answers and placing all burden on the support team to handle even basic inquiries that could be self-served.

As someone who answers customer questions daily, I can tell you: "If I can't find the answer in our knowledge base in 30 seconds, the customer certainly can't." This site doesn't give customers - or support agents - that option.

---

## Strengths

### 1. Clear Service Documentation
The Solutions and How We Work pages provide comprehensive descriptions of what the company does. This helps set clear expectations and reduces confusion about services offered.

### 2. 24-Hour Response Time Commitment
The website prominently displays a 24-hour response time commitment, which is excellent for setting customer expectations and managing satisfaction.

### 3. Transparent Process
The "How We Work" page outlines a clear four-step engagement process. This transparency reduces "What happens next?" questions that support teams commonly receive.

### 4. Accessible Design
The website follows WCAG 2.2 standards, making it usable for customers with disabilities. This reduces accessibility-related support requests.

### 5. Simple Contact Form
The contact form is straightforward and not overly complex, which minimizes friction for customers reaching out. However, this simplicity comes at the cost of qualification (see Weaknesses).

### 6. Clear CTAs
"Get Started" and "Contact Us" buttons are consistently placed throughout the site, making it easy for customers to know how to reach out.

### 7. Mobile-Friendly Navigation
The responsive design ensures customers can access information from any device, which is crucial since many support inquiries come from mobile users.

---

## Weaknesses

### 1. No Knowledge Base or Help Center
**Critical Issue:** There is no searchable knowledge base, help center, or documentation repository.

This means:
- Customers cannot find answers to common questions independently
- Support agents have nowhere to direct customers for self-service
- Every question requires direct support interaction
- No way to reduce support ticket volume through self-service
- Support team wastes time answering repetitive basic questions

A knowledge base should cover:
- Common questions about each service
- Pricing and payment information
- Engagement process details
- Technical requirements
- Industry-specific guidance
- Case studies and examples

**Impact on Support:** I estimate this lack of self-service resources increases support volume by 40-50% unnecessarily.

### 2. Missing FAQ Section
**Critical Issue:** No Frequently Asked Questions section exists anywhere on the site.

Common questions I would expect customers to ask:
- How much do your services cost?
- How long does a typical project take?
- Do you work with companies in [specific industry]?
- What geographic areas do you serve?
- Can we start with a small project?
- What happens if we're not satisfied?
- Do you offer ongoing support after project completion?
- What information do you need to provide a quote?
- How does billing work?
- What's your refund/cancellation policy?

Without FAQs, every prospect asks these same questions individually through the contact form.

**Impact on Support:** 30-40% of support inquiries are likely FAQs that could be answered instantly on the site.

### 3. No Multiple Contact Options
Only one contact method exists: a web form.

Missing contact options:
- No phone number displayed
- No email address visible
- No live chat for immediate questions
- No support ticket system
- No meeting scheduler link
- No contact hours listed
- No office address shown

**Impact:** Customers who prefer phone or email contact are forced into a channel they may not want to use, creating friction and potentially losing leads.

### 4. No Self-Service Support Resources
There are no resources for customers to help themselves:
- No downloadable guides or PDFs
- No video tutorials or demos
- No case studies with detailed solutions
- No templates or worksheets
- No calculators or tools
- No troubleshooting guides
- No glossary of terms

**Impact:** Every customer needs hand-holding through basic information gathering.

### 5. No Support Documentation for Common Issues
As a support agent, I need internal resources to reference. This site provides no:
- Service comparison guides
- Detailed feature lists
- Technical specifications
- Integration information
- Best practices documentation
- Common objections and responses

**Impact:** Support team has no centralized resource to reference when answering customer questions, leading to inconsistent responses.

### 6. Missing Service-Level Information
No clear support commitments are documented:
- No support hours specified (business hours? weekends?)
- No escalation procedures
- No SLA details beyond "24-hour response"
- No emergency contact procedures
- No after-hours support information

**Impact:** Customer expectations aren't managed, leading to frustration when responses come during off-hours.

### 7. No Customer Portal or Account Access
No self-service portal where customers can:
- Check project status
- View proposals or contracts
- Access project documentation
- Submit change requests
- View billing history
- Update contact information

**Impact:** All status inquiries must go through support, increasing support load unnecessarily.

### 8. No Searchability
The website has no search function, making it difficult for customers to find specific information quickly.

**Impact:** Customers can't efficiently locate information, leading to more support contacts.

### 9. No Proactive Communication Tools
Missing features that reduce support burden:
- No status page for known issues
- No updates or announcements section
- No newsletter for keeping customers informed
- No blog with helpful tips and insights
- No resource library

**Impact:** Support team must reactively answer questions instead of proactively providing information.

### 10. Insufficient Pricing Transparency
While pricing models are mentioned, there are no:
- Ballpark price ranges
- Sample project costs
- Pricing calculators
- Clear payment terms
- Invoice examples

**Impact:** "How much will this cost?" is likely the #1 support question, and it requires full discovery to answer.

### 11. No Chatbot or Automated Responses
No automation to handle common questions:
- No chatbot for basic inquiries
- No automated email responses with helpful links
- No knowledge base suggestions
- No smart routing based on inquiry type

**Impact:** Support team must manually respond to every inquiry, even basic ones that could be automated.

### 12. No Feedback Mechanism
No way for customers to:
- Rate support interactions
- Suggest documentation improvements
- Report missing information
- Contribute to knowledge base
- Request new resources

**Impact:** Support team has no data-driven way to improve documentation based on actual customer needs.

### 13. Limited Contact Form Functionality
The contact form doesn't:
- Suggest relevant help articles before submission
- Route to appropriate team member
- Set expectations about response time
- Provide confirmation with tracking number
- Offer alternative resources while waiting

**Impact:** Lost opportunity to deflect tickets or set proper expectations.

## Specific Issues

### Homepage
**Issues:**
1. No prominent "Help" or "Support" link in navigation
2. No FAQ section linked
3. No trust indicators about support quality
4. No customer testimonials about support experience

**Recommendation:** Add "Help Center" to main navigation and include support-focused trust signals.

### Get Started (Contact) Page
**Issues:**
1. Form doesn't suggest help resources before submission
2. No indication of what happens after form submission
3. No tracking number or confirmation code provided
4. No option to search knowledge base first
5. Company field is optional, but important for B2B support
6. No field to indicate urgency level
7. No dropdown to categorize inquiry type

**Recommendation:** Add knowledge base search above form, include inquiry type selector, and provide clear next steps.

### Solutions Page
**Issues:**
1. No FAQs specific to each service
2. No "common questions" sections
3. No downloadable service overviews
4. No video explanations
5. No detailed technical requirements
6. No example scenarios or case studies

**Recommendation:** Add collapsible FAQ sections under each service with answers to common questions.

### How We Work Page
**Issues:**
1. Good process documentation, but no "What if..." scenarios
2. No information about handling changes or issues
3. No escalation procedures if problems arise
4. No client responsibilities outlined
5. No typical timeline examples

**Recommendation:** Add FAQ section addressing edge cases and potential issues.

### About Page
**Issues:**
1. No team contact information
2. No support team introduction
3. No support philosophy or commitments
4. No customer success stories
5. No trust signals about support quality

**Recommendation:** Add section about customer support commitment and team expertise.

### Careers Page
**Issues:**
1. No mention of support roles
2. No information about support team culture
3. No training and development for support staff

**Recommendation:** If hiring support staff, outline support career opportunities.

## The Support Agent's Perspective

Let me be honest about what it's like supporting customers with this website:

### Daily Frustrations

**Repetitive Questions:** Without FAQs, I answer the same 10-15 questions every single day. "How much does it cost?" "How long will it take?" "Do you work with [industry]?" "What do I need to get started?"

It's exhausting. I feel like a broken record.

**No Resources to Share:** When a customer asks a complex question, I have nothing to send them. No guides, no articles, no videos. I have to type out a lengthy email explaining everything from scratch every single time.

Then tomorrow, someone asks the exact same question, and I do it all over again.

**Inconsistent Responses:** Without centralized documentation, different support agents probably give slightly different answers to the same questions. This leads to customer confusion and trust issues.

**Time Wasted:** I estimate I spend 4-5 hours per day answering questions that could be handled by a good FAQ page or knowledge base. That's half my workday wasted on preventable inquiries.

**Customer Frustration:** Customers don't want to wait 24 hours to learn if you serve their industry or what your typical pricing looks like. They want instant answers. When they can't get them, they go to a competitor who provides self-service resources.

### What I Need

As a support agent, here's what would make my job easier and customers happier:

1. **Comprehensive FAQ:** So I can link to it instead of typing the same answer for the 100th time
2. **Knowledge Base:** With searchable articles I can reference and update
3. **Internal Documentation:** With talking points, objection handlers, and detailed service specs
4. **Email Templates:** For common responses that I can personalize
5. **Video Resources:** To send to visual learners
6. **Tracking System:** To see common issues and prioritize documentation
7. **Self-Service Portal:** So customers can check status without asking me
8. **Chatbot:** To handle simple questions while I focus on complex issues

### The Best Support Interaction

Here's a quote from my persona profile that sums it up:

> "The best support interaction is the one that doesn't need to happen because the customer found their answer."

This website creates work for me. It should be eliminating work by empowering customers to help themselves.

## Final Thoughts

Look, I understand this is a consultancy website, not a SaaS product with ongoing support needs. But even consulting businesses need support resources.

Your potential customers have questions. Currently, every single one of those questions becomes a support ticket. That's not scalable, not efficient, and not what customers expect in 2025.

I've worked at companies with amazing self-service resources, and I've worked at companies like this one. The difference is night and day. At companies with good knowledge bases:
- Support volume is 40-50% lower
- Customer satisfaction is higher because they get instant answers
- Support agents have more time for complex, high-value interactions
- Documentation improves over time based on actual customer questions
- The business scales without proportionally scaling support staff

At companies without self-service resources:
- Support is constantly overwhelmed
- Customers are frustrated by unnecessary wait times
- Agents burn out answering the same questions repeatedly
- Response quality suffers from volume pressure
- Business growth requires hiring more support staff

**Priority One:** Create an FAQ section. Start with 15-20 questions covering pricing, timelines, process, and services. This single change would probably reduce support inquiries by 30% overnight.

**Priority Two:** Build a basic knowledge base with articles for each service, getting started guides, and process documentation.

**Priority Three:** Add multiple contact options and improve the contact form with better routing and expectation setting.

These aren't huge lifts, but they'll make a massive difference for both customers and the support team. Trust me - as someone who lives and breathes customer support, I know what works and what doesn't.

This website doesn't currently work from a support perspective. But with relatively modest improvements, it could be a support powerhouse that empowers customers and frees up support resources for high-value interactions.
