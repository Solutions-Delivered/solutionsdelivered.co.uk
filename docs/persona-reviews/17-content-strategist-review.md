# Content Strategy Review: Solutions Delivered Website

**Reviewer:** Rachel Green, Head of Content Strategy
**Date:** November 20, 2025
**Review Type:** Comprehensive Content Strategy Assessment

---

## Executive Summary

I've conducted a thorough content strategy review of the Solutions Delivered website, examining content architecture, quality, governance, and alignment with user intent. The website demonstrates solid foundational content with clear messaging and good structural organization. However, it represents a "brochure" approach to content rather than a strategic content ecosystem designed to attract, engage, and nurture prospects through their journey.

The site successfully communicates what the company does and how to engage them, but it misses significant opportunities to demonstrate expertise, build trust over time, and address the full spectrum of user needs throughout the buyer's journey. There's no evidence of ongoing content production, thought leadership, or educational resources that would establish Solutions Delivered as an authority in their field.

**Overall Rating: 6.5/10**

---

## 1. Strengths

### 1.1 Clear Content Architecture
The site has a logical, well-organized structure that makes sense to users. The navigation is clean with six primary pages (Home, About, Solutions, How We Work, Careers, Get Started) plus supporting legal pages. This flat architecture works well for a small consultancy and users can find information quickly.

The information hierarchy is sensible:
- Home → Overview and value proposition
- Solutions → Service details
- How We Work → Process and approach
- About → Company information
- Careers → Employment opportunities
- Get Started → Contact and conversion

### 1.2 Consistent Tone and Voice
The content maintains a professional yet approachable tone throughout. The writing is clear, jargon-free (mostly), and speaks directly to business decision-makers. There's good consistency in:
- Voice (professional, collaborative, transparent)
- Style (conversational but authoritative)
- Messaging (tailored solutions, direct collaboration, no-bloat philosophy)

Key brand messages are reinforced consistently:
- UK-based consultancy
- Direct team collaboration
- No-bloat philosophy
- WCAG 2.2 compliance
- Tailored solutions

### 1.3 User-Focused Messaging
The content addresses user needs rather than just talking about the company. Phrases like "Your trusted partner" and "Let's discuss how we can help" demonstrate user-centric thinking. The Solutions page does a good job of explaining "What You Get" and "Key Benefits" rather than just listing features.

The "How We Work" page is particularly strong in setting expectations and building trust by explaining what clients can expect in terms of communication, delivery, pricing, and working arrangements.

### 1.4 Clear Calls-to-Action
Every page has clear, prominent CTAs that guide users toward the next step. The "Get Started" button appears consistently in the navigation and throughout content. CTAs are contextual and varied:
- "Get Started Today"
- "View Our Solutions"
- "Contact Us Today"
- "Get in Touch"

The contact form itself is well-designed with appropriate fields, helpful microcopy, and clear error handling.

### 1.5 SEO Fundamentals in Place
The content includes basic SEO elements:
- Descriptive page titles
- Meta descriptions for all pages
- Schema markup on Solutions and Get Started pages
- Clean URL structure (trailing slashes, descriptive paths)
- XML sitemap with appropriate priorities
- Semantic HTML structure

### 1.6 Accessibility-Minded Content
The content structure supports WCAG compliance with proper heading hierarchies, descriptive link text, and form labels. There's no "click here" or ambiguous links—everything is descriptive. The site practices what it preaches about accessibility.

## 2. Weaknesses

### 2.1 No Content Marketing Strategy
This is the most significant gap. There's no evidence of ongoing content production or thought leadership:

**Missing:**
- Blog or insights section
- Case studies or client success stories
- Whitepapers or guides
- Industry commentary or analysis
- Educational resources
- Webinars or video content
- Downloadable templates or tools

The site is entirely transactional—it exists to convert immediate inquiries but does nothing to build audience, demonstrate expertise, or nurture prospects who aren't ready to buy now. This is a massive missed opportunity for a consultancy where trust and expertise are critical.

### 2.2 Insufficient Social Proof
There are no testimonials, client logos, case studies, or results-driven content. For a consultancy, this is a critical weakness. Prospects need to see evidence that you've solved problems for companies like theirs.

**What's Missing:**
- Client testimonials (with attribution)
- Case studies showing problem → solution → results
- Client logos or "trusted by" section
- Project examples or portfolio
- Results and metrics from past engagements
- Industry recognition or awards

The "trust indicators" scattered throughout (e.g., "WCAG 2.2 Compliant", "Direct Team Collaboration") are helpful but not sufficient. These are promises about how you work, not proof that you deliver results.

### 2.3 Limited Content for Buyer's Journey Stages
The content primarily serves users at the decision stage (ready to contact) but provides little for earlier stages:

**Awareness Stage (Severely Lacking):**
- No content that helps users recognize they have a problem
- No educational content about business challenges
- No thought leadership establishing expertise
- Nothing to attract users who don't yet know they need your services

**Consideration Stage (Partially Addressed):**
- Solutions page explains what you offer (good)
- How We Work page explains your approach (good)
- But no comparison content, decision frameworks, or deep dives into methodologies

**Decision Stage (Well Covered):**
- Clear CTAs and contact forms (excellent)
- Transparent about process and expectations (good)

The site assumes prospects already know they need your specific services and are ready to reach out. That's a tiny segment of your potential market.

### 2.4 Shallow Content Depth
While the existing content is well-written, it's fairly superficial. For example:

**Service Management Page:**
- Describes what you do in 2-3 paragraphs
- Lists 4 generic benefits
- Doesn't explain methodologies, frameworks, or approaches
- No examples of challenges you solve
- No indication of what makes your approach unique

Compare this to what prospects actually want to know:
- What specific problems do you solve?
- How do you approach process optimization differently?
- What frameworks or methodologies do you use?
- What results have you achieved for similar companies?
- How long does a typical engagement last?
- What's the ROI or expected outcomes?

This pattern repeats across all service pages. The content answers "what" but barely touches "why" or "how."

### 2.5 No Content Governance Structure
There's no evidence of content governance:
- No content ownership indicated
- No review or update schedules
- No content lifecycle management
- No editorial calendar
- No content quality standards documented
- No version control or change tracking

The legal pages say "Last Updated: [current date]" which appears to be dynamic—this means there's no actual review process, just an auto-generated date. That's not content governance.

### 2.6 Missing Content for User Intent
Several obvious user intents aren't addressed:

**"How much does it cost?"**
- Pricing is mentioned vaguely ("competitive rates", "transparent pricing")
- No pricing frameworks, day rates, or even ranges
- No explanation of what factors affect cost
- This creates friction and may lose prospects who won't engage without ballpark figures

**"What's the process really like?"**
- The 4-step process is too high-level
- No typical timelines for engagements
- No explanation of what you need from clients
- No preparation checklists or pre-engagement materials

**"Who have you worked with?"**
- No client examples
- No industry specializations mentioned
- No company size or type indicators
- Unclear if you serve SMBs, enterprises, or both

**"What makes you different?"**
- Generic differentiators that any consultancy could claim
- No unique methodologies or proprietary approaches mentioned
- "No-bloat philosophy" is good but underdeveloped

### 2.7 No FAQ or Support Content
There's no FAQ section to address common questions. This means:
- Sales team has to answer the same questions repeatedly
- Prospects might leave rather than reach out with "basic" questions
- No opportunity to address objections proactively
- Missing easy SEO wins for question-based searches

Common questions that should be addressed:
- Do you work with companies outside the UK?
- What size companies do you typically work with?
- How long is a typical engagement?
- Do you offer ongoing/retainer services?
- What industries do you specialize in?
- What if we're not sure which service we need?

### 2.8 Content Gaps for Specific Audiences
The content doesn't segment or personalize for different audiences:

**Technical Buyers:**
- Limited technical depth about web development approaches
- No discussion of technology stack, hosting, maintenance
- No information about ongoing support or SLAs

**C-Suite/Budget Holders:**
- No ROI frameworks or business case content
- Limited discussion of strategic outcomes vs. tactical deliverables
- No content about minimizing risk or ensuring successful adoption

**HR/Recruiting Audience:**
- Careers page is generic and doesn't sell the company culture
- No content about what makes Solutions Delivered a great place to work
- No employee stories or day-in-the-life content
- Missing information about benefits, work arrangements, career growth

### 2.9 Limited Semantic Depth
While the content uses relevant keywords naturally, it lacks semantic depth:
- No comprehensive coverage of related topics
- No entity relationships established through content
- Limited opportunities for featured snippets
- No pillar page/topic cluster strategy

For example, the Service Management page could have supporting content about:
- ITIL frameworks
- Process optimization methodologies
- Change management best practices
- KPIs for service management success
- Common service management challenges by industry

### 2.10 No Content Measurement Strategy
There's no indication that content performance is tracked or optimized:
- No evidence of A/B testing headlines or CTAs
- No content analytics discussed
- No conversion tracking mentioned
- No user behavior analysis informing content decisions

## 3. Specific Issues

### 3.1 Homepage
**Issues:**
- Services overview is good but could link to specific section anchors on Solutions page
- "Trust indicators" are vague (what does "No-Bloat Philosophy" mean to a first-time visitor?)
- No social proof above the fold
- Missing a clear value proposition that differentiates from competitors
- The hero tagline "Delivering Solutions is in Our DNA" is generic

**Opportunities:**
- Add client logos or testimonial quotes
- Make trust indicators more specific and meaningful
- Include a statistic or result (e.g., "95% client satisfaction rate")
- Create a more compelling, specific value proposition

### 3.2 About Page
**Issues:**
- Very generic—could apply to any consultancy
- No team information or founder story
- No company history or evolution
- No specifics about expertise or experience
- "Years of real-world experience" is vague—how many years? What experience?

**Missing Content:**
- Team bios (even just leadership)
- Company story and founding principles
- Specific expertise areas or certifications
- Company size and structure
- Client success metrics

### 3.3 Solutions Page
**Issues:**
- Each service gets roughly equal treatment regardless of demand/importance
- No guidance on which service is right for different situations
- No discussion of service combinations or integrated approaches
- Content is benefit-focused but light on methodology
- No indication of typical project timelines or scope

**Opportunities:**
- Add "When you need this service" sections
- Include typical challenges solved
- Provide more methodological depth
- Add case study links (when available)
- Create decision tree or service selector

### 3.4 How We Work Page
**Strengths:** This is actually one of the better pages—good content here.

**Minor Issues:**
- "When We're a Good Fit" section is helpful but could be stronger with negative indicators too (when you're NOT a good fit)
- Could benefit from client testimonials about the experience
- Process steps could have more detail about client involvement

### 3.5 Careers Page
**Issues:**
- Extremely generic—"challenging work", "work-life balance" could be any company
- No actual job openings listed
- No application process explained
- No company culture content
- No employee testimonials or photos
- No information about compensation, benefits, or career development

**This page reads like filler content rather than a genuine recruitment tool.**

### 3.6 Get Started Page
**Strengths:** Well-executed contact form with good UX.

**Issues:**
- Could pre-qualify leads with service selection or project type
- No estimated response time for different contact types
- Could offer alternative contact methods (phone, email) for those who prefer not to fill forms
- No live chat option for immediate questions

### 3.7 Privacy & Terms Pages
**Strengths:** Comprehensive, legally sound, UK GDPR compliant.

**Issues:**
- Very lengthy—could benefit from a summary/TL;DR at the top
- No version history or effective date tracking
- Could be more user-friendly with collapsible sections

## 4. Recommendations

### 4.1 CRITICAL: Develop Content Marketing Strategy (Priority 1)

**Why:** You can't build authority, attract organic traffic, or nurture prospects without ongoing content production.

**Immediate Actions:**
1. **Start a Blog/Insights Section:**
   - Publish weekly or bi-weekly
   - Mix of educational content and thought leadership
   - Topics: process optimization, project management tips, change management, web development best practices
   - Write to attract awareness-stage prospects

2. **Create 3-5 Foundational Case Studies:**
   - Problem → Solution → Results format
   - Include metrics and outcomes
   - Get client permission (can anonymize if needed)
   - Showcase different services and industries

3. **Develop Downloadable Resources:**
   - "Process Optimization Checklist"
   - "Project Management Readiness Assessment"
   - "Change Management Framework Guide"
   - Gate these with email capture for lead generation

4. **Establish Content Calendar:**
   - Define publishing frequency
   - Assign content ownership
   - Plan content themes by quarter
   - Align with business goals and sales needs

### 4.2 Add Social Proof Throughout (Priority 1)

**Immediate Actions:**
1. **Homepage:**
   - Add client logo bar ("Trusted by" section)
   - Include 2-3 rotating testimonial quotes
   - Add a results statistic (projects delivered, client satisfaction, etc.)

2. **Solutions Page:**
   - Add a testimonial for each service if possible
   - Include brief case study teasers with "Read full case study" links
   - Show typical results or outcomes

3. **About Page:**
   - Add team photos and bios
   - Include credentials, certifications, years of experience
   - Show company milestones or achievements

4. **Create Dedicated Case Studies/Work Page:**
   - Showcase 5-10 projects
   - Organize by service type or industry
   - Include before/after comparisons where possible

### 4.3 Deepen Existing Content (Priority 2)

**For Each Service Page:**
1. Add "When You Need This Service" section
2. Include typical challenges addressed
3. Explain your methodology/approach in detail
4. Add "What Makes Our Approach Different"
5. Include typical engagement timelines
6. Show sample project phases or milestones
7. Link to related resources (blog posts, guides, case studies)

- Overview (current content)

- What Makes Us Different

2. **Create comprehensive FAQ page** organized by category:
   - Services & Capabilities
   - Process & Engagement

   - Company & Team
   - Technical Questions (for web development)

3. **Add contextual mini-FAQs** on key pages:
   - Solutions page: FAQs about each service
   - How We Work page: FAQs about process
   - Careers page: FAQs about working here

### 4.5 Implement Content Governance (Priority 2)

**Immediate Actions:**
1. **Document Content Standards:**
   - Tone of voice guidelines
   - Writing style guide
   - Brand messaging framework
   - SEO standards

2. **Establish Content Ownership:**
   - Assign page owners
   - Define review schedules (e.g., quarterly reviews)
   - Set up approval workflows for new content

3. **Create Content Calendar:**
   - Plan blog topics 2-3 months ahead
   - Schedule content reviews
   - Track performance metrics

4. **Set Up Content Metrics:**
   - Define KPIs for each content type
   - Track engagement, conversions, search performance
   - Review and optimize quarterly

### 4.6 Address Pricing Transparency (Priority 3)

**Why:** "How much does it cost?" is one of the first questions prospects have. Not addressing it creates friction.

**Options:**
1. **Add Pricing Framework Page:**
   - Explain what factors affect cost
   - Provide day rates or project ranges
   - Offer pricing calculators or estimation tools

2. **Add "Investment" Sections to Service Pages:**
   - Typical project ranges
   - Factors that affect cost
   - Payment models (fixed-price vs. day-rate)

3. **Be Transparent About Minimum Engagements:**
   - If you have minimum project sizes, state them

   - Form abandonment analysis
   - Exit page analysis

4. **Content Audits:**
   - Quarterly content performance reviews
   - Identify top and bottom performers
   - Update, consolidate, or remove underperforming content
   - Expand high-performing topics

## 9. Content Strategy Framework Recommendations

### 9.1 Content Mission Statement

**Recommended Mission:**
"We create practical, actionable content that helps UK businesses understand their operational challenges and discover effective solutions for process optimization, project success, and organizational transformation. Through honest, jargon-free guidance and real-world examples, we demonstrate our expertise and build trust with prospects before they ever reach out."
