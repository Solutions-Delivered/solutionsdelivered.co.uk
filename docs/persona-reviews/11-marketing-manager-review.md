# Marketing Manager Review - Solutions Delivered Website

**Reviewer:** Emma Richardson, Head of Marketing
**Date:** 2025-11-20
**Review Type:** Comprehensive Marketing Audit

---

## Executive Summary

As a marketing professional with 12 years of experience, I've reviewed the Solutions Delivered website through the lens of lead generation, brand positioning, conversion optimization, and marketing infrastructure. While the website demonstrates strong fundamentals in design and accessibility, it falls significantly short as a marketing asset.

**The Bottom Line:** This website looks professional but doesn't work hard enough for the business. It's a digital brochure when it should be a lead generation engine. There's no content marketing infrastructure, minimal social proof, no analytics implementation visible, and limited conversion paths. For a consultancy competing in a crowded B2B market, this represents a significant missed opportunity.

**Quick Stats:**
- **Conversion Paths:** 1 primary (contact form only)
- **Lead Magnets:** 0
- **Case Studies:** 0
- **Client Testimonials:** 0
- **Blog/Content Hub:** None
- **Analytics Visible:** None
- **Social Proof:** Generic trust indicators only
- **CTA Placement:** Good (multiple placements)
- **Brand Consistency:** Excellent

---

## Strengths

### 1. Strong Visual Brand Identity
The website has excellent brand consistency throughout. The blue colour palette (#198fd9, #1a7fc7, #0f6ba8) is distinctive and professional, used consistently across all pages. Typography is clean and readable. The gradient effects and modern design language create a premium feel that positions the company appropriately for a B2B consultancy.

### 2. Clear Value Proposition
The hero section immediately communicates "Delivering Solutions is in Our DNA" with supporting copy that explains what they do. The value proposition is front and center, which is critical for reducing bounce rates. The tagline is memorable and reinforces the company name effectively.

### 3. Excellent CTA Distribution
CTAs are strategically placed throughout the site:
- Hero section (dual CTAs: primary "Get Started" + secondary "View Solutions")
- End of every major section
- Sticky header with "Get Started" button
- Footer contact section
- Multiple entry points to the conversion form

The "Get Started" CTA is consistently styled and prominent, making it easy for prospects to take action regardless of where they are on the site.

### 4. WCAG 2.2 Compliance
Accessibility is a significant marketing advantage. It demonstrates quality, attention to detail, and corporate responsibility. This positions Solutions Delivered as a premium provider and can be leveraged in marketing messaging. It's also excellent for SEO and reduces legal risk.

### 5. Mobile-Responsive Design
The site appears to be fully responsive with good mobile navigation patterns (hamburger menu, appropriate touch targets). With mobile traffic dominating B2B research, this is essential.

### 6. Technical SEO Foundations
- Proper meta descriptions on all pages
- Schema.org markup implemented (Organization, LocalBusiness, Services)
- Clean URL structure (trailing slashes, readable paths)
- Canonical tags present
- Sitemap exists
- Semantic HTML structure

### 7. Clear Service Differentiation
The four core services (Web Development, Service Management, Project Management, Business Change) are well-articulated with individual sections, benefits, and anchor links. This makes it easy for different buyer personas to find relevant information quickly.

### 8. Professional "How We Work" Page
The process explanation (Initial Discussion → Proposal & Planning → Collaborative Delivery → Handover & Support) sets realistic expectations and reduces perceived risk. This transparency is valuable for building trust with prospects.

## Weaknesses

### 1. **CRITICAL: Zero Content Marketing Infrastructure**
This is my biggest concern. There is no blog, no resources section, no downloadable content, no guides, no whitepapers—nothing. This means:
- **No organic traffic growth strategy** - You can't rank for long-tail keywords without content
- **No lead nurturing capability** - No way to capture early-stage prospects
- **No thought leadership positioning** - Competitors with blogs will be seen as experts
- **No email marketing funnel** - Nothing to drive newsletter subscriptions
- **No remarketing opportunities** - No content to bring people back with

In today's B2B environment, content marketing isn't optional. Prospects do 60-70% of their research before contacting vendors. Without content, you're not part of that research phase.

### 2. **CRITICAL: No Analytics or Tracking Implementation**
I reviewed the source code and found no Google Analytics, no Google Tag Manager, no marketing automation scripts, no conversion tracking pixels. This means:
- **You can't measure what's working** - No data on traffic sources, user behavior, or conversion paths
- **No conversion rate optimization** - Can't identify drop-off points
- **Can't calculate marketing ROI** - No way to attribute leads to channels
- **No remarketing capability** - Can't retarget visitors with ads
- **Flying blind on campaign performance** - Can't track UTM parameters or campaign effectiveness

This is unacceptable for any business website in 2025. You need Google Analytics 4, Tag Manager, and ideally HubSpot or similar marketing automation integration.

### 3. **CRITICAL: Zero Social Proof**
The website has no:
- Client testimonials
- Case studies
- Client logos
- Project results/metrics
- Awards or certifications
- Industry recognition

The "trust indicators" (WCAG Compliant, Direct Team Collaboration, No-Bloat Philosophy) are generic statements, not evidence. B2B buyers need to see that you've solved problems for companies like theirs. Without this, conversion rates will suffer significantly.

### 4. Limited Conversion Paths
There's only ONE way to convert: fill out the contact form. This misses opportunities to capture leads at different stages of the buyer journey:
- **No newsletter signup** - Can't nurture early-stage prospects
- **No resource downloads** - Can't capture contact info for gated content
- **No consultation booking calendar** - Adds friction (email back-and-forth instead of instant booking)
- **No live chat** - Misses real-time engagement opportunities
- **No phone number visible** - Some prospects prefer to call

Different personas have different preferences. A single contact form limits lead volume.

### 5. Weak Lead Qualification
The contact form collects: Name, Email, Company (optional), Message. This isn't enough to qualify leads effectively. You don't know:
- Company size
- Budget range
- Timeline
- Specific service interest
- How they found you

This means sales will spend time with unqualified leads, or worse, qualified leads will get inadequate follow-up.

### 6. No Campaign Landing Page Capability
Every page is a permanent site page. There's no infrastructure for creating campaign-specific landing pages for:
- Paid advertising campaigns
- Email marketing campaigns
- Partner referrals
- Industry events
- Seasonal promotions

This makes it difficult to run targeted campaigns with optimized messaging and removes the ability to A/B test different value propositions.

### 7. Limited SEO Keyword Targeting
While technical SEO is solid, the content is generic. Pages target broad terms like "business solutions" but miss specific long-tail opportunities:
- "Laravel development company UK"
- "ITIL service management consultant"
- "business change management consultancy"
- "project management for digital transformation"

Without a content strategy, you're not capturing search traffic from prospects actively looking for your specific services.

### 8. No Persona-Specific Journeys
The website treats all visitors the same. There's no segmentation for:
- CIOs vs. CTOs vs. Project Managers
- Small business vs. enterprise
- Specific industries (healthcare, finance, retail)
- Problem types (digital transformation, process improvement, emergency recovery)

Personalized experiences convert better. Right now, everyone gets the same generic journey.

### 9. Missing Marketing Integration Points
No visible integration with:
- HubSpot/Marketo/Pardot (marketing automation)
- LinkedIn (social selling)
- CRM (lead capture to sales handoff)
- Email marketing platforms
- Webinar platforms
- Review sites (G2, Clutch, Trustpilot)

This creates manual work and data silos that hurt lead response time and follow-up quality.

### 10. Careers Page Disconnect
The Careers page doesn't connect to marketing goals:
- No employee advocacy program
- No culture content for employer branding
- No way to capture talent pool contacts
- Doesn't reinforce "why work with us" for clients (great team = great delivery)

This page could support recruitment marketing AND client acquisition marketing simultaneously.

## Specific Issues

### Homepage Issues

1. **Hero Section:** While visually strong, the hero lacks a supporting credibility statement. Consider adding: "Trusted by 50+ UK businesses" or similar with client logos.

2. **Trust Indicators:** "WCAG 2.2 Compliant," "Direct Team Collaboration," "No-Bloat Philosophy" are differentiators but lack proof. Add links to case studies or certifications.

3. **Services Section:** Good overview, but each service card should have a "Download Service Overview PDF" CTA to capture early-stage leads.

4. **No Above-the-Fold Social Proof:** The hero section should include at minimum a client logo strip or a brief testimonial quote.

### Solutions Page Issues

1. **No Pricing Guidance:** B2B buyers want to understand if they can afford you. Even ranges like "Small projects from £5k" help qualify leads.

2. **Missing FAQs:** Each service should have an FAQ section addressing common objections and questions.

3. **No Case Study Links:** After describing each service, there should be "See how we helped [Client Name] achieve [Result]"

4. **No Service Comparison:** Prospects may not know which service they need. A comparison table or decision tree would help.

### About Page Issues

1. **No Team Photos/Bios:** People buy from people. Show your team with names, roles, and brief backgrounds.

2. **No Company History:** When founded, growth milestones, notable achievements. This builds credibility.

3. **No Certifications/Accreditations:** List professional certifications, partnerships, industry memberships.

4. **Missing "Our Clients" Section:** Industry logos or list would strengthen trust significantly.

### How We Work Page Issues

1. **No Timeline Expectations:** "Initial Discussion within 24 hours" is good, but what about the full engagement timeline?

2. **No Pricing Model Explanation:** Fixed-price vs. day-rate is mentioned briefly, but needs expansion and typical project costs.

3. **No Risk Reversal:** No money-back guarantee, satisfaction guarantee, or trial period mentioned.

### Get Started Page Issues

1. **Form is Too Simple:** Not collecting enough qualification data (company size, budget, timeline, how they found you).

2. **No Alternative Contact Methods:** No phone number, no office address, no live chat option.

3. **No Expectation Setting:** "We'll respond within 24 hours" is mentioned in hero but not near the form itself.

4. **No Privacy/Security Reassurance:** Should mention GDPR compliance, data handling, etc. near form.

5. **No Calendar Booking Option:** Tools like Calendly would reduce friction and improve conversion rates.

### Careers Page Issues

1. **No Employee Stories:** Should feature profiles of current team members and their career journeys.

2. **No Application Process Clarity:** What happens after they contact you?

3. **No Benefits Detail:** "Work-Life Balance" and "Competitive Pay" are mentioned but not detailed.

4. **No Culture Content:** Photos, videos, or detailed descriptions of what it's like to work there.

### Technical/Infrastructure Issues

1. **No Google Analytics:** Cannot measure traffic, sources, behavior, conversions.

2. **No Google Tag Manager:** Cannot deploy marketing tags without developer help.

3. **No Conversion Tracking:** Cannot track form submissions, button clicks, or micro-conversions.

4. **No Heatmapping/Session Recording:** Tools like Hotjar would show exactly how visitors interact with the site.

5. **No A/B Testing Framework:** Cannot test different headlines, CTAs, or layouts to improve conversion rates.

6. **No Marketing Automation:** Cannot nurture leads with automated email sequences.

7. **No CRM Integration:** Leads from contact form likely go to email, not directly into a sales pipeline.

8. **No Live Chat:** Missing real-time engagement opportunities.

9. **No Exit-Intent Popups:** Missing opportunity to capture emails from bouncing visitors.

10. **No Remarketing Pixels:** Cannot retarget website visitors with paid advertising.

## Recommendations

### Immediate Priority (Next 30 Days)

#### 1. Implement Analytics and Tracking (CRITICAL)
**Why:** You're flying blind without data. Every day without analytics is lost intelligence.

**Actions:**
- Install Google Analytics 4 with proper event tracking
- Set up Google Tag Manager for flexible tag deployment
- Configure goal tracking for form submissions, button clicks, key page views
- Install Facebook Pixel and LinkedIn Insight Tag for B2B remarketing
- Set up Google Search Console and verify the property
- Create a measurement plan documenting all tracked events

**Estimated Impact:** Foundation for all future optimization efforts. Essential.

#### 2. Add Core Social Proof Elements
**Why:** Trust is the #1 barrier to conversion in B2B services. Prospects need to see evidence of success.

**Actions:**
- Create 3-5 case studies (anonymized if necessary) with specific results
- Add client testimonial section to homepage with photos and full names
- Create a client logo strip for homepage (minimum 8-10 recognizable brands)
- Add "Results" statistics to homepage (e.g., "£2M+ in client savings," "98% client satisfaction")
- Create video testimonials for maximum impact (even iPhone quality is fine)

**Estimated Impact:** 20-30% improvement in conversion rate based on industry benchmarks.

#### 3. Enhance Lead Capture on Contact Form
**Why:** Better qualification means higher quality leads and more efficient sales process.

**Actions:**
- Add fields: Company Size, Budget Range, Timeline, Primary Interest (service), How Did You Hear About Us
- Make company name required (not optional)
- Add smart conditional logic (if they select Web Development, ask about tech stack)
- Implement calendar booking option (Calendly or similar) as alternative to form
- Add live chat widget for real-time engagement
- Set up auto-responder email with next steps and timeline

**Estimated Impact:** 15-20% increase in lead quality, reducing sales time waste.

### High Priority (60-90 Days)

#### 4. Launch Content Marketing Program
**Why:** Content is the engine for organic growth, thought leadership, and lead nurturing.

**Actions:**
- Create "Resources" or "Insights" section in main navigation
- Launch blog with 2-4 posts per month on topics like:
  - "How to Choose a Laravel Development Partner"
  - "ITIL Service Management Best Practices for SMBs"
  - "Project Management Pitfalls in Digital Transformation"
  - "Business Change Management Checklist"
- Create 2-3 gated resources (whitepapers, guides, templates) for lead capture
- Develop content calendar aligned with buyer journey stages
- Promote content via LinkedIn and email
- Optimize all content for SEO with target keywords

**Estimated Impact:** 100-200% increase in organic traffic within 6 months. Positions company as thought leader.

#### 5. Build Persona-Specific Landing Pages
**Why:** Generic messaging converts poorly. Specific messaging for specific audiences converts better.

**Actions:**
- Create landing pages for key personas:
  - "Service Management for Healthcare Organizations"
  - "Digital Transformation for Financial Services"
  - "Laravel Development for SaaS Startups"
  - "Project Management for Regulatory Compliance"
- Each page should have persona-specific pain points, case studies, and CTAs
- Use for targeted paid advertising campaigns
- Include exit-intent popups offering relevant resources

**Estimated Impact:** 25-40% improvement in paid campaign conversion rates.

#### 6. Implement Marketing Automation
**Why:** Nurture leads who aren't ready to buy now. Stay top-of-mind until they are.

**Actions:**
- Choose platform (HubSpot, Marketo, or Pardot recommended for B2B)
- Integrate with contact form and CRM
- Set up lead scoring model
- Create email nurture sequences:
  - Welcome series for newsletter subscribers
  - Resource download follow-up
  - Re-engagement for inactive leads
  - Event/webinar invitations
- Create behavioral triggers (visited pricing 3x, downloaded 2 resources, etc.)

**Estimated Impact:** 30-50% increase in lead-to-opportunity conversion rate.

### Medium Priority (90-180 Days)

#### 7. Develop Comprehensive SEO Strategy
**Why:** Organic search is the highest ROI channel for B2B lead generation.

**Actions:**
- Conduct keyword research for each service area
- Create service-specific sub-pages (e.g., "Laravel E-commerce Development," "ITIL Process Design")
- Optimize existing pages with target keywords in headers, meta descriptions, and content
- Build location pages if you serve specific UK regions
- Create FAQ pages for each service to capture question-based searches
- Develop backlink acquisition strategy (guest posts, partner links, directory listings)
- Monitor rankings and traffic monthly, adjust strategy quarterly

**Estimated Impact:** 150-300% increase in organic traffic within 12 months.

#### 8. Create Video Content
**Why:** Video increases engagement, dwell time, and trust. It's also highly shareable.

**Actions:**
- Create "About Us" video (2-3 minutes) for homepage
- Film client testimonial videos (1-2 minutes each)
- Create service explainer videos (90 seconds each)
- Produce thought leadership content for LinkedIn and YouTube
- Add videos to key conversion pages (About, Services, Get Started)
- Host on YouTube for additional discoverability

**Estimated Impact:** 20-30% increase in time on site. 10-15% lift in conversion rate on pages with video.

#### 9. Build Email Marketing Program
**Why:** Email has the highest ROI of any marketing channel (typically $36-$42 per $1 spent).

**Actions:**
- Add newsletter signup to footer and blog sidebar
- Create monthly newsletter with:
  - Industry insights
  - New blog posts
  - Case study highlights
  - Company news
- Segment list by interest area and engagement level
- A/B test subject lines and send times
- Track open rates, click rates, and conversions

**Estimated Impact:** 25-35% increase in repeat website visits. Significant increase in lead volume.

#### 10. Launch Paid Advertising Campaigns
**Why:** Organic takes time. Paid provides immediate visibility and lead flow while organic ramps up.

**Actions:**
- Start with LinkedIn Ads (best B2B targeting)
- Create campaigns by service and persona
- Use content offers (whitepapers, guides) as lead magnets
- Implement retargeting for website visitors
- Test Google Ads for high-intent keywords
- Set budget at £1,500-£3,000/month initially
- Track cost per lead and cost per acquisition religiously

**Estimated Impact:** Immediate lead flow. Target: 15-30 qualified leads per month at £75-150 cost per lead.

### Long-Term Strategic (6-12+ Months)

#### 11. Build Partner/Referral Program
Create structured partner program with agencies, complementary service providers, and past clients to drive qualified referrals.

#### 12. Develop Customer Advocacy Program
Turn satisfied clients into advocates with structured testimonial requests, case study creation, and referral incentives.

#### 13. Create Online Community or Forum
Build engaged community around service management, project management, or business change topics.

#### 14. Launch Annual Industry Report
Conduct original research and publish findings to generate PR, backlinks, and thought leadership positioning.

#### 15. Implement Account-Based Marketing (ABM)
If you have specific target accounts, create personalized campaigns with custom content, outreach, and experiences.

## Final Thoughts

I want to emphasize: this is not a bad website. The design is professional, the messaging is clear, and the technical foundation is solid. Many companies have worse starting points.

However, as someone responsible for driving leads and demonstrating marketing ROI, I see this as an underperforming asset. It's doing the absolute minimum—providing information and a contact form—when it should be actively:
- Attracting qualified traffic through content
- Building trust through social proof
- Capturing leads at multiple journey stages
- Nurturing prospects until they're ready to buy
- Providing data to optimize performance continuously

The recommendations I've outlined aren't nice-to-haves. They're standard practice for modern B2B marketing in 2025. Without them, you're at a significant competitive disadvantage.

**My recommendation to leadership:** Invest in transforming this from a digital brochure into a lead generation engine. Start with analytics and social proof (low cost, high impact), then build toward a comprehensive content marketing program. The alternative is continuing to rely entirely on outbound sales and referrals, which isn't scalable.

The website should be your best salesperson, working 24/7 to attract, qualify, and convert prospects. Right now, it's just answering the phone.
