# Consultant Review: Solutions Delivered Website

**Reviewer:** Alexandra Singh, Independent Management Consultant
**Date:** November 20, 2025
**Review Type:** Partnership Evaluation & Professional Assessment
**Focus Areas:** Partnership Potential, Service Clarity, Professional Reputation, Collaboration Opportunities

---

## 1. Executive Summary

I've conducted a thorough evaluation of the Solutions Delivered website from the perspective of a potential partner, referral source, and market competitor. As an independent consultant specializing in business transformation and change management, I'm always assessing other professionals in my network for collaboration opportunities, referral potential, and market positioning.

**Key Finding:** Solutions Delivered presents a professional, accessible website with clear service offerings that overlap with my practice in some areas and complement it in others. However, the website lacks critical information that would help me assess partnership viability: there's no team information, no case studies to validate quality, no partnership/collaboration section, and no thought leadership content to demonstrate expertise depth.

**The Partnership Question:** Can I confidently refer my clients to this consultancy? Not yet. While the service descriptions are clear and the technical quality appears high, there's insufficient evidence of track record, team expertise, or collaborative working approach to make that trust-based decision.

**Overall Assessment:** 6/10 - Clear positioning with professional presentation, but missing the credibility markers and partnership signals needed for professional referrals

## 2. Strengths

The website provides crystal-clear descriptions of their four core services:
- **Web Development:** Laravel-based, WCAG 2.2 compliant, no-bloat philosophy
- **Service Management:** ITIL-aligned process optimization
- **Project Management:** Risk mitigation and delivery oversight
- **Business Change:** Organizational transformation support

This clarity helps me immediately understand where we overlap (Business Change, Project Management) and where we complement (Web Development, Service Management). I appreciate knowing exactly what they do - it makes referral decisions straightforward.

### 2.2 Strong Accessibility Commitment
The emphasis on WCAG 2.2 compliance throughout the site demonstrates professional standards I value:
- Accessibility built-in, not bolted-on
- Competitive advantage for public sector work
- Demonstrates attention to detail and quality
- Reduces legal risk for clients

This is a strong quality indicator that suggests they deliver professional work.

### 2.3 Professional Brand Presentation
- Clean, modern design that reflects competence
- Consistent messaging across pages
- Well-structured information architecture
- Professional copywriting without excessive jargon
- UK-based positioning clearly stated

The website looks like a legitimate consultancy, not a one-person operation posing as a firm.

### 2.4 Collaborative Approach Messaging
The "How We Work" page emphasizes:
- Direct collaboration with client teams
- Transparent communication
- No layers of bureaucracy
- Partnership approach over vendor relationship

This aligns with my values and suggests cultural compatibility for potential collaboration.

### 2.5 Clear Value Propositions
- "No-Bloat Philosophy" (for web development)
- "Direct Team Collaboration" (no account managers)
- "WCAG 2.2 Compliant" (accessibility focus)

These differentiators help position them clearly in the market.

### 2.6 Technical Quality Indicators
From reviewing the technical implementation:

This suggests they practice what they preach about quality.

## 3. Weaknesses

### 3.1 **CRITICAL: No Team Information**
There is absolutely no information about who works at Solutions Delivered:
- No "Team" page
- No bios or backgrounds
- No qualifications or certifications
- No LinkedIn profiles linked
- No photos or names
- No indication of team size

**Impact on Partnership:** I cannot refer clients to faceless consultants. I need to know:
- Who will actually do the work?
- What are their qualifications?
- Do they have relevant industry experience?
- Are they qualified to handle enterprise clients?
- What methodologies/frameworks do they use?

This is a **major red flag** for professional referrals. My reputation is on the line when I recommend someone.

### 3.2 **CRITICAL: No Case Studies or Portfolio**
Zero evidence of past work:
- No case studies with measurable outcomes
- No client examples (even anonymized)
- No portfolio pieces
- No "success stories" section
- No ROI statistics or impact metrics

**Impact:** I cannot validate their claims about delivering solutions. Anyone can say they do "Business Change" - show me a transformation you've led. What were the measurable outcomes? What challenges did you overcome?

Without this, I'm being asked to trust blindly. That's not how professional referral networks work.

### 3.3 **CRITICAL: No Client Testimonials**
Not a single client testimonial or reference:
- No quotes from satisfied clients
- No star ratings or reviews
- No third-party validation
- No industry awards or recognition
- No professional certifications mentioned

**Professional Standard:** In consulting, testimonials are currency. They're how we demonstrate trustworthiness and build credibility. The absence is conspicuous.

### 3.4 No Partnership Information
There's no section addressing:
- Collaboration opportunities
- Subcontracting arrangements
- Referral partnerships
- Associate consultant programs
- How they work with other firms

**Impact:** Are they even open to partnerships? Do they see other consultants as competitors or potential collaborators? This information gap makes it hard to explore working together.

### 3.5 No Thought Leadership Content
Missing content that would demonstrate expertise:
- No blog or insights section
- No whitepapers or guides
- No webinars or presentations
- No published articles
- No industry commentary

**Professional Expectation:** Senior consultants establish credibility through thought leadership. The absence suggests either:
- They're too new to have built this content
- They don't see value in sharing expertise
- They lack the depth to comment authoritatively

Any of these options is concerning.

### 3.6 Vague Scale Indicators
The website doesn't clarify:
- What size clients do they serve? (SMB? Enterprise? Public sector?)
- What's the typical project value range?
- How many clients have they served?
- What's their capacity for new work?
- Do they work solo or have a team?

**Impact on Referrals:** I need to know if a £500k transformation project is within their capability or if they focus on smaller engagements. The mismatch wastes everyone's time.

### 3.7 No Methodology Detail
While they mention services, there's no depth on:
- What change management methodologies do they use? (Prosci? Kotter? Agile?)
- What project management framework? (PRINCE2? Agile? Hybrid?)
- What service management standards? (ITIL v3? ITIL 4? Custom?)
- What tools and techniques do they employ?

**Professional Concern:** Methodology matters. If I refer a client with an Agile mindset to a rigid waterfall shop (or vice versa), that's a bad match.

### 3.8 No Professional Network Indicators
Missing signals about their professional standing:
- No professional body memberships mentioned
- No industry associations
- No conference speaking
- No certifications displayed
- No partner ecosystem

**Network Assessment:** I don't see how they're connected to the broader professional community. Are they isolated? Are they engaged with industry trends?

### 3.9 Unclear Competitive Positioning
While the services are clear, the positioning is ambiguous:
- How do they compare to larger firms like Accenture, Deloitte, PA Consulting?
- What's their sweet spot vs. boutique agencies?
- Why choose them over freelancers?
- What's their unique value proposition beyond "no bloat"?

**Market Context:** I need to understand where they fit in the competitive landscape to make appropriate referrals.

### 3.10 No Pricing Transparency
Zero pricing information:
- No day rates
- No project size ranges
- No pricing models explained
- No ROI frameworks

**Referral Impact:** If I refer someone expecting £10k and they quote £100k, that's embarrassing for everyone. Some ballpark guidance would help.

The About page exists but is remarkably shallow:
- Generic mission statement
- Vague "experienced team" claims (but who?)
- Values listed but not demonstrated
- No founding story or company history
- No explanation of what drives them

**What I Need:** Authentic stories about why they started this business, what problems they've solved, what drives their approach. This builds connection and trust.

### 4.2 Contact Form Only
The only way to reach them is through a contact form:
- No direct phone number displayed
- No office address
- No LinkedIn company page link

**Professional Concern:** For business development conversations, I'd prefer a direct phone call or LinkedIn message. The form feels like a barrier.

**Note:** While there may be security/spam reasons for this, it doesn't facilitate peer-to-peer professional networking.

### 4.3 No Client Types Specified
Who do they actually work with?
- Sectors: Healthcare? Finance? Manufacturing? Public sector?
- Sizes: Startups? SMBs? Mid-market? Enterprise?
- Geographies: UK-only? International?
- Buyer personas: CTO? COO? Change Director?

The Careers page is generic:

- No indication they're hiring
- No detail about working there

**Signal:** This suggests either:
- It's a very small team (1-3 people?)
- They're not actively growing

This impacts my assessment of their capacity and stability.

### 4.5 No Social Proof Integration
The site has no:

**Referral Need:** The more specific the positioning, the better I can match clients.

### 4.7 No Risk Mitigation Information
For expensive consulting engagements, clients want to know:

- What happens if things go wrong?
- What guarantees or warranties?
- How do you handle scope changes?

**Trust Factor:** These details matter for enterprise referrals.

### 4.8 No Content Marketing
Missed opportunities to demonstrate expertise:
- No "Insights" section with articles
- No downloadable resources or templates
- No newsletter signup
- No webinars or events
- No podcast appearances

**Thought Leadership Gap:** In consulting, content is how we demonstrate depth of expertise. The absence is noticeable.

## 5. Recommendations

### Priority 1: Immediate (Critical for Referral Confidence)

#### 5.1 Create Team/Leadership Page (8-12 hours)
**Action:** Introduce the actual people behind Solutions Delivered.

**Content per team member:**
- Professional photo
- Name and title
- Background and experience
- Relevant qualifications/certifications
- Areas of expertise
- LinkedIn profile link
- Personal approach/philosophy (optional)

**Why This Matters:** I need to know who I'm referring clients to. Names, faces, qualifications, and track records build trust.

**Example Structure:**
```
Alex Thompson - Founder & Principal Consultant
- 15 years in service management transformation
- ITIL 4 Managing Professional
- Led £2M transformation at Fortune 500 company
- Previously: Senior Consultant at [Major Firm]
```

**Business Value:** This single addition would immediately increase my confidence in making referrals by 40%.

#### 5.2 Add Case Studies Section (12-20 hours)
**Action:** Create 3-5 case studies demonstrating successful delivery.

**Structure per case study:**
- **Client Context:** Industry, size, challenge (anonymize if needed)
- **Problem Statement:** What was broken? What were they trying to achieve?
- **Approach:** How did you solve it? What methodology?
- **Delivery:** What did you actually do? Timeline? Team size?
- **Outcomes:** Measurable results (% improvement, cost savings, time reduction)
- **Testimonial:** Client quote (even if anonymized as "CTO of Healthcare Software Company")

**Why This Matters:** Case studies are proof. They show you've done this before and achieved results.

**Referral Impact:** Each strong case study increases referral confidence by 15-20%.

#### 5.3 Add Testimonials Throughout (4-6 hours)
**Action:** Collect and display client testimonials on relevant pages.

**Placement:**
- Solutions page: Service-specific testimonials
- About page: General company testimonials
- Home page: Featured testimonial or two
- Dedicated testimonials/reviews page

**Format:**
- Client quote
- Name and title
- Company (or "Leading UK Healthcare Provider" if anonymized)
- Optional photo

**Minimum Viable:** Start with 5-8 strong testimonials. Quality over quantity.

**Why This Matters:** Social proof is essential for trust. In consulting, word-of-mouth is everything.

### Priority 2: Important (Within 4 Weeks)

#### 5.4 Create Partnership/Collaboration Page (6-8 hours)
**Action:** Explicitly address partnership opportunities.

**Content:**
- How you work with other consultancies
- Subcontracting arrangements
- Referral partner program
- Associate consultant opportunities
- Complementary services you're looking for
- How to explore collaboration

**Why This Matters:** Makes it clear you're open to partnerships and explains the path forward.

**Example Messaging:**
```
"We actively collaborate with complementary consultancies.
If you work in areas like [X, Y, Z] and seek technical partners
for web development or service management, let's talk."
```

#### 5.5 Add "Our Approach/Methodology" Detail (8-10 hours)
**Action:** Create detailed methodology pages or section.

**Content per service:**
- **Business Change:** What change management framework? (Prosci? ADKAR? Custom?)
- **Project Management:** What methodology? (PRINCE2? Agile? Hybrid?)
- **Service Management:** What standard? (ITIL 4? ISO 20000?)
- **Web Development:** What process? (Agile? Scrum? Kanban?)

**Include:**
- Typical project phases
- Deliverables at each stage
- How you engage stakeholders
- Quality assurance approach
- Knowledge transfer process

**Why This Matters:** Demonstrates depth and helps me assess methodology fit for clients.

#### 5.6 Add Client Types/Ideal Customer Profile (4-6 hours)
**Action:** Clarify who you serve best.

**Content:**
- Industries you specialize in
- Company sizes (employee count, revenue range)
- Common scenarios/use cases
- Project value ranges (£10k-£50k? £50k-£500k?)
- Geographic focus
- Buyer personas

**Why This Matters:** Helps me identify which of my clients are good fits.

**Example:**
```
"We work best with mid-sized UK organizations (50-500 employees)
undergoing digital transformation or significant operational change.
Typical projects range from £20k to £150k."
```

#### 5.7 Add Pricing Guidance/Transparency (4-6 hours)
**Action:** Provide ballpark pricing to set expectations.

**Content:**
- Day rate range (£800-£1,500/day typical for UK consultants)
- Typical project costs by service
- What's included in pricing
- How you structure engagements (fixed-price? Time & materials?)
- Payment terms

**Why This Matters:** Avoids wasting time on poor-fit inquiries and sets realistic expectations.

**Approach:** Many consultancies say "Starting from £XX" or "Typical projects range £XX-£XX" without giving exact quotes.

#### 5.8 Create Professional Credentials Section (3-4 hours)
**Action:** Display certifications, memberships, and accreditations.

**Content:**
- Professional certifications (PMP, PRINCE2, ITIL, Change Management certs)
- Professional body memberships (APM, CMI, BCS, etc.)
- Industry accreditations
- Insurance coverage (Professional Indemnity)
- Quality standards (ISO certifications if applicable)

**Why This Matters:** Demonstrates professional standing and commitment to standards.

### Priority 3: Enhancement (Within 2-3 Months)

#### 5.9 Launch Thought Leadership Content (Ongoing)
**Action:** Start publishing insights and expertise.

**Content Types:**
- Blog/Insights section with monthly articles
- LinkedIn company updates
- Industry commentary

**Topics aligned to services:**

#### 5.10 Create Resource Library (12-16 hours)
**Action:** Offer downloadable resources that demonstrate expertise.

**Resources:**
- Checklists (e.g., "Pre-Project Risk Assessment Checklist")

- Whitepapers on your methodology
- Frameworks you've developed

**Why This Matters:** Demonstrates generosity, builds authority, generates leads, creates touchpoints.

**Lead Capture:** Optional email gate for larger resources.

#### 5.11 Build Social Media Presence (Ongoing)
**Action:** Establish active presence on professional networks.

**Platforms:**
- LinkedIn (company page + personal profiles)
- Twitter/X (for industry commentary)
- Maybe: YouTube (for explainer content)

**Content:**
- Share insights from blog
- Comment on industry trends
- Engage with professional community
- Showcase project successes (with permission)
- Celebrate team achievements

**Why This Matters:** Demonstrates active engagement with professional community, creates discoverability, humanizes the brand.

#### 5.12 Add "How We're Different" Section (4-6 hours)
**Action:** Create competitive differentiation content.

**Structure:**
- Us vs. Large Firms (Big 4): More personalized, direct access, cost-effective
- Us vs. Boutique Firms: Technical depth, proven methodology, cross-functional
- Us vs. Freelancers: Team capacity, quality assurance, business continuity

**Positioning:**
- When to choose Solutions Delivered
- When to choose alternatives
- Our sweet spot

**Why This Matters:** Helps partners understand your competitive position and make appropriate referrals.

#### 5.13 Implement Newsletter (6-8 hours initial setup)
**Action:** Create monthly/quarterly email newsletter.

**Content:**
- Industry insights
- Case study highlights
- Company updates
- Upcoming events or webinars
- Useful resources

**Audience:**
- Clients (past and current)
- Prospects
- Partners and referral sources
- Professional network

**Why This Matters:** Maintains visibility, nurtures relationships, demonstrates ongoing expertise.

## 8. Final Thoughts

As an independent consultant with 16 years of experience, I've learned that **reputation is everything in this business**. When I refer a client to another consultancy, I'm putting my professional reputation on the line. If they do excellent work, it reflects well on me. If they fail, it damages my relationships.

That's why I cannot currently refer clients to Solutions Delivered, despite the professional website and clear service positioning. **I need proof.**

### What I'd Need to See

**To Refer a £10k-£25k project:**
- 2-3 case studies showing successful delivery
- LinkedIn profiles for key team members
- 3-4 client testimonials
- Clear methodology overview

**To Refer a £50k+ project:**
- Everything above, plus:
- Detailed case study in relevant sector
- Professional certifications displayed
- Conversation with principal consultant
- Client references available

**To Explore Partnership:**
- Partnership/collaboration page explaining approach
- Understanding of team capacity
- Discussion of complementary strengths
- Trial collaboration on smaller project

### The Opportunity

The good news is that all of these gaps are fixable. Solutions Delivered has:
- Clear service positioning (hard to get right)
- Professional website presence
- Collaborative messaging
- Technical capabilities (based on site quality)

What's missing is the **evidence and access**. Adding team information, case studies, testimonials, and partnership details would immediately increase this rating from 6/10 to 8+/10.

### My Recommendation to Solutions Delivered

**You've built a foundation; now add the credibility layer.**

In consulting, clients (and partners like me) buy people, not websites. Show us who you are. Prove you've done this before. Demonstrate your expertise through thought leadership. Make it easy for professional peers to assess whether we should work together.

**The Website is Good. Make It Great.**

Right now, you're a professionally presented mystery. With relatively modest additional effort (team page, case studies, testimonials), you could be a consultancy I actively refer to clients and seek partnerships with.

**I want to be able to trust you with my clients. Give me the information I need to do that.**
