# Website Review: Existing Customer Perspective

**Reviewer:** Andrew Morrison
**Role:** Operations Director
**Company:** Mid-size manufacturing company
**Relationship:** Existing customer (2 years)
**Review Date:** November 20, 2025
**Review Type:** Existing Customer Experience Assessment

---

## Executive Summary

As a two-year customer of Solutions Delivered, I'm frankly disappointed with the website. It's clear the company has invested significant effort into attracting new customers, but has completely forgotten about those of us who are already paying for their services. There's no customer portal, no clear support access, no resources, and no indication that the company values its existing client relationships. If I were evaluating whether to renew our contract, this website would raise serious concerns about whether Solutions Delivered is truly committed to long-term partnerships.

**Overall Rating: 3/10**

The website looks professional and would probably appeal to prospective customers, but for someone who's been working with the company for two years, it feels like I've been forgotten. This isn't acceptable for a consultancy that claims to value "collaborative partnerships."

---

## Strengths

### 1. Professional Presentation
The website is well-designed, accessible, and professionally presented. It clearly demonstrates technical competence and attention to quality. As an existing customer, I can at least feel confident recommending the company based on their public-facing image.

### 2. Clear Service Descriptions
The Solutions page provides comprehensive descriptions of all services offered. This is useful when I'm considering expanding our engagement to additional service areas. I can easily understand what's available and whether it might fit our needs.

### 3. Process Transparency
The "How We Work" page clearly outlines the engagement process. While this is primarily aimed at new customers, it's reassuring to see that the collaborative approach we've experienced is consistently communicated.

### 4. Accessibility Features
As someone who values inclusive practices, I appreciate that the website is WCAG 2.2 compliant. This aligns with the quality standards we've experienced in our project work together.

### 5. Contact Options Available
There is at least a contact form available, even if it's not specifically designed for existing customers. In a pinch, I could use it to reach out, though I'd prefer more direct access to my account team.

---

## Weaknesses

### 1. **CRITICAL: No Customer Portal or Login**
This is my biggest concern. After two years as a customer, I have no way to log in to access:
- My account information
- Project documentation
- Contract details
- Billing information
- Communication history
- Active project status

I'm forced to dig through email archives or contact the team directly for information I should be able to access on demand. This is incredibly inefficient and doesn't reflect the professional service we receive in our actual project work.

### 2. **No Clear Support Access**
When I have an urgent issue or question, where do I go? The website offers:
- A generic contact form (same one new prospects use)
- No support phone number
- No support email address
- No emergency contact information
- No ticketing system
- No indication of support hours or SLA

I need to know my CFO can get rapid assistance during a critical issue, not wait 24 hours for a generic form response. This is unacceptable for an ongoing service relationship.

### 3. **No Resources or Knowledge Base**
Where are the resources that would help me get more value from our engagement?
- Best practice guides
- Templates and frameworks
- How-to documentation
- FAQs
- Troubleshooting guides
- Industry insights

I'm paying for expertise, but there's no self-service knowledge that could help my team between engagements or extend the value of what we've learned.

### 4. **No Evidence of Innovation or Updates**
How do I know Solutions Delivered is staying current and bringing fresh ideas? There's no:
- Company news or blog
- Service updates or announcements
- New offerings or capabilities
- Case studies demonstrating recent successes
- Thought leadership content

My CFO wants to see continued value and innovation. This website suggests the company is static and possibly behind the times.

### 5. **No Account Management Visibility**
Who is my account manager? How do I contact them? What's their availability? There's absolutely no information about:
- Account team contacts
- Dedicated support channels
- Customer success resources
- Account review schedules

The website treats me the same as someone who's never heard of the company. That's insulting to a two-year customer relationship.

### 6. **No Path for Service Expansion**
While the Solutions page lists all services, there's no customer-specific path for:
- Requesting additional services
- Scheduling a consultation about new needs
- Understanding how to expand our engagement
- Accessing customer-specific pricing

I need to use the same "Get Started" form as a cold lead. This doesn't reflect our established relationship.

### 7. **No Trust Building for Renewal Decisions**
When justifying our continued investment to stakeholders, I need evidence:
- Success stories and case studies
- Client testimonials
- ROI data and metrics
- Industry recognition or certifications
- Awards or achievements

None of this is present. I'm left to rely solely on our direct experience, which makes renewal conversations harder than they should be.

### 8. **No Customer Community or Peer Connections**
Are other companies facing similar challenges? Could we learn from peer experiences? The website offers no:
- Customer community
- User groups
- Customer events
- Webinars or training sessions
- Networking opportunities

I'd value connections with other Operations Directors who are using similar services.

### 9. **No Feedback Mechanism**
How do I provide feedback on our services? There's no:
- Customer satisfaction surveys
- Feedback forms
- Review or testimonial submission
- Suggestion box
- Net Promoter Score collection

This suggests the company isn't actively seeking to improve or measure customer satisfaction.

### 10. **Generic Communication**
The entire website speaks to prospects, not customers. Every call-to-action is "Get Started" or "Contact Us." There's no acknowledgment that existing customers might visit this site with different needs.

## Summary and Final Thoughts

I've been a satisfied customer of Solutions Delivered for two years, and our actual working relationship has been excellent. The team is responsive, professional, and delivers quality work. However, you wouldn't know any of that from visiting this website.

**The disconnect is concerning.** It suggests one of two things:
1. The company is so focused on growth that it's neglecting existing customer relationships
2. The website simply hasn't been updated to reflect the company's customer-centric values

Either way, it creates a problem when I need to justify our continued investment to the CFO and board. I can't point them to case studies, success metrics, or even basic support information. I can't access our account details, billing information, or project documentation without going through my email archives.

**This matters for retention and renewal.** When our contract comes up for renewal, my CFO will ask:
- "What have they done for us lately?"
- "Are they innovating and staying current?"
- "What's our ROI?"
- "How do we access support if we need it?"

Right now, I'd struggle to answer those questions using the website as a resource. I'd be relying entirely on our direct experience and personal communication, which puts more pressure on the account team and makes me more vulnerable if there's a personnel change.

**The irony is apparent:** Solutions Delivered talks about building "genuine partnerships" and "collaborative relationships" on their How We Work page. But partnerships require ongoing communication, support, and mutual value creation. The website provides none of that for existing customers.

**My recommendation to Solutions Delivered:** Invest in your existing customer experience with the same energy you've put into new customer acquisition. Build a customer portal, create resources, publish success stories, and make it easy for customers like me to get support, access information, and justify our continued investment. You've built a strong foundation with the quality of your actual service delivery. Now reflect that in how you support customers through your digital presence.

**For now, I'll continue working with Solutions Delivered because the team and work quality are excellent.** But I'd feel much more confident about our long-term partnership if the website demonstrated the same level of customer commitment that we experience in our day-to-day interactions.

## Appendix: Specific User Journeys That Fail

### Journey 1: "I Need Support Urgently"
**Scenario:** Production system issue requires immediate help
**Current Experience:**
1. Visit website looking for support number
2. Find no support contact anywhere
3. Navigate to "Get Started" contact form (same as new prospects)
4. Fill out form, wait up to 24 hours for response
5. Meanwhile, try to find email in inbox from months ago

**Should Be:**
1. Click "Support" in header
2. See support phone number, email, and chat options
3. Call immediately for urgent issues or log into portal
4. Create support ticket with priority level
5. Receive immediate acknowledgment with SLA commitment

### Journey 2: "I Need to Review Our Contract Terms"
**Scenario:** Finance team asks for contract details for budget planning
**Current Experience:**
1. Search through email for original contract
2. Realize it's lost in archive from 2 years ago
3. Email account manager asking for copy
4. Wait for response and file transfer
5. Manual process each time contract details needed

**Should Be:**
1. Log into customer portal
2. Navigate to "Contracts & Billing"
3. Download current contract immediately
4. Access invoicing history
5. Self-service whenever needed

### Journey 3: "I Want to Explore Adding Another Service"
**Scenario:** New project needs additional service capability
**Current Experience:**
1. Review Solutions page (same information as 2 years ago)
2. Submit generic "Get Started" form
3. Wait for initial consultation call
4. Re-explain existing relationship
5. Eventually get to pricing discussion

**Should Be:**
1. Log into customer portal
2. Click "Add Services" option
3. See customer-specific pricing for additional services
4. Request consultation with account manager directly
5. Streamlined process that recognizes existing relationship

### Journey 4: "I Need to Justify Our Investment to the CFO"
**Scenario:** Budget review requires ROI justification
**Current Experience:**
1. Search website for case studies - find none
2. Look for success metrics - find none
3. Try to find testimonials - find none
4. Rely entirely on personal notes and anecdotal evidence
5. Struggle to make compelling data-driven case

**Should Be:**
1. Log into customer portal
2. Access "Your Success Metrics" dashboard
3. Download executive summary of our engagement
4. Review case studies from similar companies
5. Present comprehensive ROI story to stakeholders
