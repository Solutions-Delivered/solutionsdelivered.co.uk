# Market Research Review - Solutions Delivered Website

**Reviewer:** Samantha Brooks, Market Research Manager
**Date:** November 20, 2025
**Review Type:** Market Research & Customer Insight Analysis
**Experience:** 11 years in market research and consumer insights

---

## Executive Summary

As a market research professional who has designed studies for dozens of B2B service companies, I approached the Solutions Delivered website looking for evidence of market understanding, research-backed positioning, and customer-centric design. What I found concerns me deeply.

This website appears to have been built entirely on assumptions rather than research. There's no evidence that anyone has:
- Talked to potential customers about their needs
- Validated the value proposition with target audiences
- Tested messaging with real prospects
- Researched competitive positioning
- Segmented the market based on actual data
- Used customer insights to inform content

**The brutal truth:** This looks like a website designed by people who *think* they know what customers want, not people who have *asked* customers what they want. That's a recipe for disappointing conversion rates and missed opportunities.

**Quick Assessment:**
- Evidence of market research: 0/10
- Target market definition: 3/10
- Persona foundation: 1/10 (generic assumptions only)
- Customer understanding: 2/10
- Market segmentation: 0/10
- Customer voice present: 0/10
- Feedback mechanisms: 1/10 (basic contact form only)

**Overall Rating: 2.5/10** - This website desperately needs a research foundation before any more money is spent on marketing or development.

## Strengths

I believe in balanced analysis, so let me start with what's working before I explain why this needs serious research investment.

### 1. Clear Service Structure
The four service areas (Web Development, Service Management, Project Management, Business Change) are clearly defined and easy to understand. Someone made a strategic decision about what to offer, and the structure is logical. Whether these are the *right* services for the *right* market is unknown without research, but the structure itself is clear.

### 2. Attempt at Value Proposition
"Delivering Solutions is in Our DNA" and the supporting copy shows someone tried to articulate differentiation. The "no-bloat philosophy," "WCAG 2.2 compliance," and "direct collaboration" are specific claims. However, without research, there's no way to know if these resonate with the target market or matter to them at all.

### 3. Process Transparency
The "How We Work" page outlines a 4-step process with specific commitments (24-hour response time, regular updates, etc.). This shows an understanding that prospects want to know what to expect. Whether these are the right reassurances for the target market is, again, unknown without research.

### 4. Accessible Design
WCAG 2.2 compliance is prominently featured, which suggests someone understands that accessibility matters. Whether this is a key decision factor for their target market—or just assumed to be important—is unclear.

## Weaknesses

Now, let me explain why this website reveals a fundamental lack of market understanding that should be addressed before any other improvements are made.

### 1. **CRITICAL: Zero Evidence of Target Market Research**

I reviewed every page looking for signs that someone has defined and researched the target market. Here's what I found:

**What's Missing:**
- No specification of target company size (startups? SMEs? enterprises?)
- No indication of target industries served
- No geographic focus beyond "UK-based" (London? Scotland? All UK?)
- No company type specification (B2B? B2C? Public sector? Private?)
- No seniority level targeting (C-suite? Middle management? Technical leads?)
- No budget band indication (£5k projects? £50k? £500k?)

**Why This Matters:**
A services firm trying to serve everyone serves no one well. The messaging on this site is generic precisely because it doesn't know who it's talking to. I would bet money that no one has:
- Created an ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) based on data
- Analyzed which customer segments are most profitable
- Researched which markets have the highest need for these services
- Validated demand in different segments
- Sized the addressable market for each service

**Research Needed:**
- Market segmentation study to identify distinct customer segments
- Total addressable market (TAM) sizing for each service area
- Competitive landscape analysis by segment
- Win/loss analysis of past projects to identify patterns

**Impact:** Without target market definition, every marketing dollar is spent inefficiently. You're fishing with a net full of holes.

### 2. **CRITICAL: No Evidence of Customer Research**

Not a single element of this website suggests customer input shaped the design, messaging, or positioning. Compare this to what a research-informed site would have:

**What's Missing:**
- Zero customer quotes or testimonials (no customer voice at all)
- No mention of customer pain points discovered through research
- No "We've heard from customers that..." statements
- No case studies showing real customer problems and solutions
- No survey data or customer insights shared
- No mention of customer feedback mechanisms beyond a contact form
- No evidence of Jobs-to-be-Done research
- No indication of customer journey mapping

**What This Reveals:**
The website describes what Solutions Delivered *does* (services) and *how* they work (process), but never demonstrates understanding of what customers *need* or *struggle with*. This is inside-out thinking, not customer-centric design.

**Example of the Problem:**
The Web Development section says: "Unlike off-the-shelf solutions laden with unnecessary features, we create streamlined, efficient systems that do exactly what you need."

**Research Questions This Raises:**
- Have you surveyed prospects to learn that "off-the-shelf bloat" is actually their top frustration?
- Or is this an assumption based on internal perspective?
- What do customers actually say when asked about their biggest challenges with web systems?
- Is "streamlined" what they want, or do they want "comprehensive" or "future-proof"?
- How do different customer segments prioritize cost vs. features vs. speed vs. quality?

**Without research, this messaging might completely miss what customers care about.**

**Research Needed:**
- Customer needs assessment through interviews and surveys
- Pain point discovery research
- Value proposition testing with target audiences
- Message testing to see which claims resonate
- Customer satisfaction studies with existing clients
- Lost opportunity interviews to understand why prospects chose competitors

**Impact:** Messaging that doesn't address real customer needs won't convert. You might have the right solution but the wrong story.

### 3. **CRITICAL: Personas Appear Assumption-Based, Not Research-Backed**

The website content suggests someone created mental models of customer personas, but there's no evidence these are based on actual research:

**Red Flags:**
- Generic language that could apply to any B2B buyer
- No specific persona names or profiles shared
- No indication of different customer types with different needs
- Same messaging for all visitors (no segmentation evident)
- No persona-specific journeys or content paths

**What Research-Backed Personas Would Look Like:**
A site informed by proper persona research would have:
- Different entry points for different persona types
- Specific use cases that reflect real customer situations
- Language and terminology that actual customers use (not internal jargon)
- Pain points articulated in the customer's words
- Success metrics that align with how different personas define success

**Example of Missing Persona Clarity:**
Who exactly is the target buyer for each service?
- For Web Development: Is it the CTO? The Operations Director? The Business Owner?
- For Service Management: Is it the IT Director? The COO? The Service Manager?
- For Project Management: Is it the PMO lead? The Programme Director?
- For Business Change: Is it the CEO? The Change Director? The Transformation Lead?

**Different personas have different pain points, different decision criteria, and different approval processes. This website treats them all the same.**

**Research Needed:**
- Persona development research (minimum 10-15 interviews per persona)
- Demographic and firmographic profiling
- Psychographic research (motivations, values, attitudes)
- Decision journey mapping for each persona
- Buying committee research (who influences, who decides, who approves)
- Persona validation through quantitative surveys

**Impact:** Generic messaging converts poorly. Specific, persona-targeted messaging can improve conversion rates by 50-200%.

## Specific Issues

### Issue 1: Assumed Pain Points vs. Researched Pain Points
**Location:** Throughout site, especially Solutions page
**Problem:** The website describes problems you assume customers have ("off-the-shelf solutions laden with unnecessary features," "complex projects require experienced oversight") without evidence these are actual top pain points.

**Example:**
Service Management section says: "optimize your internal processes to maximize efficiency." But:
- Is efficiency the top concern, or is it effectiveness?
- Do customers use the word "optimize" or do they say "fix" or "simplify"?
- What specific processes are most painful for them?

**Research Recommendation:**
- Conduct pain point discovery interviews with 15-20 prospects and customers
- Ask: "What's your biggest challenge with [service area]?"
- Use their exact words in your copy
- Prioritize pain points by frequency and intensity
- Test messaging with A/B tests

**Impact:** Speaking to real pain points in customer language can increase conversion by 30-50%.

### Issue 2: No Proof of Market Demand
**Location:** Entire strategy
**Problem:** There's no evidence that sufficient market demand exists for these specific services in this specific combination.

**Research Questions:**
- How large is the market for each service in the UK?
- What's the growth rate of each market segment?
- Are these services frequently purchased together or separately?
- What's the typical buying cycle for each?
- Who are the main competitors in each space?
- What market share could you realistically capture?

**Research Recommendation:**
- Commission or conduct market sizing research
- Analyze search volume for key terms
- Survey target companies about service needs and budgets
- Study competitive market share
- Forecast addressable market by segment

**Impact:** Without demand validation, you might be investing in services with limited market opportunity.

### Issue 3: Generic Targeting Creates Weak Positioning
**Location:** All pages
**Problem:** The website doesn't specify who it's for, which makes it harder for the right people to self-identify.

**Better Approach with Research:**
"We help 50-250 employee UK professional services firms modernize their operations through accessible technology and proven processes."

This specific targeting would:
- Attract the right visitors
- Repel the wrong visitors (saving everyone time)
- Enable persona-specific content
- Improve SEO for specific terms
- Make competitive comparison easier

**Research Recommendation:**
- Analyze your best/most profitable past clients
- Identify common patterns (size, industry, needs, budget)
- Research these segments to understand market size
- Choose target segments based on attractiveness and fit
- Test targeted messaging vs. generic messaging

**Impact:** Focused targeting typically improves qualified lead volume by 40-60% while reducing unqualified leads.

### Issue 4: No Jobs-to-be-Done Evidence
**Location:** Service descriptions
**Problem:** The website describes *what* you do, not *why* customers hire you (the "job" they need done).

**Example:**
Web Development section focuses on Laravel, WCAG compliance, and "no-bloat." But what job is the customer trying to get done?
- Replace a failing legacy system?
- Launch a new digital product?
- Improve customer experience?
- Reduce technical debt?
- Enable business growth?

**Research Recommendation:**
- Conduct Jobs-to-be-Done interviews
- Ask: "What were you trying to accomplish when you looked for this service?"
- Understand the functional, emotional, and social dimensions
- Identify competing alternatives (including doing nothing)
- Map the decision journey from "first thought" to "hired"

**Impact:** JTBD research reveals what really drives decisions, leading to more effective positioning and messaging.

### Issue 5: Missing Buyer Committee Research
**Location:** Entire buyer journey
**Problem:** B2B purchases involve multiple stakeholders, but the website doesn't acknowledge this or speak to different roles.

**Research Questions:**
- Who initiates the buying process for each service?
- Who influences the decision?
- Who has budget authority?
- Who has veto power?
- What does each stakeholder care about?
- What objections does each stakeholder have?

**Example:**
For a Project Management engagement:
- Project Manager wants: expertise, reduced risk, on-time delivery
- CFO wants: ROI, budget certainty, value for money
- CEO wants: strategic outcomes, competitive advantage
- IT Director wants: technical compatibility, minimal disruption

**One message can't satisfy all of them. You need multiple messages for multiple audiences.**

**Research Recommendation:**
- Interview buying committees from past projects
- Map decision-making units (DMUs) for each service
- Identify key stakeholders and their priorities
- Create content for each stakeholder role
- Develop multi-stakeholder sales materials

**Impact:** Understanding buying committees can reduce sales cycle length by 20-30% and improve close rates significantly.

### Issue 6: No Pricing Research Informing Strategy
**Location:** How We Work page mentions "transparent pricing" but shows none
**Problem:** No indication that pricing has been researched or tested with market.

**Research Questions:**
- What's the price sensitivity of each segment?
- What's the perceived value of each service?
- How do your prices compare to competitors?
- What pricing model do customers prefer (fixed vs. day rate vs. value-based)?
- What's the optimal price point for each service?
- How does payment structure affect buying decisions?

**Research Recommendation:**
- Price sensitivity analysis (Van Westendorp method)
- Competitive pricing benchmarking
- Customer interviews about pricing preferences
- A/B test different pricing presentations
- Conjoint analysis to understand price/feature trade-offs

**Impact:** Optimal pricing based on research can increase revenue by 10-30% without reducing volume.

## Recommendations

I'm going to prioritize recommendations differently than other reviewers might. Before adding case studies, testimonials, or more marketing content, you need to build a research foundation. Otherwise, you're just adding unvalidated content to an unvalidated strategy.

### Phase 1: Foundation Research (Weeks 1-8) - MUST DO FIRST

#### 1. Target Market Definition Study (CRITICAL - Week 1-2)
**Why First:** You can't make good decisions about anything else without knowing who you're targeting.

**Research Activities:**
- Analyze your past clients: Who were most profitable? Most enjoyable? Best fit?
- Segment analysis: Identify distinct customer types with different needs
- Market sizing: How big is each potential segment?
- Competitive analysis: Who else serves each segment and how?
- Strategic choice: Which 1-2 segments will you focus on?

**Deliverables:**
- ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) document
- Market segmentation framework
- Target market prioritization with rationale
- TAM/SAM/SOM analysis for each service

**Investment:** £5,000-£10,000 for professional research support
**DIY Alternative:** £0 but 40-60 hours of internal work

**Impact:** This informs every other decision. Don't skip this.

#### 2. Customer Pain Point & Needs Research (CRITICAL - Week 3-4)
**Why Second:** You need to understand what problems you're actually solving.

**Research Activities:**
- 15-20 depth interviews with current customers
- 10-15 interviews with lost opportunities
- 5-10 interviews with prospects who chose competitors
- Pain point discovery: "What's frustrating about [current situation]?"
- Needs assessment: "What would the ideal solution look like?"
- Language capture: Record exact words and phrases customers use

**Deliverables:**
- Top 10 pain points ranked by frequency and intensity
- Customer needs hierarchy
- Voice of customer quotes document
- Jobs-to-be-Done analysis
- Key insight summary

**Investment:** £3,000-£7,000 with external researcher
**DIY Alternative:** £0 but 30-40 hours (can be done by founders/team)

**Impact:** This will transform your messaging from assumptions to customer truth.

#### 3. Persona Development Research (HIGH PRIORITY - Week 5-6)
**Why Third:** Now that you know your target market and their needs, build detailed personas.

**Research Activities:**
- Demographic/firmographic profiling of each persona
- Psychographic research (motivations, values, priorities)
- Decision journey mapping for each persona
- Buying committee analysis (who influences each persona)
- Day-in-the-life research to understand context
- Channel preferences and content consumption habits

**Deliverables:**
- 3-4 detailed research-backed persona documents
- Persona empathy maps
- Decision journey maps for each persona
- Buying committee structures
- Message testing results for each persona

**Investment:** £5,000-£8,000 for professional persona research
**DIY Alternative:** £0 but 40-50 hours of internal work

**Impact:** Research-backed personas enable personalized marketing that converts 2-3x better than generic approaches.

#### 4. Value Proposition Testing (HIGH PRIORITY - Week 7-8)
**Why Fourth:** Test your positioning and messaging with real prospects before finalizing.

**Research Activities:**
- Create 3-4 alternative value propositions
- Test with 30-50 target prospects (online survey + interviews)
- A/B test landing pages with different messaging
- Measure comprehension, differentiation, relevance, credibility
- Iterate based on feedback

**Deliverables:**
- Validated value proposition for each service
- Key message architecture
- Proof points that resonate most
- Messaging hierarchy by persona
- Positioning statement

**Investment:** £4,000-£6,000 for professional testing
**DIY Alternative:** £0 but 20-30 hours using free tools (UsabilityHub, Google Optimize)

**Impact:** Tested messaging can improve conversion rates by 30-100% compared to untested messaging.

### The Fundamental Problem

This website was built using internal opinions about what customers want, not actual research into what customers need. That's backwards. Every dollar spent on this website before doing proper research is a dollar with unknown ROI.

### What Happens Without Research

Based on my 11 years of experience, here's what typically happens to companies that skip market research:

1. **Poor conversion rates** - Generic messaging converts at 1-2% instead of 3-5%
2. **Wrong customer acquisition** - Attract people who aren't good fits
3. **High CAC** - Customer acquisition cost stays high because targeting is poor
4. **Lost competitive deals** - Lose to competitors who understand market better
5. **Inefficient marketing spend** - Money wasted on wrong channels, wrong messages
6. **Slow growth** - Can't scale because positioning isn't clear
7. **Team frustration** - Sales complains leads are bad, marketing complains sales doesn't follow up

### Why This Matters More Than Other Issues

Other reviewers will tell you to add:
- Case studies (yes, needed)
- Testimonials (yes, needed)
- Better content (yes, needed)
- Analytics (yes, needed)

**But here's the truth:** If you add all that content without a research foundation, you'll just be adding more unvalidated content to an unvalidated strategy.

**You'll be optimizing in the wrong direction.**

### The Research-First Approach

Here's what I recommend instead:

**Months 1-2:** Stop all major website changes. Do foundation research.
- Who exactly are you targeting?
- What do they actually need?
- What language do they use?
- How do they make decisions?

**Months 3-4:** Based on research, revise strategy.
- Refine positioning
- Rewrite key messages
- Restructure content
- Create persona journeys

**Months 5+:** Then add tactical elements.
- Case studies (but now you know which ones to highlight)
- Testimonials (but now you know which quotes will resonate)
- Content (but now you know what topics matter)
- Ads (but now you know what messages to test)

**This sequence prevents wasting money on content that doesn't connect.**

Maybe. If you're lucky. If your assumptions happen to be right. If the market happens to match your mental model.

**But why gamble?**

For £20,000-£50,000 in research investment, you could know instead of guess. You could build on evidence instead of assumptions. You could market with confidence instead of hope.

**Stop.** Don't spend another pound on marketing, content, or advertising until you've done the foundation research.

Here's why:
- If your targeting is wrong, all marketing is wasted

- If your positioning isn't differentiated, you'll lose on price

**Invest £30,000-£50,000 in proper market research over the next 3-4 months.** Get clear on who you're targeting, what they need, how they decide, and what messaging resonates.
