# Competitive Review: Solutions Delivered Website
**Reviewer:** Helen Carter, Founder & CEO
**Company:** Competing Professional Services Firm (Business Transformation & Change Management)
**Location:** Birmingham, UK
**Review Date:** November 20, 2025
**Overall Rating:** 5/10

---

## Executive Summary

As the CEO of a competing professional services firm specializing in business transformation and change management, I've conducted a thorough competitive analysis of Solutions Delivered. I'm evaluating them as both a competitive threat and a potential acquisition or partnership target in the UK consultancy market.

**My frank assessment:** Solutions Delivered is not currently a significant competitive threat, but they occupy an interesting niche that could become problematic if they execute properly.

They've positioned themselves at the intersection of web development and business consultancy—a combination that's relatively uncommon in our market. Most firms are either pure tech shops or pure consultancies. This hybrid positioning gives them a unique angle, particularly for clients who need both digital transformation AND the business change support to make it stick.

**Key Competitive Insight:** They're small, deliberately opaque, and appear to be operating as a boutique consultancy rather than pursuing aggressive growth. They're not competing for the same large transformation programs I typically pursue, but they could be stealing smaller engagements and potentially growing into mid-market work over time.

**Market Position:** They're positioned below my firm in terms of engagement size and complexity, but above typical freelancers and small dev shops. They're in the "dangerous middle"—not small enough to ignore, not large enough to compete head-to-head.

**Threat Level:** Currently LOW (3/10), but with potential to become MODERATE (6/10) if they build proof points in accessibility-focused government work or digital transformation space.

## Strengths (Their Competitive Advantages)

### 1. Unique Hybrid Positioning

**What I See:** They combine Laravel web development with business consultancy services (Service Management, Project Management, Business Change). This is genuinely differentiated.

**Why It Matters:** Most competitors are either developers OR consultants. This hybrid lets them own the entire transformation journey—build the system AND help clients through the change. That's powerful for clients who don't want to coordinate multiple vendors.

**Competitive Threat:** This positioning could make them dangerous in digital transformation projects where the technical build is just one component. While I can deliver strategy and change management, I need to partner with developers. They can do both in-house.

**My Assessment:** If they can prove they do both well (big "if"), this is a genuine competitive advantage. Right now it looks like positioning, not proven capability.

### 2. Accessibility-First Approach

**What I See:** WCAG 2.2 compliance is front and center on their website. It's not an add-on—it's part of their core positioning. Trust indicators mention it repeatedly.

**Why It Matters:** Government, NHS, education, and public sector work requires accessibility compliance. Most developers treat this as an afterthought or subcontract it out. If they're building it in from day one, that's a real efficiency and cost advantage.

**Competitive Threat Assessment:** HIGH in public sector tenders where accessibility is weighted heavily. They could underbid competitors who need to retrofit accessibility or hire specialists.

**What Worries Me:** If they start winning accessible web build contracts and documenting case studies, they could own this niche. "The Accessible Digital Transformation Consultancy" is a positioning no one else has clearly claimed.

**Counter Strategy:** We need to add accessibility expertise to our partner network or risk losing public sector digital transformation opportunities.

### 3. No-Bloat Philosophy

**What I See:** Explicit positioning against over-engineered solutions. "Only what you need—nothing more, nothing less." This directly targets client frustration with enterprise software and big consultancy deliverables.

**Why It Matters:** I've lost deals because clients perceive large consultancies as expensive and over-complicated. This "lean and focused" positioning appeals to that segment.

**Competitive Advantage:** Works well for SME clients and organizations burned by previous large consultancy engagements. These clients actively avoid firms like mine.

**Market Segment:** They're positioning for the "fed up with big consultancy" segment. That's a real market, and it's growing.

### 4. Direct Collaboration Model

**What I See:** They emphasize working directly with consultants/developers, no account managers, direct access, 24-hour response times.

**Why It Matters:** This is anti-positioning against my business model. Large consultancies have account management layers, which clients sometimes view as "overhead" that slows things down and adds cost.

**Competitive Positioning:** Smart. They're explicitly saying "We're not like the big firms." This resonates with clients who value access and speed over process and governance.

**Vulnerability They're Exploiting:** The exact complaints I hear in lost deal post-mortems—"too corporate," "too many layers," "slow to respond."

**My Take:** This works until they grow. You can't scale without some structure. But for now, it's a legitimate advantage against firms my size.

### 5. Clear Technology Stack

**What I See:** Laravel-specific development positioning. They're not "we build on any platform"—they're explicit about their technology choice.

**Why It Matters:**
- Attracts clients specifically seeking Laravel expertise
- Attracts Laravel developers looking for project work
- Makes them the obvious choice for Laravel-specific needs
- Builds genuine technical expertise rather than being generalists

**Competitive Threat:** Limited. This narrows their market considerably, but makes them nearly unbeatable in their chosen niche. Classic positioning trade-off.

**My Assessment:** Smart specialization. They'll lose broad-market opportunities but win higher percentages of Laravel opportunities.

### 6. Transparent Process Documentation

**What I See:** The "How We Work" page clearly explains their 4-step engagement process, communication expectations, pricing structure, and working arrangements.

**Why It Matters:** Most consultancies (including mine) hide process details behind sales conversations. This transparency reduces buyer friction and builds trust earlier in the sales cycle.

**Competitive Comparison:** More transparent than my firm's website. We require calls to understand our process, which creates a higher barrier to entry for prospects.

**Market Advantage:** Helps them punch above their weight in early-stage research. Prospects can self-qualify before contact.

### 7. UK-Based Remote-First Model

**What I See:** Clear UK positioning without being location-limited. Remote-first with on-site options.

**Why It Matters:** Post-pandemic, many clients prefer remote consultancy relationships. This positioning works with market preferences while maintaining "UK-based" credibility.

**Competitive Neutrality:** This is table stakes now, not a differentiator. But they've communicated it clearly.

## Specific Issues

### Issue 1: Positioning Confusion

**What I Observe:** Are they a web development shop that does consultancy? Or a consultancy that builds websites? The positioning isn't clear.

**Competitive Implication:** This confusion makes it hard for them to "own" any particular market position. They risk being seen as neither fish nor fowl.

**Recommendation:** Choose primary positioning (I'd suggest "Digital Transformation Consultancy with In-House Development") and make it crystal clear.

### Issue 2: Business Change Service Lacks Credibility

**Problem:** As someone whose entire business is transformation and change management, their Business Change description feels superficial.

**Specific Gaps:**
- No change management methodology mentioned
- No framework or approach articulated
- No measurement or success criteria
- No certifications or qualifications
- No case studies demonstrating change expertise

**Competitive Advantage (Mine):** When we compete for change management work, I have:
- Prosci-certified practitioners
- Documented change frameworks
- 15+ years of transformation experience
- Published case studies with outcomes
- Speaking credentials and thought leadership

**Their Vulnerability:** If they're serious about change management work, they need to significantly strengthen this service area. Currently, it looks like an afterthought.

**Market Impact:** They cannot compete for serious transformation programs without strengthening this positioning.

### Issue 3: No Competitive Differentiation Statement

**What's Missing:** No "Why Choose Us" or "What Makes Us Different" content.

**Why This Matters:** Buyers are comparing multiple vendors. Making the comparison easy (in your favor) is smart business.

**Competitive Implication:** They're making buyers do the work of figuring out how they differ from alternatives. Many won't bother.

**My Advantage:** We clearly articulate what makes us different from big consultancies, boutique firms, and change management freelancers.

### Issue 4: Service Management Vagueness

**Problem:** The Service Management description is generic. As someone who competes in this space, I need to see:
- ITIL alignment (mentioned in schema but not prominent)
- Which ITIL processes they implement
- Tools they work with (ServiceNow, Jira, etc.)
- Certification levels
- Process maturity assessments
- Implementation methodologies

**Competitive Assessment:** This service feels underdeveloped. It reads like they've added it to expand their service menu, not because it's a core competency.

**Market Opportunity:** If they can't articulate service management depth, they can't compete for ITIL implementation projects.

### Issue 5: No Risk Mitigation for Buyers

**What's Missing:** Nothing addresses buyer risk or concerns:
- What if we're not satisfied?
- What are contract terms?
- Are there engagement minimums?
- What's your guarantee or warranty?
- What happens if the project goes wrong?

**Competitive Advantage:** Mature consultancies have policies, guarantees, and risk mitigation processes. Their absence suggests operational immaturity.

**Market Impact:** Risk-averse enterprise buyers need these assurances. Without them, Solutions Delivered appears too risky for larger engagements.

### Issue 6: Scale Ambiguity

**Problem:** I can't determine if they can handle:
- Multiple concurrent projects
- Large team requirements
- Complex multi-phase programs
- Geographic distribution
- Extended timelines

**Competitive Intelligence:** This makes it impossible to assess which opportunities they can actually compete for.

**Their Vulnerability:** If I'm a buyer with a significant program, scale ambiguity is disqualifying. Better to choose a known quantity.

## Recommendations

### For Solutions Delivered (If They Want to Compete Effectively)

#### Priority 1: Choose Your Primary Battle

**Decision Required:** Are you primarily a web development firm that offers consultancy, or a consultancy that builds websites?

**Why This Matters:** Trying to be both equally makes you weak at both. Pick one as primary, position the other as complementary.

**My Recommendation:** Position as "Digital Transformation Consultancy with In-House Development Capability." This makes consultancy primary, development a differentiating capability.

**Market Positioning:** This lets you compete for transformation programs (where margins are higher) with the unique advantage of integrated development.

#### Priority 2: Build Proof Points (CRITICAL)

**Immediate Actions:**
1. Document 5-7 case studies with metrics, outcomes, and client quotes
2. Secure 8-10 client testimonials (anonymous if necessary)
3. Show before/after examples with ROI
4. Demonstrate breadth across services and industries

**Why This Matters:** Without proof, you can't scale beyond referral-based work. You're leaving money on the table and can't compete in any bid situation.

**Competitive Impact:** Case studies would immediately elevate your competitive position from "unknown" to "proven." This is your highest ROI activity.

#### Priority 3: Pick a Vertical or Specialize

**Strategy:** Choose 2-3 industries to focus on and build depth:
- Public sector (leveraging accessibility strength)
- Healthcare (accessibility + digital transformation)
- Professional services (process optimization + web presence)

**Why This Matters:** Specialists beat generalists. Industry-focused positioning makes you the obvious choice for that vertical.

**Competitive Advantage:** Claim "Accessible Digital Transformation for UK Public Sector" and own that space. No one else has that positioning clearly.

#### Priority 4: Strengthen Business Change Credentials

**Required Actions:**
- Get Prosci or Change Management Institute certification
- Develop and document your change framework
- Create change management methodology content
- Build change management case studies
- Add change management thought leadership

**Why This Matters:** If you're serious about competing for business change work, you need credentials. Otherwise, remove it from your services to avoid credibility damage.

**Alternative:** Partner with certified change management practitioners rather than claiming it as core capability.

#### Priority 5: Show Your Team

**Transparency Needed:**
- Key team member bios
- Certifications and qualifications
- Years of experience
- Project backgrounds
- Areas of expertise

**Why This Matters:** B2B buyers buy people, not companies. Show them who they're getting.

**Risk:** Yes, this reveals you're small. But you're already perceived as small. Own it as an advantage ("senior expertise, no junior overhead").

#### Priority 6: Clarify Your Scale and Capacity

**Be Honest About:**
- Team size and structure
- Concurrent project capacity
- Typical engagement size
- Project complexity range
- What's in scope vs. out of scope

**Why This Matters:** Buyers need to know if you can handle their work. Ambiguity leads to disqualification.

**Strategic Positioning:** "We focus on engagements in the £20k-£100k range where senior expertise and direct access matter more than large teams."

#### Priority 7: Build Thought Leadership

**Actions:**
- Start a blog focused on accessible digital transformation
- Publish quarterly insights on digital transformation trends
- Create guides and frameworks
- Speak at conferences
- Build LinkedIn presence
- Contribute to industry discussions

**Why This Matters:** Thought leadership builds authority and generates inbound opportunities. It also makes you visible to competitors (like me), but that's the price of market presence.

#### Priority 8: Join the Professional Ecosystem

**Immediate Actions:**
- Join relevant professional bodies (BCS, APM, Change Management Institute)
- Become a Laravel Partner
- Establish technology partnerships
- Join industry associations
- Display credentials prominently

**Why This Matters:** Partnerships provide credibility, referrals, and ecosystem benefits. Operating independently limits your reach.

### For My Firm (Competitive Response Strategy)

#### Monitor But Don't Prioritize

**Assessment:** Solutions Delivered is not currently a significant threat to my core business (large transformation programs, enterprise change management).

**Monitoring Cadence:** Quarterly website reviews to check for:
- Case study additions
- Team expansion
- Service positioning changes
- Public sector wins
- Thought leadership emergence

#### Address Accessibility Gap

**Action Required:** Their accessibility positioning exposes a gap in my offering. I need to:
- Add accessibility expertise to partner network

- Position for accessible digital transformation opportunities
- Counter their advantage in public sector space

#### Protect Against "Anti-Big-Consultancy" Positioning

**Strategy:** Acknowledge that some clients prefer boutique firms, position our value differently:
- Governance and structure as risk mitigation
- Account management as dedicated support
- Team depth as capability assurance
- Proven methodology as efficiency

**Market Segment:** Accept that "burned by big consultancy" clients aren't our target. Focus on clients who value structure and scale.

#### Consider Partnership Rather Than Competition

**Opportunity:** Their development capability + our change management expertise could be complementary.

**Potential Value:**
- Refer web development work to them
- Partner on digital transformation programs
- Co-deliver integrated solutions
- Potential acquisition target if they build value

**Next Step:** Monitor their growth and success. If they build strong proof points and client base, explore partnership conversations.

## Final Thoughts

As a competing CEO, here's what I think is really going on with Solutions Delivered:

**The Truth:** They're almost certainly a 1-3 person operation running a boutique consultancy. They've built a professional web presence and positioned themselves reasonably well, but they haven't invested in the proof points and market presence needed to scale beyond referral-based work.

**The Smart Moves They've Made:**
1. Combining web development with consultancy services is genuinely differentiated
2. Accessibility-first positioning could be a powerful niche
3. Anti-big-consultancy messaging resonates with a real market segment
4. Process transparency reduces sales friction
5. Laravel specialization creates a focused position

**The Critical Mistakes:**
1. No proof of capability = can't scale beyond referrals
2. Hiding team size/credentials = appears untrustworthy or too small
3. Four services without depth = jack-of-all-trades perception
4. No market presence = invisible to buyers and competitors
5. Generic business change positioning = can't compete with specialists

**My Honest Assessment as a Competitor:**

**Should I worry about them?** Not really. They're not competing for the transformation programs that drive my revenue. They're playing in a different segment.

**Could they become a threat?** Yes, but only if they:
- Build strong proof points in accessible digital transformation
- Claim and own the public sector accessibility niche
- Add serious change management credentials
- Start winning and documenting mid-market successes

**Would I want to acquire them?** Maybe, but only if they build client relationships and recurring revenue. Right now, there's nothing to buy except their positioning (which I could replicate) and their website (which isn't that hard to build).

**Would I want to partner with them?** Potentially. If they're genuinely good at accessible Laravel development, that could complement my change management work. But I'd need to see proof of capability first.

**The Bigger Picture:**

Solutions Delivered represents a common pattern in the UK consultancy market: smart positioning, professional presentation, but insufficient proof and scale to break through to the next level. They're stuck in the "boutique consultancy gap"—too big to be a freelancer, too small to compete with established firms, without the proof points to justify premium positioning.

They have two paths forward:

**Path 1 (Lifestyle Business):** Stay small, stay boutique, work on referrals, maintain good margins, don't try to scale. This is perfectly viable and probably what they're doing.

**Path 2 (Growth Business):** Document everything, build proof, specialize hard on accessibility + public sector, invest in thought leadership, hire to scale, and become "the" accessible digital transformation firm. This requires significant investment and risk.

Right now, they're stuck between these paths—presenting like they want to be Path 2 (professional website, four services, growth positioning) while operating like Path 1 (no proof, no team visibility, no market presence).

**My Advice to Them (If They Care What a Competitor Thinks):**

Pick Path 1 or Path 2. You can't straddle both.

If Path 1: Own being boutique. "Senior expertise, no overhead, direct access" positioning. Remove services you don't genuinely specialize in. Focus on what you do best.

If Path 2: Document obsessively, specialize aggressively (I'd pick accessible digital transformation for UK public sector), build proof, hire, invest in market presence, and compete properly.

Right now you're invisible to me as a competitor and likely invisible to many buyers. That's safe but limiting.

**The Bottom Line:**

From my competitive perspective, Solutions Delivered is currently not a threat. But the foundations for becoming one are there. If they execute on their accessibility positioning and build proof points in public sector work, they could carve out a niche that's difficult for larger firms like mine to compete in.

I'll monitor them quarterly, but I'm not changing my strategy based on their current market position.

**Competitive Threat Level: LOW (Current) / MODERATE (Potential)**
