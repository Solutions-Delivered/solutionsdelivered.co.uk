# Sales Representative Review: Solutions Delivered Website
**Reviewer:** Daniel Foster, Senior Sales Executive
**Review Date:** November 20, 2025
**Overall Rating:** 4/10

---

## Executive Summary

I'm Daniel Foster, and I've spent the last 9 years on the front lines of B2B sales, having thousands of conversations with prospects, handling objections, and closing deals. I've reviewed the Solutions Delivered website from the perspective of someone who needs practical tools to educate prospects, overcome objections, and accelerate the sales process.

Here's the reality: this website is professionally designed and clearly explains what Solutions Delivered does, but it gives me almost nothing to work with when I'm in an actual sales conversation. I can't point prospects to case studies that prove our value. I can't send them comparison guides to help them understand why we're different. I can't share ROI calculators to build a business case. I don't have testimonials to reinforce trust.

**The Bottom Line:** This website might generate leads, but it doesn't help me close them. It's like sending me into battle without ammunition.

When a prospect asks "Why should we choose you?" or "Can you show me similar projects you've completed?" or "How do I justify this investment to my board?" - I have nowhere to point them on this website. That's a serious problem.

---

## Strengths

### 1. Clear Service Descriptions
I appreciate that I can quickly explain what we offer. The four core services (Web Development, Service Management, Project Management, Business Change) are well-articulated. When prospects ask "What exactly do you do?" I can confidently point them to the Solutions page.

The descriptions are clear enough that prospects understand the offering without needing extensive explanation from me.

### 2. Transparent Process
The "How We Work" page is genuinely helpful. When prospects ask "What's your process?" I can send them this link. The four-step engagement process sets clear expectations and reduces uncertainty.

This transparency builds trust early in the conversation, which is valuable. It answers the "What happens next?" question that prospects always have.

### 3. Accessibility as a Differentiator
The WCAG 2.2 compliance and "no-bloat philosophy" messages give me talking points for differentiation. In an increasingly compliance-focused market, this matters.

I can use this when competing against firms that don't prioritize accessibility. It's a genuine competitive advantage, though I wish we had more proof points around it.

### 4. Professional Presentation
The website looks professional and modern. I'm not embarrassed to send prospects here. The design quality reflects well on our capabilities, especially for web development prospects who will judge us by our own site.

### 5. Response Time Commitment
The 24-hour response time commitment is excellent for lead nurturing. It gives me a specific promise to make during sales conversations: "Fill out this form and we'll respond within 24 hours."

This helps me maintain momentum when prospects are ready to move forward.

### 6. Direct Collaboration Messaging
The emphasis on working directly with the team (not through account managers or layers of bureaucracy) is a strong selling point. SME buyers appreciate this.

I use this to differentiate us from larger consultancies. It resonates particularly well with prospects who've had bad experiences with agency bloat.

## Weaknesses

### 1. Zero Case Studies or Success Stories
**Critical Problem for Sales**

This is my biggest frustration. I literally have zero case studies to share with prospects. None. Not one example of:
- A problem we've solved
- A client we've helped
- Results we've delivered
- Outcomes we've achieved

Every single sales conversation includes the question "Can you show me examples of similar work?" My answer has to be "Let me create something custom for you" rather than "Here are three relevant case studies."

**Impact on My Sales Process:**
- I lose credibility when I can't provide social proof
- Prospects question whether we've actually done this before
- I can't demonstrate pattern matching ("You're like Company X, here's what we did for them")
- Competitive disadvantage - other firms show case studies, we don't
- Longer sales cycles because prospects need more convincing

**What I Need:**
- 5-10 detailed case studies across different services
- Before/after scenarios showing transformation
- Specific metrics and outcomes (time saved, cost reduced, efficiency gained)
- Client testimonials embedded in the case studies
- Industry-specific examples I can match to prospect situations

This is costing me deals. I guarantee it.

### 2. No Testimonials or Social Proof
**Critical Problem for Sales**

Beyond case studies, there are no testimonials anywhere. No client quotes. No video testimonials. No reviews. Nothing.

When prospects are on the fence, social proof tips the scales. I need to be able to say "Here's what our clients say about working with us" and point to credible third-party validation.

**What I Need:**
- Written testimonials with client names and companies (where possible)
- Video testimonials (even short 30-second clips)
- Client logos showing brands we've worked with
- Aggregate ratings or satisfaction scores
- LinkedIn recommendations I can reference

### 3. No ROI Tools or Business Case Support
**Major Problem for Sales**

Many of my prospects need to justify the investment to someone else - their board, their CFO, their leadership team. They ask me "How do I build the business case for this?"

I have nothing to give them. No:
- ROI calculator
- Business case template
- Cost-benefit analysis framework
- Payback period estimator
- Value realization timeline

**Impact:**
- Prospects struggle to get internal buy-in
- Deals stall in approval stages
- I can't help champions sell internally
- Longer sales cycles
- Lost deals due to inability to justify investment

**What I Need:**
- Simple ROI calculator for each service line
- Downloadable business case template
- One-pagers showing typical ROI timelines
- Cost of inaction calculators
- Templates prospects can customize for their executives

### 4. No Competitive Comparison Content
**Major Problem for Sales**

Prospects always ask "Why should we choose you over [Competitor X]?" or "How are you different from other consultancies?"

I can articulate this verbally, but I have no content to support my claims. No comparison guides, no battlecards, no differentiation documents.

**What I Need:**
- "Why Choose Us" page with clear differentiation
- Comparison guides (us vs. large agencies, us vs. freelancers, us vs. off-the-shelf solutions)
- Unique methodology or framework documentation
- Proprietary tools or approaches showcased
- Team credentials and expertise highlighted

Without this, every sales conversation requires me to manually explain differentiation rather than reinforcing with supporting content.

### 5. No Objection-Handling Content
**Major Problem for Sales**

I face the same objections repeatedly:
- "Your competitors are cheaper"
- "Why shouldn't we just use WordPress/Wix?"
- "Can't our internal team do this?"
- "What if it doesn't work out?"
- "How do you handle scope changes?"
- "We've been burned by consultants before"

The website doesn't address any of these proactively. I'm handling every objection from scratch rather than pointing prospects to well-crafted responses.

**What I Need:**
- Comprehensive FAQ addressing common concerns
- "Myth vs. Reality" content for each service
- Risk reversal statements
- Guarantees or satisfaction policies documented
- Comparison content addressing "Why not DIY?" questions

### 6. No Downloadable Sales Resources
**Major Problem for Sales**

I have nothing to send prospects during the nurture phase. No:
- Service overview PDFs
- One-pagers for each offering
- Capability statements
- Portfolio documents
- Pricing guides
- Process documents

When a prospect says "Send me something I can review" or "Can you give me materials to share with my team?" I'm creating everything from scratch.

**Impact:**
- Inconsistent messaging across prospects
- Time wasted creating custom materials
- Missed opportunities when prospects want immediate resources
- Unprofessional appearance vs. competitors with polished materials

**What I Need:**
- Professional one-pagers for each service
- PDF capability statement
- Downloadable process guides
- Pricing overview documents
- Email templates I can customize

### 7. No Pricing Information
**Significant Problem for Sales**

The website mentions "fixed-price" and "day-rate" options but gives zero pricing guidance. This creates problems:

**Early Stage:**
- Prospects can't self-qualify on budget
- I waste time on prospects who can't afford us
- Or I lose prospects who think we're too expensive (when we might not be)

**Mid-to-Late Stage:**
- "What's this going to cost?" is always the first real question
- I have no framework to reference
- Competitive disadvantage if competitors show ranges

**What I Need:**
- Budget ranges for typical projects
- Starting prices or day rates
- Package options with ballpark pricing
- "Projects typically range from X to Y" guidance
- Pricing factors explained clearly

### 8. No Industry-Specific Content
**Moderate Problem for Sales**

Every prospect wants to know "Have you worked in our industry?" The website treats all industries identically.

I can't point healthcare prospects to healthcare case studies. Can't show retail clients retail examples. Can't demonstrate financial services expertise.

**What I Need:**
- Industry-specific landing pages or sections
- Vertical case studies
- Industry-specific challenges addressed
- Sector expertise highlighted

### 9. Missing Lead Magnets for Nurture
**Moderate Problem for Sales**

Not every prospect is ready to buy immediately. Many need nurturing over weeks or months. But I have nothing to keep them engaged:
- No whitepapers to share
- No guides or ebooks
- No webinars to invite them to

**Impact:**

**What I Need:**
- Downloadable guides for each service area
- Email newsletter I can subscribe prospects to
- Free assessment or audit offers
- Educational webinars or workshops
- Checklists, templates, tools

### 10. Limited Contact Options
**Moderate Problem for Sales**

There's only a contact form. Some prospects prefer to:
- Call directly
- Book a meeting immediately
- Message via chat
- Connect on LinkedIn

Missing these options means we lose prospects who won't fill out forms.

**What I Need:**
- Phone number displayed (even if for callbacks)
- Meeting scheduler integration (Calendly or similar)
- Live chat for quick questions
- LinkedIn profile links
- Multiple pathways to engagement

### 11. No Demo or Proof of Capability
**Moderate Problem for Sales**

For web development specifically, prospects want to see examples of our work. But the website shows no:
- Portfolio of sites we've built
- Live demos
- Before/after examples
- Code quality demonstrations
- Technical capability proof

**What I Need:**
- Portfolio page with client work examples
- Demo environments or prototypes
- GitHub profile or code samples
- Technical case studies
- Awards or recognition

### 12. Weak CTAs and Next Steps
**Moderate Problem for Sales**

Every CTA says "Get Started" or "Contact Us." But different prospects need different next steps:

- Some want pricing information
- Some want to download a guide
- Some want to book a consultation
- Some want to see case studies
- Some want to schedule a demo

One generic CTA doesn't serve these different needs.

**What I Need:**
- Multiple CTA options based on buyer stage
- "Download Guide," "View Pricing," "See Examples," "Book Consultation"
- Progressive engagement paths
- Clear value proposition for each CTA

## Specific Issues

### Homepage
**Sales Enablement Gap:**
The homepage looks professional but offers no sales support. When I send prospects here, they see:
- What we do (good)
- Our approach (good)
- CTAs to contact us (good)

But they don't see:
- Proof we've done this successfully
- Social validation from other clients
- Specific outcomes or metrics
- Reasons to choose us over competitors
- Resources to learn more

**Recommendation:** Add:
- Client testimonials or logos in hero section
- Success metrics: "100+ projects delivered, 95% client satisfaction"
- Featured case study section
- "As featured in" or "Certified by" trust badges
- Secondary CTAs for researchers: "Download Our Capability Statement"

### Solutions Page
**Sales Enablement Gap:**
Each service is well-described, but I can't use this page to close deals because it's missing:
- Case studies for each service
- Typical outcomes and ROI
- Pricing guidance
- Who this is for / who this isn't for
- FAQs addressing common objections
- Client testimonials specific to each service

**Recommendation:** Enhance each service section with:
- "Success Story: How we helped [Client Type]"
- "Typical Results: X% efficiency improvement, Y% cost reduction"
- "Best Suited For: Organizations with [characteristics]"
- "Investment Range: Projects typically £X-£Y"
- "Common Questions" section

### How We Work Page
**Sales Enablement Gap:**
This page is actually quite good for explaining process, but it could better support sales by adding:
- What happens if we're not a good fit?
- How do you handle scope changes?
- What if we're not satisfied?
- Testimonials about the working relationship
- Case study showing the process in action

**Recommendation:**
- Add "When We're NOT a Good Fit" section (builds trust)
- Include process testimonials
- Add FAQ section addressing process concerns
- Show example timeline with milestones

### Get Started Page (Contact Form)
**Sales Enablement Gap:**
The form is simple (good for conversion) but provides no qualification or segmentation:
- Doesn't ask which service they're interested in
- Doesn't ask about timeline or urgency
- Doesn't ask about budget range
- Doesn't offer different engagement options

**Recommendation:**
- Add "Which service interests you?" dropdown
- Add "When are you looking to start?" field
- Add "What's your budget range?" (optional)
- Offer alternative engagement: "Not ready yet? Download our guide"

### About Page
**Sales Enablement Gap:**
The About page explains values and mission but doesn't build credibility or trust through:
- Team credentials and bios
- Years of experience or project count
- Certifications and professional memberships
- Company history and milestones
- Awards or recognition

**Recommendation:** Add:
- Team profiles with credentials
- "Founded in 20XX, delivered 100+ projects"
- Professional certifications (PRINCE2, Agile, etc.)
- Industry affiliations
- Client retention metrics

I want to be direct: I can't effectively sell without ammunition. This website is like sending a soldier into battle unarmed.

That's not a marketing problem. That's a sales problem.

**My Top 3 Urgent Needs:**
1. Case studies (5-10 detailed examples across services)
2. Testimonials (written and video from satisfied clients)
