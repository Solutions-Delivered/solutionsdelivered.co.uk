# Recruitment Manager Review - Solutions Delivered Website

**Reviewer:** Karen Thompson, Head of People & Talent
**Date:** 2025-11-20
**Review Type:** Comprehensive Recruitment & Employer Branding Audit

---

## Executive Summary

As a recruitment professional with 15 years of experience in talent acquisition and employer branding, I've reviewed the Solutions Delivered website through the lens of talent attraction, candidate experience, and recruitment marketing. While the careers page exists and touches on important themes, it fundamentally fails to function as an effective recruitment tool.

**The Bottom Line:** This careers page is a missed opportunity. In today's competitive talent market, your careers page is your first interview with candidates. This one doesn't make the case for why talented professionals should choose Solutions Delivered over competitors. It's generic, lacks authenticity, provides minimal practical information, and offers no way to actually apply for roles.

**Quick Stats:**
- **Job Listings:** 0 (no current openings displayed)
- **Application Process:** Contact form only (not recruitment-specific)
- **Employee Testimonials:** 0
- **Culture Content:** Generic statements, no authentic proof
- **Real Employee Photos:** 0 (no team visible)
- **Benefits Details:** Vague mentions, no specifics
- **Salary Information:** None
- **Career Paths:** Generic mention, no details
- **DEI Content:** 0
- **ATS Integration:** None visible
- **Video Content:** 0

## Strengths

### 1. Careers Page Exists
At least there IS a dedicated careers page, which is more than some small consultancies have. It's accessible from the main navigation, which means candidates can find it easily. The page is at `/careers` which is a standard, logical URL structure.

### 2. Good Visual Hierarchy and Readability
The page follows the same clean, professional design as the rest of the site. Text is readable, sections are well-organized with clear headings, and the layout is logical (Why Join → What We Look For → Opportunities → CTA). This makes it easy for candidates to scan quickly.

### 3. Addresses Key "Why Join" Themes
The page attempts to cover important recruitment themes:
- Challenging work
- Continuous learning
- Collaborative culture
- Meaningful impact
- Work-life balance
- Career growth

These are the right topics to address. The problem is execution, not intent.

### 4. "What We Look For" Section
Having a section that explicitly states what you're looking for in candidates is helpful. It sets expectations and helps candidates self-select. The four criteria listed (Experience and Expertise, Problem-Solving Skills, Communication Excellence, Collaborative Mindset) are appropriate for a consultancy.

### 5. Accessibility Compliant
Like the rest of the site, the careers page appears to be WCAG 2.2 compliant. This matters for accessibility but also signals attention to detail and inclusivity—qualities that matter to candidates, especially those with disabilities.

### 6. Mobile Responsive
The page works on mobile devices, which is critical since many candidates browse job opportunities on their phones during commutes or lunch breaks.

### 7. Call-to-Action Present
There IS a clear CTA to get in touch, with multiple touchpoints throughout the page. The "Get in Touch" and "Contact Us" buttons are prominent and consistently styled.

## Weaknesses

### 1. **CRITICAL: Zero Authenticity**
This is my biggest concern. Every single piece of content on this page could have been written by ChatGPT for a generic consultancy. There is:
- No real employee stories or quotes
- No specific examples of actual projects or challenges
- No authentic culture content
- No personality or unique voice
- No photos of actual team members
- No video content showing real people

**Why this matters:** Candidates can smell generic content from a mile away. Everyone claims to offer "challenging work," "collaborative culture," and "work-life balance." Without authentic proof, these are just empty promises. In 2025, candidates research companies thoroughly—they're looking for EVIDENCE, not marketing speak.

**The problem:** Stock graphics and generic bullet points don't differentiate you from the hundreds of other consultancies making identical claims. You need to SHOW your culture, not just tell people about it.

### 2. **CRITICAL: No Job Listings or Application Process**
The "Current Opportunities" section says "We're always looking for talented individuals" but shows ZERO actual job listings. This is a fundamental failure for a careers page.

**Problems with this approach:**
- **No specific roles to apply for** - Candidates need concrete positions, not vague "we're hiring" statements
- **Forces speculative applications** - This adds friction and reduces application volume
- **No role-specific information** - Candidates can't assess if they're qualified
- **No salary ranges** - This wastes time for candidates outside your budget range
- **No clear application path** - Clicking "Get in Touch" goes to a generic contact form that asks for "Name, Email, Company, Message"

**Why this matters:** Even if you don't have current openings, you should still list the TYPES of roles you typically hire for with "Join our talent pool" functionality. Candidates searching for specific positions (e.g., "Senior Laravel Developer" or "ITIL Consultant") won't find you because you don't list those roles.

### 3. **CRITICAL: No Employee Testimonials or Stories**
There are zero quotes, stories, or testimonials from actual employees. This is a huge missed opportunity.

**What's missing:**
- Employee testimonials with photos and full names
- Career journey stories ("How I grew from consultant to senior consultant to team lead")
- Day-in-the-life content
- Onboarding experiences
- Team member profiles

**Why this matters:** "Your careers page is your first interview. Make it count." (That's MY quote, by the way.) Candidates want to hear from real people who work there NOW. They want to know: What's it actually like? Will I fit in? Are people happy? Do they stay long-term?

### 4. No Benefits Detail
The page mentions:
- "Work-Life Balance" - Flexible working arrangements
- "Career Growth" - Clear career progression paths

But provides ZERO specifics:
- What's the actual flexibility? (Remote? Hybrid? Fully flexible hours?)
- What's the holiday allowance?
- What about pension contributions?
- Private health insurance?
- Professional development budget?
- Conference attendance?
- Certification support?
- Parental leave policies?
- Wellness programs?
- Equipment provided?
- Home office stipend?

**Why this matters:** Benefits are a major deciding factor, especially for senior candidates comparing multiple offers. Being vague makes candidates assume you're hiding below-market compensation or benefits.

### 5. No Team Visibility
There are no photos of the team anywhere on the site (not just careers page—I checked About page too). No names, no faces, no bios, no LinkedIn links.

**Why this matters:** People join teams, not companies. Candidates want to know who they'll be working with. They'll Google your team anyway—might as well control the narrative. Plus, showing diverse team members supports DEI goals (which you're not addressing at all).

### 6. Zero DEI (Diversity, Equity, Inclusion) Content
There is no mention of:
- Diversity and inclusion commitments
- Equal opportunity employer statement
- Accessibility accommodations during hiring
- Diverse representation in leadership
- Employee resource groups
- DEI initiatives or progress

**Why this matters:** In 2025, DEI isn't optional—it's table stakes, especially for younger talent. Not addressing it signals either:
1. You don't value diversity (red flag for diverse candidates)
2. You haven't thought about it (suggests immature company culture)

Either way, you're losing candidates from underrepresented groups who will choose competitors with visible DEI commitments.

### 7. No Career Path Information
The page mentions "Clear career progression paths" but doesn't explain what that means:
- What are the levels? (Junior → Mid → Senior → Principal → Team Lead?)
- What's the typical timeline for progression?
- What determines promotions?
- Are there specialist vs. management tracks?
- What does a career at Solutions Delivered look like over 3-5 years?

**Why this matters:** Career-minded candidates (your best hires) are thinking long-term. They want to know there's a future, not just a job.

### 8. No Salary or Compensation Information
Not even ranges. I understand some companies avoid this, but transparency is increasingly expected:
- 72% of candidates consider salary range in job descriptions important (LinkedIn 2024 data)
- Pay transparency laws are expanding (UK started requiring some disclosures in 2024)
- Listing ranges attracts more qualified applicants and reduces time-wasters

**Why this matters:** Without salary information, you're filtering OUT confident, well-paid candidates (who won't bother applying if they think you're below market) and attracting more applications from underqualified or desperate candidates (who will apply to anything).

### 9. No Culture-Defining Content
The page lists generic values but doesn't demonstrate actual culture:
- What's a typical team meeting like?
- How do you celebrate wins?
- What do you do for team building?
- How do you handle failure/learning?
- What's the decision-making process?
- What's the communication style?
- What's the work environment? (Office? Remote? Hybrid?)
- What tools do you use?

**Why this matters:** Culture fit is the #1 predictor of long-term retention. Candidates self-select based on culture signals. Right now, you're giving them nothing to work with, so you'll get applications from people who may not fit at all.

### 10. No Onboarding or First-90-Days Information
What happens after someone accepts an offer? Candidates wonder:
- What's onboarding like?
- How long until I'm productive?
- Who will mentor me?
- What training is provided?
- What support is available?

**Why this matters:** The unknown is scary. Reducing uncertainty about the joining process reduces offer acceptance anxiety and improves close rates.

### 11. No Location or Remote Work Information
The About page says "UK-based" but:
- Where's the office?
- Is there an office?
- Can people work remotely?
- Is it hybrid?
- If remote, can you hire anywhere in the UK?
- International candidates allowed?

**Why this matters:** Location and flexibility are TOP candidate priorities post-pandemic. Many candidates filter jobs by location/remote options FIRST.

### 12. No ATS Integration or Talent Pool Management
There's no applicant tracking system integration visible. This means:
- No way to track candidate sources
- No structured application pipeline
- Likely manual email management (inefficient)
- No candidate nurturing capability
- No talent pool for future openings
- Poor candidate experience with inconsistent follow-up

**Why this matters:** "Every friction point in the application process loses good candidates to competitors." (Another one of my quotes.) A clunky, generic contact form instead of a proper application portal signals amateur-hour recruitment practices.

### 13. Missing Recruitment Marketing Elements
No:
- Employee referral program mentioned
- "Join our talent network" functionality
- Newsletter for career updates
- LinkedIn company page link
- Glassdoor/Indeed reviews linked or displayed
- Recruitment event mentions
- Graduate program information
- Internship opportunities

**Why this matters:** Modern recruitment is marketing. You need multiple touchpoints and nurturing mechanisms for passive candidates who aren't ready to apply today but might be in 6 months.

## Specific Issues

### Careers Page Header Section

**Good:**
- Clear headline: "Join Our Team at Solutions Delivered"
- Subheading explains the opportunity: "Make a real impact on businesses across the UK while growing your career"
- Consistent visual design with rest of site

**Issues:**
1. No employee photo or video in header - missed opportunity for authentic connection
2. Subheading is generic - could apply to any consultancy
3. No trust indicators specific to employment (e.g., "4.8/5 Glassdoor rating" or "Best Places to Work 2024")

### "Why Join Solutions Delivered?" Section

This section has the right structure (6 benefits with icons) but fails in execution:

**1. Challenging Work**
- **What it says:** "Work on diverse projects across multiple industries, solving complex business challenges every day."
- **Problem:** Generic. EVERY consultancy says this.
- **What it should say:** Specific example like "Recent project: Led the digital transformation for a £50M healthcare provider, working directly with C-suite" or quote from employee: "I've worked in retail, healthcare, and finance all in my first year—I never get bored."

**2. Continuous Learning**
- **What it says:** "Access to training, certifications, and professional development opportunities to grow your skills."
- **Problem:** Vague. What training? What budget?
- **What it should say:** "£2,000 annual training budget per employee. We've sent team members to Laravel Live UK, AWS re:Invent, and Prince2 certification courses. 100% of training costs covered."

**3. Collaborative Culture**
- **What it says:** "Work with a talented team of professionals who value collaboration and knowledge sharing."
- **Problem:** Meaningless without evidence.
- **What it should say:** "Weekly knowledge-sharing sessions where team members present new techniques. Pair programming on complex problems. Open Slack channel where anyone can ask anyone anything. See what Sarah, our Lead Developer, says about the team..." [testimonial]

**4. Meaningful Impact**
- **What it says:** "See the direct impact of your work as you help organizations transform and succeed."
- **Problem:** Vague and unmeasurable.
- **What it should say:** "Our consultants have helped clients save £2M+ in operational costs, reduce process time by 60%, and achieve digital transformation 3 months ahead of schedule. You'll present results directly to client leadership teams."

**5. Work-Life Balance**
- **What it says:** "Flexible working arrangements and a culture that respects personal time and well-being."
- **Problem:** What does "flexible" actually mean?
- **What it should say:** "Hybrid working (2 days in office if you want, full remote if you prefer). Flexible core hours (9am-3pm). No expectation to check emails outside business hours. 25 days holiday plus bank holidays, plus your birthday off."

**6. Career Growth**
- **What it says:** "Clear career progression paths with opportunities to advance and take on leadership roles."
- **Problem:** No evidence this is real.
- **What it should say:** "Average promotion timeline: 18-24 months. 40% of our senior consultants started as junior consultants. See Jake's journey from Graduate Consultant to Team Lead in 3 years."

### "What We Look For" Section

This section is better—more specific—but still has issues:

**Good:**
- Four clear criteria
- Explains reasoning for each

**Issues:**
1. No mention of values fit or culture fit
2. Missing technical skills/qualifications that would help candidates self-assess
3. No "nice to haves" vs. "must haves" distinction
4. Doesn't address different seniority levels (are these for junior or senior roles?)

**Suggestion:** This should be role-specific. A Senior Laravel Developer and a Business Change Consultant need different skills. Generic criteria don't help anyone.

### "Current Opportunities" Section

**Critical Problems:**

1. **No job listings** - This section is essentially useless without actual roles listed

2. **Copy is weak:**
   - "We're always looking for talented individuals" - too vague
   - "Even if we don't have an open position that matches your skills right now" - this is a NEGATIVE frame. Never lead with "we might not have anything for you"

3. **CTA goes to generic contact form** - Clicking "Get in Touch" doesn't go to a careers-specific application form, just the standard business contact form

4. **No talent pool signup** - Should be collecting resumes/CVs for future roles

5. **No role suggestions** - Should at least say "We typically hire for: Senior Consultants, Project Managers, Laravel Developers, Business Analysts" so candidates know what to expect

**What this section SHOULD include:**
- List of current openings (even if it's zero, say "No current openings - check back soon or join our talent network")
- If you have roles: Job title, location, salary range, brief description, "Apply Now" button for each
- If no current roles: "Join our Talent Network" form collecting: Name, Email, Role Interest, Location Preference, Resume/CV upload, LinkedIn profile
- Expected hiring timeline: "We typically hire in Q1 and Q3"
- Referral program: "Know someone great? Refer them and earn £500 if they're hired"

### Final Call-to-Action Section

**Good:**
- Visually prominent with gradient background
- Clear headline: "Ready to Make a Difference?"
- CTA button is visible

**Issues:**
1. Copy is still generic: "Start your journey with Solutions Delivered today"
2. Trust indicator at bottom ("We'd love to hear from you • Flexible working • Career growth opportunities") is good, but could be stronger with specific proof
3. No alternative actions (follow on LinkedIn, join newsletter, join talent pool)
4. Only one CTA - should offer options for different readiness levels

## Recommendations

### Immediate Priority (Next 30 Days)

#### 1. Add Real Employee Testimonials and Photos (CRITICAL)
**Why:** Authenticity is non-negotiable in 2025 recruitment marketing.

**Actions:**
- Interview 3-5 current employees (different roles, tenures, backgrounds)
- Get quotes about: Why they joined, what surprised them, what they love, career growth they've experienced
- Take professional (or even good iPhone) photos of each person
- Include full names and actual job titles
- Add to careers page with prominent placement
- Bonus: Create short video testimonials (30-60 seconds each)

**Estimated Impact:** 25-35% increase in application quality. Candidates will feel more confident applying.

#### 2. Create Specific Job Role Templates (CRITICAL)
**Why:** Even without current openings, you need to show what roles you hire for.

**Actions:**
- Create 4-6 "typical role" descriptions:
  - Senior Consultant - Service Management
  - Project Manager
  - Senior Laravel Developer
  - Business Analyst
  - Junior Consultant (Graduate Program)
- For each, include:
  - Full job description
  - Required vs. preferred qualifications
  - Salary range (or at least indicative level: £40-60k)
  - Day-in-the-life content
  - Growth path from this role
- Add "Currently recruiting" toggle so you can show/hide as needed
- If not actively hiring, label as "Join Talent Pool for Future Openings"

**Estimated Impact:** 100-200% increase in relevant applications. Candidates can self-assess fit.

#### 3. Add Detailed Benefits Information
**Why:** Benefits are a major decision factor and lack of transparency raises red flags.

**Actions:**
- Create comprehensive benefits section including:
  - Holiday allowance (specific days)
  - Pension contribution (specific %)
  - Health insurance details
  - Training budget (specific £ amount)
  - Certification support specifics
  - Equipment provided
  - Remote work policy (detailed)
  - Flexible hours explanation
  - Parental leave policy
  - Sick leave policy
  - Wellness benefits
- Use visual presentation (icons, cards, or table)
- Compare to market if you're competitive

**Estimated Impact:** 20-30% increase in senior candidate applications. Reduces compensation questions during recruitment.

#### 4. Implement Proper Application Process
**Why:** A generic contact form screams "we're not serious about hiring."

**Actions:**
- Create careers-specific form with fields:
  - Full name
  - Email
  - Phone
  - Location/Willing to relocate
  - Role interest (dropdown)
  - Resume/CV upload
  - Cover letter (optional)
  - LinkedIn profile
  - How did you hear about us?
  - Earliest start date
  - Salary expectations
- Set up auto-responder: "Thanks for applying. We review all applications within 5 business days. Here's what to expect next..."
- Create "Join Talent Pool" alternative form for when no specific roles match
- Consider calendar booking link for "Exploratory Chat" option

**Estimated Impact:** 40-50% improvement in candidate experience. Signals professionalism.

### High Priority (60-90 Days)

#### 5. Build Team Visibility Content
**Why:** People join teams, not companies.

**Actions:**
- Create team page with:
  - Photos and bios of all team members
  - Names, roles, tenure
  - Brief backgrounds (previous companies, specializations)
  - Fun facts or personal interests
  - LinkedIn profile links
- Add leadership team section to About page
- Include team photos in Careers page
- Create "Day in the Life" content for different roles
- Film or write about team events, celebrations, working sessions

**Estimated Impact:** 30-40% increase in candidate engagement. Humanizes the company.

#### 6. Develop DEI Content and Commitments
**Why:** DEI is table stakes for modern talent acquisition.

**Actions:**
- Draft clear DEI statement:
  - Commitment to equal opportunity
  - Specific initiatives (diverse interview panels, unbiased job descriptions)
  - Current diversity metrics (if you can share them)
  - Goals for improvement
- Add to Careers page prominently
- Include "Equal Opportunity Employer" statement in all job postings
- Describe accessibility accommodations available during interview process
- Highlight any employee resource groups or diversity initiatives
- Show diverse representation in team photos (authentic, not staged)

**Estimated Impact:** 20-30% increase in applications from underrepresented groups. Protects against discrimination claims.

#### 7. Create Career Path Visualizations
**Why:** Career-minded candidates need to see the future.

**Actions:**
- Map out typical career progressions for each track:
  - IC (Individual Contributor) Track: Junior → Mid-Level → Senior → Principal → Distinguished Consultant
  - Management Track: Consultant → Senior Consultant → Team Lead → Head of Practice → Director
- Create visual diagrams showing paths
- Include typical timelines (e.g., "Junior to Mid-Level: 18-24 months with strong performance")
- Describe what determines promotion (skills, impact, client feedback)
- Include example career stories: "Sarah joined as a Junior Consultant in 2021. By 2023, she was leading client projects. In 2024, she became our youngest Team Lead."

**Estimated Impact:** 25-35% increase in retention. Candidates see long-term opportunity.

#### 8. Implement Basic ATS or Recruitment Pipeline
**Why:** Manual recruitment doesn't scale and creates poor candidate experience.

**Actions:**
- Evaluate ATS options:
  - Simple: BambooHR, Workable (£100-200/month)
  - Mid-range: Greenhouse, Lever (£300-500/month)
  - Enterprise: Taleo, iCIMS (probably overkill for your size)
- Implement chosen system
- Integrate with careers page for applications
- Set up automated workflows:
  - Application received email
  - Application under review (after 5 days)
  - Rejection emails (personalized templates)
  - Interview invitation emails
  - Follow-up reminders for hiring team
- Create candidate pipeline stages: Applied → Reviewed → Phone Screen → Interview → Offer → Hired
- Track time-to-hire and source metrics

**Estimated Impact:** 50-60% improvement in recruitment efficiency. Better candidate experience.

### Medium Priority (90-180 Days)

#### 9. Build Content Marketing for Recruitment
**Why:** Passive candidates (often the best) need nurturing over time.

**Actions:**
- Create recruitment blog or "Life at Solutions Delivered" section:
  - Employee spotlight interviews
  - Behind-the-scenes content
  - Project case studies (from employee perspective)
  - Career growth stories
  - Day-in-the-life posts
  - "What we learned this month" roundups
- Publish 2-4 posts per month
- Share on LinkedIn company page and encourage employees to share
- Add "Join our talent newsletter" signup to careers page
- Send quarterly talent newsletter with: company updates, new openings, team spotlights, industry insights

**Estimated Impact:** 2-3x increase in passive candidate applications. Builds talent pipeline.

#### 10. Create Video Content
**Why:** Video builds connection and trust faster than text.

**Actions:**
- Film "About Our Company" video (2-3 minutes):
  - Founder or leadership speaking about mission and values
  - Clips of team working, collaborating, celebrating
  - Client testimonials about working with your team
  - Office tour (if applicable) or remote work setup examples
- Film employee testimonial videos (3-5 people, 60-90 seconds each):
  - Why did you join?
  - What's surprised you most?
  - What's a typical day like?
  - What do you love about working here?
- Film "Day in the Life" videos for different roles
- Add to Careers page, About page, and LinkedIn
- Host on YouTube for discoverability

**Estimated Impact:** 40-50% increase in time spent on careers page. Higher emotional connection.

#### 11. Develop Employee Referral Program
**Why:** Employee referrals are highest quality hires with best retention.

**Actions:**
- Create structured referral program:
  - £500-£1,000 bonus for successful referrals (paid after 6 months employment)
  - Make it easy: Simple referral form, regular reminders about open roles
  - Track referral sources in ATS
- Promote program internally and on careers page
- Recognize top referrers
- Add section to careers page: "Know someone great? We offer £500 referral bonuses!"

**Estimated Impact:** 30-40% of hires could come from referrals (industry benchmark). Lower cost per hire.

#### 12. Build LinkedIn Employer Brand Presence
**Why:** LinkedIn is THE platform for professional recruiting.

**Actions:**
- Optimize LinkedIn Company Page:
  - Complete all profile fields
  - Add compelling company description
  - Upload team photos and culture content
  - List all employees with correct job titles
  - Ensure company logo matches website
- Post regularly (2-3x per week):
  - New job openings
  - Employee spotlights
  - Company milestones
  - Industry insights
  - Behind-the-scenes content
- Encourage employees to:
  - Update profiles with company info
  - Engage with company posts
  - Share job openings
  - Write recommendations for each other
- Use LinkedIn Recruiter for active sourcing
- Link to careers page from all LinkedIn content

**Estimated Impact:** 3-5x increase in candidate discovery. LinkedIn is where professionals look first.

### Long-Term Strategic (6-12+ Months)

#### 13. Launch Graduate/Early Career Program
Structure formal entry-level program with clear training path, mentorship, and cohort experience.

#### 14. Create Employee Advocacy Program
Turn employees into brand ambassadors with guidelines, content suggestions, and recognition for sharing company content.

#### 15. Develop Internship Program
Create structured internship opportunities for university students. Builds future talent pipeline.

#### 16. Build Employer Brand Awards Strategy
Apply for "Best Places to Work" awards, get Glassdoor reviews, pursue industry recognition. Use in recruitment marketing.

#### 17. Create Candidate Nurture Campaigns
Build automated email sequences for talent pool members: monthly updates, relevant content, new opportunities.
