# Website Review: Solutions Delivered
## Potential Employee / Job Applicant Perspective

**Reviewer:** Jessica Moore
**Role:** Business Analyst (5 years experience)
**Date:** November 20, 2025
**Review Focus:** Career opportunities, company culture, employer branding

---

## Executive Summary

As a Business Analyst with 5 years of experience actively exploring new opportunities, I approached the Solutions Delivered website hoping to gain insight into their company culture, working environment, and career opportunities. While the website demonstrates professional polish and clear service offerings for clients, it falls significantly short in addressing the needs of potential employees like myself.

The careers page exists and touches on some important themes like work-life balance and career growth, but it lacks the substance and authenticity I need to make an informed decision about pursuing employment here. There are no actual job openings listed, no employee testimonials, no team information, and critically, no salary transparency. The page feels more like a placeholder than a genuine recruitment tool.

**Overall Impression:** The company has clearly invested in their client-facing brand but seems to have neglected their employer brand. For someone like me who researches companies thoroughly before applying, this raises questions about whether they're actually hiring and how serious they are about attracting top talent.

---

## Strengths

### 1. Careers Page Exists
At least there's a dedicated careers page at `/careers/`, which is more than many small consultancies offer. It shows they've thought about recruitment, even if the execution is incomplete.

### 2. Clear Company Values
The About page articulates three core values (Integrity, Collaboration, Excellence) which gives me some sense of what the company stands for. These align with what I'm looking for in an employer.

### 3. Benefits Mentioned
The careers page lists six compelling benefits:
- Challenging Work
- Continuous Learning
- Collaborative Culture
- Meaningful Impact
- Work-Life Balance
- Career Growth

These hit the right notes for what I care about, showing they understand what professionals like me want.

### 4. Transparent Work Arrangements
The "How We Work" page clearly states they're "Remote-first, UK-based" with flexible scheduling and on-site visits available for local clients. This transparency about working arrangements is exactly what I need to know.

### 5. What They Look For
The careers page outlines what they seek in candidates:
- Experience and Expertise
- Problem-Solving Skills
- Communication Excellence
- Collaborative Mindset

This helps me self-assess whether I'd be a good fit.

### 6. Well-Designed, Accessible Website
The site is clean, professional, WCAG 2.2 compliant, and easy to navigate. This suggests attention to quality and detail, which reflects well on the company's work standards.

### 7. Clear Engagement Process
The "How We Work" page outlines a transparent four-step process with realistic expectations about communication, delivery, and pricing. If this applies to how they manage internally, it's promising.

---

## Weaknesses

### 1. No Actual Job Openings
**Critical Issue:** The careers page has no current job listings. Instead, it says "We're always looking for talented individuals" with a generic "Get in Touch" button. This is a massive red flag. Are they actually hiring? Is this just for show? It feels like they haven't updated their careers page in months—or worse, they're not serious about recruitment.

### 2. Zero Employee Testimonials
There are no employee stories, quotes, or testimonials anywhere on the site. Every careers page I research has this. Without hearing from real employees, I have no way to validate whether the "collaborative culture" and "work-life balance" claims are genuine or just marketing fluff.

### 3. No Team Information
I can't see who I'd be working with. There's no "Meet the Team" section, no leadership bios, no photos of actual employees. The entire site uses generic design elements instead of real people. This makes the company feel impersonal and raises questions: How big is the team? Who would I report to? What's the leadership like?

### 4. No Salary Information
**Major Pain Point:** As someone who values transparency, the complete absence of salary ranges is frustrating. My persona quote says it perfectly: "If you can't tell me the salary range upfront, I'm not interested." I'm not going through an entire application process just to find out the role pays below market rate.

### 5. Missing Benefits Details
While they mention "work-life balance" and "career growth," there's no information about:
- Health insurance or benefits packages
- Pension contributions
- Annual leave allowance
- Professional development budget
- Training or certification support
- Equipment provided for remote work
- Parental leave policies

These details matter when evaluating total compensation.

### 6. No Diversity and Inclusion Statement
There's nothing about D&I, equal opportunities, or inclusive workplace practices. For a company founded in 2025, this feels like a significant oversight. It makes me wonder if they've thought about workplace culture beyond surface-level values.

### 7. Vague Career Progression
"Career Growth" is listed as a benefit with the description "Clear career progression paths with opportunities to advance and take on leadership roles." But what does that actually mean? What are the levels? How long does progression typically take? What's required to advance? It's too vague to be useful.

### 8. No Day-to-Day Work Insights
I can't get a sense of what working here would actually be like. What projects would I work on? What does a typical week look like? What tools and methodologies do they use? Without this, it's impossible to assess if the work would be interesting and fulfilling.

### 9. Generic Stock Content Feel
While there aren't actual stock photos, the complete absence of real people, real projects, or real employee stories makes everything feel templated and impersonal. It's all polish, no substance.

### 10. No Company Size or Growth Information
Is this a 5-person startup or a 50-person established firm? Are they growing? Stable? This information would help me assess stability and opportunity.

## Specific Issues

### Issue 1: Inactive Careers Section
**Page:** `/careers/`
**Problem:** The "Current Opportunities" section says there are no specific openings but encourages contact anyway. This feels passive and unclear. If they're hiring, they should say what for. If they're not hiring, why have a careers page?
**Impact:** Makes me question if they're actually recruiting or just keeping a careers page for appearances.

### Issue 2: Missing Application Process
**Page:** `/careers/`
**Problem:** There's no clear application process. Do I send a CV? Is there an application form? What's the timeline? The only CTA is "Get in Touch" which links to a generic contact form.
**Impact:** Creates friction and uncertainty. I don't know what to expect if I reach out.

### Issue 3: No Glassdoor or Review Platform Links
**Location:** Entire site
**Problem:** There are no links to Glassdoor, LinkedIn company page, or any third-party review platforms where I could read employee reviews.
**Impact:** I have to do extra research to find authentic employee perspectives, and the company misses an opportunity to address or showcase positive reviews.

### Issue 4: Benefits Are Listed But Not Detailed
**Page:** `/careers/`
**Problem:** Benefits like "Continuous Learning" mention "access to training, certifications, and professional development opportunities" but provide no specifics. How much budget? What certifications? Are there examples?
**Impact:** Feels like marketing copy rather than genuine information I can evaluate.

### Issue 5: No Remote Work Policy Details
**Page:** `/how-we-work/`
**Problem:** While it says "Remote-first," there's no detail about expectations. Fully remote? Hybrid with quarterly meetups? Required office days? What equipment is provided?
**Impact:** I can't assess if their remote arrangement matches my needs.

### Issue 6: Generic "Get Started" CTAs
**Location:** Multiple pages
**Problem:** All CTAs lead to the same generic contact form. There's no dedicated "Apply Now" or "Join Our Team" pathway with relevant fields for job applications.
**Impact:** Makes the recruitment process feel like an afterthought rather than a strategic priority.

## Recommendations

### Priority 1: Add Real Job Openings (Or Remove False Expectations)
**Action:** Either:
- Post actual job openings with detailed descriptions, requirements, and salary ranges, OR
- Remove the careers page entirely if not actively hiring, OR
- Be explicit: "We're not currently hiring but welcome speculative applications" with a timeline for when to check back

**Why:** The current approach wastes job seekers' time and damages credibility.

### Priority 2: Add Employee Testimonials and Stories
**Action:** Feature 3-5 employee testimonials with:
- Real names and roles
- Photos (if they consent)
- Genuine quotes about what it's like to work here
- Specific examples of projects, growth, or culture

**Why:** This is the #1 thing I look for to validate company claims about culture and work environment.

### Priority 3: Create a "Meet the Team" Section
**Action:** Add profiles for at least the leadership/senior team with:
- Photos
- Backgrounds and expertise
- What they're working on
- Why they joined Solutions Delivered

**Why:** Helps me understand who I'd be working with and makes the company feel real and approachable.

### Priority 4: Add Salary Transparency
**Action:** For each role (current or typical), include:
- Salary range or day rate
- Experience level required
- Total compensation information

**Why:** Salary transparency is increasingly expected and saves everyone time. It shows confidence and fairness.

### Priority 5: Detail the Benefits Package
**Action:** Create a comprehensive benefits section covering:
- Health and wellness benefits
- Pension/retirement contributions
- Holiday allowance and leave policies
- Professional development budget and examples
- Remote work equipment and setup
- Any other perks or benefits

**Why:** I need this information to compare total compensation with other opportunities.

### Priority 6: Add Diversity and Inclusion Statement
**Action:** Create a clear D&I statement covering:
- Commitment to equal opportunity
- Inclusive hiring practices
- Support for diverse team members
- Any D&I initiatives or employee resource groups

**Why:** Shows modern values and helps diverse candidates feel welcome.

### Priority 7: Show Real Work and Projects
**Action:** Add case studies or examples from an employee perspective:
- Types of projects consultants work on
- Technologies and methodologies used
- Day-to-day work examples
- Client industries and challenges tackled

**Why:** Helps me assess if the work would be interesting and aligned with my skills and interests.

### Priority 8: Clarify Career Progression
**Action:** Create a clear career framework showing:
- Role levels (e.g., Consultant → Senior Consultant → Principal)
- Typical progression timelines
- What's required to advance
- Professional development support at each level

**Why:** I need to understand growth potential and what success looks like here.

### Priority 9: Add Company Size and Growth Information
**Action:** Include basic information:
- Team size
- Years in operation
- Growth trajectory
- Client base size

**Why:** Helps me assess stability and opportunity.

### Priority 10: Create a Dedicated Application Process
**Action:** Build a proper job application flow with:
- Application form specific to job applications
- Clear fields for CV, cover letter, portfolio
- Timeline expectations (e.g., "We respond within 5 business days")
- Next steps in the process

**Why:** Makes applying easier and shows professionalism in recruitment.

## Final Thoughts

As Jessica Moore, I'm left feeling uncertain and underwhelmed by this careers section. The website does an excellent job presenting Solutions Delivered to potential clients, but it completely fails to present them as an employer worth considering.

The company clearly has some positive attributes—they value work-life balance, they're remote-first, they claim to invest in professional development. But without evidence, testimonials, or even a single job posting, I can't verify any of it.

**My honest reaction:** I probably wouldn't apply here based on this website alone. The lack of job openings, salary information, and employee testimonials sends a message that either:
1. They're not serious about hiring
2. They haven't invested in employer branding
3. They have something to hide

None of those are appealing to a selective candidate like me.

**What would make me reconsider:** If I saw this company recommended by someone in my network, I might reach out despite the website's shortcomings. But as a cold prospect researching companies? This wouldn't make my shortlist.

**Bottom line:** Solutions Delivered needs to decide if they're serious about recruitment. If they are, they need to completely overhaul their careers section with real jobs, real people, real compensation details, and real evidence of their claimed culture and values. Until then, they're missing out on talented candidates who do their homework before applying.
