# Partnership / B2B Manager Review - Solutions Delivered Website

**Reviewer:** Catherine Walsh
**Role:** Head of Strategic Partnerships
**Company Type:** B2B Software Company
**Date:** November 20, 2025
**Perspective:** Evaluating partnership opportunities and partnership readiness

---

## Executive Summary

As Head of Strategic Partnerships with 18 years of experience in business development, I approached the Solutions Delivered website looking to assess potential partnership opportunities and evaluate their readiness as a strategic partner. My evaluation focuses on whether this organization could be a reliable, complementary partner for our B2B software company and our clients.

**Overall Assessment:** The website presents a professionally designed consultancy with clear service offerings and strong technical credentials (WCAG 2.2 compliance, Laravel expertise). However, it lacks critical partnership infrastructure and company transparency that would enable me to evaluate them as a potential strategic partner. While their services appear complementary to software implementation and organizational change initiatives, the absence of partnership information, case studies, company size indicators, and track record makes it impossible to assess partnership viability or present them to our executive team.

**Overall Rating: 4.5/10**

This is a well-built website that effectively communicates their services to direct clients but completely misses the mark for partnership development and B2B relationship building.

## Strengths

### 1. Clear Service Differentiation
The four core service areas are well-defined and immediately clear:
- Web Development (Laravel-based systems)
- Service Management
- Project Management
- Business Change

This clarity helps me quickly identify where our services might complement theirs. Their focus on process optimization and organizational change aligns well with software implementation projects where clients need change management support alongside technical deployment.

### 2. Professional Presentation and Quality Standards
The website itself demonstrates their technical capabilities:
- WCAG 2.2 compliance shows they understand accessibility standards
- Professional design and user experience
- Clear commitment to quality ("no-bloat philosophy")
- Technical sophistication evident in the Laravel implementation

This level of professionalism gives me some confidence that they take their work seriously and maintain high standards.

### 3. Complementary Service Offerings
Their services don't directly compete with typical software solutions:
- They focus on implementation, process design, and change management rather than software products
- Their expertise could fill gaps in our service delivery where clients need organizational support
- The combination of technical (web development) and consultative (change management, project management) services suggests they understand both sides of business transformation

### 4. Direct Collaboration Model
The emphasis on direct team collaboration and removal of intermediaries is appealing:
- "Direct access—no account managers or layers of bureaucracy"
- Working directly with the team could streamline joint delivery
- Transparent communication approach suggests they'd be straightforward partners

### 5. UK-Based with Clear Geographic Focus
Their UK location is clearly stated, which helps with:
- Understanding their market and reach
- Assessing geographic fit with our client base
- Considering compliance and data protection alignment

This is the most significant weakness for my evaluation. There is:
- No partnership program page or information
- No indication they work with partners at all
- No business development contact for partnerships
- No partner portal or resources
- No information about how partners might work with them
- No framework for referral, reseller, or integration partnerships

For a partnership professional, this absence sends a clear message: partnerships are not part of their current business model. This makes it impossible to assess whether they're even interested in strategic partnerships or have the infrastructure to support them.

### 2. No Case Studies or Proof of Capability
I cannot find a single case study, client testimonial, or portfolio example:
- No evidence of successful projects
- No client names or industries served (even anonymized examples)
- No metrics or outcomes from previous work
- No testimonials from satisfied clients
- No partner references or existing partnership examples

Without these, I cannot:
- Validate their claimed expertise

- Evaluate their client satisfaction levels

- Unclear capacity to handle multiple engagements simultaneously

The direct collaboration messaging and lack of team information suggests this might be a very small operation (possibly solo), but I'm left guessing. This uncertainty creates significant risk for partnership consideration.

### 4. No Professional Credentials or Recognition
I cannot find any indicators of professional standing:
- No industry certifications (ITIL, PRINCE2, PMP, etc.)
- No professional memberships or affiliations
- No awards or recognition
- No published thought leadership or expertise demonstrations
- No evidence of specialist accreditations

For a consultancy offering service management and project management services, the absence of recognized frameworks and certifications is concerning.

### 5. Missing Technical Integration Information
For potential technical partnerships, there's no information about:

- Integration capabilities with other systems
- Technical partnership frameworks
- White-label or co-branding options
- System interoperability approaches

Their web development focus on Laravel suggests technical sophistication, but there's no indication of how their solutions might integrate with partner systems or whether they have partnership-ready technical infrastructure.

### 6. No Track Record Indicators
Basic business credibility markers are absent:
- Years in business not stated
- Number of clients served (even approximately)
- Industries served with any detail
- Project completion rates or success metrics
- Longevity indicators that would demonstrate stability

For partnership evaluation, I need to assess whether this organization will be around in 2-3 years when our joint clients might need ongoing support.

### 7. Absence of Existing Partnership Evidence
There are no signs of current partnerships:
- No partner logos or references
- No technology partner acknowledgments (beyond Laravel/Tailwind)
- No collaborative delivery models described
- No indication they understand partnership dynamics
- No partner testimonials about working with them

This suggests they either don't pursue partnerships or haven't structured their business to support them.

## Specific Issues

### Partnership Positioning Issues

**Issue:** Generic client-focused messaging with no B2B or partnership orientation
- All content addresses direct clients only
- No acknowledgment of channel partners, referral sources, or strategic partners
- Value propositions framed entirely around direct engagement
- No partnership benefits or value propositions articulated

**Impact:** Partnership professionals like myself cannot see how we'd fit into their business model or what value partnership would create. There's no "in" for starting a partnership conversation beyond generic business inquiry.

**Issue:** No business development contact differentiation
- Single contact form that appears designed for end clients
- No dedicated partnership inquiry mechanism
- No indication of who handles business development versus client delivery
- Unclear who would be the right person to discuss strategic partnerships

**Impact:** I'd have to use the same contact method as a direct client, which doesn't signal that I'm approaching them about a strategic relationship. This creates friction and suggests they're not set up to handle partnership inquiries differently from sales leads.

### Company Transparency Issues

**Issue:** Vague company information
- "Solutions Delivered" is a generic name that's difficult to research
- No company registration numbers or legal entity details
- No "About the Team" or leadership information
- No company history or origin story
- Careers page exists but doesn't mention current team size or roles

**Impact:** I cannot conduct proper due diligence or assess their stability and capacity. Our legal and compliance teams would struggle to vet them as a partner. The lack of transparency raises questions about what they might be hiding or whether they're just very small and trying to appear larger.

**Issue:** Missing capacity and capability indicators
- No indication of how many concurrent projects they can handle
- No team specializations or role descriptions
- No mention of associates, contractors, or flexible capacity models
- Unclear whether they can scale to meet demand

**Impact:** For partnership planning, I need to understand if they could handle referrals from multiple partners simultaneously or if capacity constraints would create delivery issues that reflect poorly on referring partners.

### Validation and Credibility Issues

**Issue:** No third-party validation
- No client testimonials or reviews
- No independent ratings or assessments
- No media mentions or press coverage
- No industry recognition or awards
- No case studies with measurable outcomes

**Impact:** Everything on the site is self-reported claims without validation. For partnership consideration, I need independent verification that they deliver on their promises. Without this, presenting them to our executive team or clients requires taking a significant reputation risk.

**Issue:** Missing technical credentials
- Laravel expertise claimed but no Laravel certification or partnership
- WCAG 2.2 compliance stated but no certification or audit reports
- No mention of ISO standards, security certifications, or compliance frameworks
- No evidence of continuing professional development

**Impact:** Their technical claims may be entirely valid, but I have no way to verify them. For clients in regulated industries or with specific compliance needs, this lack of credentials could be disqualifying.

## Recommendations

### Critical Priority (Must Address for Partnership Viability)

**1. Create Partnership Program Infrastructure**
- Develop a dedicated partnership page outlining your approach to strategic relationships
- Define clear partnership models: referral, reseller, integration, co-marketing
- Establish partner requirements, benefits, and expectations
- Create a dedicated partnership inquiry process separate from client leads
- Assign a business development contact for partnership discussions

**Rationale:** Without basic partnership infrastructure, potential partners will assume you're not interested in or equipped for partnership relationships. This is the foundation for all partnership development.

**2. Develop Case Studies and Proof Points**
- Create 3-5 detailed case studies showing successful project delivery
- Include client testimonials (even anonymized if confidentiality is required)
- Show metrics, outcomes, and measurable business impact
- Demonstrate your expertise across different industries or problem types
- Include both technical projects (web development) and consulting engagements

**Rationale:** Case studies are essential for partnership evaluation. I need evidence I can show our executive team and clients to validate your capabilities and reduce partnership risk.

**3. Enhance Company Transparency**
- Clearly state your company structure (limited company, partnership, sole trader)
- Provide company registration details for verification
- Share company history: when founded, growth trajectory, milestones
- Indicate team size or capacity (even as ranges: "team of 5-10," "network of associates")
- Include leadership profiles or team information showing expertise areas

**Rationale:** Partnership requires mutual transparency. Hiding basic company information creates mistrust and prevents the due diligence necessary for partnership approval. Being open about your size (even if small) allows partners to understand how to work with you effectively.

### High Priority (Significantly Improve Partnership Appeal)

**4. Display Professional Credentials**
- List relevant certifications (ITIL, PRINCE2, PMP, Agile certifications)
- Show professional memberships (BCS, APM, CMI, etc.)
- Include any industry awards or recognition received
- Display technology partnerships (Laravel, if applicable)
- Show WCAG certification or audit reports if available

**Rationale:** Credentials provide shorthand validation of expertise and reduce the need for extensive vetting. They also help partners justify partnerships to their own organizations.

**5. Create Track Record Indicators**
- State years in business clearly
- Provide aggregate statistics (number of projects delivered, clients served)
- List industries or sectors you've worked in successfully
- Show project types and complexity levels you handle
- Include any long-term client relationships (without naming if confidential)

**Rationale:** Partners need to assess your stability and experience level. Even if you're relatively new, being transparent about your track record allows partners to make informed decisions rather than guessing.

**6. Develop Integration and Technical Partnership Information**
- Document your approach to system integration
- Describe API capabilities if applicable to your web development work
- Outline technical partnership frameworks for software integrations
- Explain your approach to working with existing client technology stacks
- Consider white-label or co-branded service delivery options

**Rationale:** For technology partners, understanding technical integration possibilities is crucial. This information opens doors to deeper partnership models beyond simple referrals.

### Medium Priority (Enhance Partnership Experience)

**7. Build Partner Resources Section**
- Create partner portal or resource area (can be password-protected initially)
- Develop partnership collateral: one-pagers, service descriptions, joint value propositions
- Provide partner FAQs addressing common questions
- Share partnership success stories as they develop
- Create co-marketing templates or materials

**Rationale:** Once partnerships exist, having resources ready makes it easier for partners to promote and work with you. Starting to build this infrastructure signals partnership readiness.

**8. Show Existing Partnerships (If Any)**
- Display logos of technology partners or professional affiliations
- Acknowledge strategic relationships that enhance your offerings
- Share partner testimonials about collaboration experiences
- Demonstrate your ability to work in partnership contexts
- Highlight complementary services through partnerships

**Rationale:** Evidence of existing partnerships validates that you understand and can effectively operate in partnership models. It reduces perceived risk for new partnerships.

**9. Articulate Partnership Value Propositions**
- Explain what partners gain from working with you
- Describe your ideal partner profile
- Outline how partnerships benefit end clients
- Show how you support partner success
- Quantify partnership benefits where possible

**Rationale:** Partners need to understand "what's in it for them" beyond the obvious. Clear value propositions help partners justify the relationship internally and prioritize working with you.

### Lower Priority (Long-term Partnership Development)

**10. Develop Thought Leadership**
- Publish case studies, articles, or insights demonstrating expertise
- Share knowledge through blog posts or resources
- Speak at industry events or webinars
- Contribute to professional communities
- Build your reputation as subject matter experts

**Rationale:** Thought leadership builds credibility and makes you more attractive as a partner. It also provides content partners can share, extending your reach.

**11. Consider Formal Partner Programs**
- As you grow, develop tiered partnership programs (silver/gold/platinum)
- Create partner benefits commensurate with partnership level
- Establish partner training or enablement programs
- Build partner certification paths for deeper alignment
- Develop partner account management approaches

**Rationale:** Formal programs create structure that makes partnerships scalable and sustainable. This becomes important as partnership volume grows.

## Direct Advice to Solutions Delivered

I'm writing this as if speaking directly to your team, because I believe you have partnership potential that's being squandered by the gaps in your website:

**You're invisible to partnership professionals.** Your website is designed entirely for direct clients, which means strategic partners looking for complementary service providers will pass right by you. We need different information than end clients need, and we evaluate potential partners through a completely different lens.

**Your lack of transparency creates risk we can't accept.** Partnership involves mutual reputation risk. When we refer a client to you or propose joint delivery, our reputation is on the line. Without case studies, credentials, company information, or track record indicators, the risk profile is simply too high for us to take seriously.

**You might be excellent, but we can't tell.** Everything on your site could be completely true—you might deliver outstanding work, have decades of experience, and maintain the highest professional standards. But without validation or proof, we have to assume you're unproven or hiding something. In partnership development, silence creates negative assumptions.

**Small isn't the problem; hidden is.** If you're a boutique consultancy or even a solo consultant with a network of associates, that's potentially fine for many partnership models. The issue isn't your size—it's that you won't tell us what your size or structure is. Partners can work with small organizations effectively, but we need to know what we're working with to structure relationships appropriately.

**Partnership could accelerate your growth.** Based on your services, you're a natural complement to software companies, systems integrators, digital agencies, and larger consultancies who need specialists. Building partnership infrastructure could open significant new business channels—but only if potential partners can evaluate and engage with you.

**Start with the basics.** You don't need a sophisticated partnership program immediately. Start by:
1. Adding 2-3 case studies with real outcomes
2. Stating clearly who you are, how big your team is, and how long you've been operating
3. Listing your credentials and certifications
4. Creating one page explaining your interest in partnerships and how to contact you about them

These four elements would move you from "impossible to evaluate" to "worth a conversation."

**The direct collaboration message cuts both ways.** Your emphasis on direct team collaboration is appealing, but it also signals limited scalability. Partners need to know you can handle multiple engagements, have backup for key resources, and won't become a bottleneck. Address capacity and scalability explicitly.

**WCAG 2.2 compliance is a partnership asset.** You've built an accessible website, which demonstrates capability that's increasingly valuable. But you don't highlight this as a service offering sufficiently, and you don't show certification or audit evidence. This could be a key differentiator for partnerships—lean into it with proof.

## Conclusion

Solutions Delivered has created a professional, well-designed website that effectively serves direct client acquisition. However, it completely fails to serve partnership development or B2B relationship building. The absence of partnership infrastructure, company transparency, validation mechanisms, and proof points makes it impossible to evaluate them as a strategic partner, regardless of their actual capabilities.

This represents a significant missed opportunity. Their service mix—combining technical web development with business consulting in service management, project management, and organizational change—is exactly the type of complementary offering that B2B software companies, systems integrators, and digital agencies actively seek as partners. With the right partnership infrastructure and transparency, they could open substantial new business channels.

My recommendation is clear: **Address the critical gaps before pursuing partnership relationships.** Until basic company information, case studies, credentials, and partnership frameworks are in place, partnership professionals will be unable to seriously consider Solutions Delivered as a partner, no matter how good their work may be.

If these foundational elements were in place, I would be interested in exploring a partnership conversation. The service fit is there, the professional presentation suggests competence, and the UK focus aligns with our market. But without the ability to conduct proper evaluation and due diligence, I cannot move forward or recommend them to our partnership committee.

**For direct clients seeking consultancy services, this website likely serves its purpose well. For partnership development, it's essentially a non-starter in its current form.**
