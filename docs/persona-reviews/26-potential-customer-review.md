# Persona Review: Potential Customer
**Reviewer:** Sarah Jenkins, Head of Transformation
**Company:** Growing services company (200 employees)
**Date:** November 20, 2025
**Review Focus:** Value proposition, trust signals, case studies, and decision-making information

---

## Executive Summary

I've been tasked with finding a partner to help our 200-employee services company with organizational change and process optimization. Solutions Delivered appeared promising at first glance with its clean, professional website and clear service offerings. However, after a thorough evaluation, I found significant gaps that make it difficult to justify this investment to my executives or confidently move forward with an engagement.

**The Good:** The website is professional, accessible, and clearly articulates services. The value proposition is straightforward, and I appreciate the transparency about their collaborative approach.

**The Bad:** Critical decision-making information is missing. There are no case studies, no client testimonials, no concrete pricing, no team credentials, and no way to understand if they've successfully helped companies like mine.

**Overall Rating: 5/10** - While the website looks professional and the services are well-described, the absence of social proof and concrete evidence makes this feel risky. I need more than marketing copy to make a six-figure investment decision.

---

## Strengths

### 1. Clear Value Proposition
✓ I immediately understood what Solutions Delivered does and who they serve. The homepage hero section is crystal clear: "Tailored IT solutions for service management, web development, and business transformation." No confusion about their focus.

### 2. Well-Structured Service Descriptions
The Solutions page provides comprehensive detail on each of their four core services:
- Web Development (Laravel-based systems)
- Service Management (process optimization)
- Project Management (risk mitigation, delivery oversight)
- Business Change (transformation leadership)

Each service has clear descriptions of what's included, which helps me understand the scope.

### 3. Process Transparency
The "How We Work" page is excellent. It outlines:
- Their 4-step engagement process (Initial Discussion → Proposal & Planning → Collaborative Delivery → Handover & Support)
- What to expect regarding communication (24-hour response time, direct access)
- Pricing approach (fixed-price or day-rate, transparent, no hidden costs)
- Working arrangements (remote-first, UK-based, flexible scheduling)

This transparency is refreshing and addresses many of my initial questions about logistics.

### 4. Accessibility Commitment
The emphasis on WCAG 2.2 compliance throughout the site demonstrates a commitment to quality and inclusive design. As someone who values accessibility, this is important to me.

### 5. User Experience
The website itself is well-designed, mobile-friendly, easy to navigate, and loads quickly. The contact form is simple and not overly demanding. Trust indicators are present on multiple pages.

### 6. Direct Collaboration Model
I appreciate the emphasis on working directly with the team without layers of account managers. This suggests I'll get actual expertise, not sales people.

---

## Weaknesses

### 1. **CRITICAL: No Case Studies**
This is my biggest concern. I need to see examples of companies like mine that Solutions Delivered has helped successfully. What were the challenges? What was implemented? What were the outcomes?

Without case studies, I have no way to:
- Verify they've done work similar to what I need
- Understand the complexity of projects they can handle
- See concrete results and ROI
- Compare their approach to competitors who DO show case studies

**Impact:** This alone might disqualify them from consideration. My executives will ask, "Who have they helped?" and I'll have no answer.

### 2. **CRITICAL: No Client Testimonials**
Zero social proof from satisfied customers. Not a single quote, testimonial, or client reference. This makes me extremely nervous.

Questions I can't answer:
- Are current clients happy with the work?
- Do they deliver on their promises?
- What do clients say about working with them?
- Are there any red flags I should know about?

**Impact:** In my research phase, I'm comparing three providers. The other two have glowing testimonials on their websites. This puts Solutions Delivered at a significant disadvantage.

### 3. **CRITICAL: No Pricing Information**
While they mention "transparent pricing" and talk about fixed-price vs. day-rate models, there are NO actual numbers. Not even ranges or starting points.

I need ballpark figures to:
- Understand if they're even in our budget range
- Get preliminary approval from finance before investing time in discussions
- Compare value against competitors
- Determine if their "no bloat" philosophy actually translates to cost savings

The "How We Work" page says pricing is transparent, but then provides no transparency whatsoever. This is frustrating and makes me question if there's something to hide.

**Impact:** As the persona profile says: "Don't make me dig for pricing. If it's 'contact for quote,' I'm moving on." I'm on the fence about whether to even reach out.

### 4. No Team Credentials
Who will actually be working with me? What are their:
- Qualifications and certifications?
- Years of experience?
- Areas of expertise?
- Background and track record?

The About page mentions "experienced team" and "years of real-world experience" but provides no specifics. No bios, no LinkedIn profiles, no credentials.

**Impact:** I need to justify this decision to our CEO. "Experienced team" won't cut it. I need names, credentials, and proof of expertise.

### 5. No Industry Examples or Company Size Targeting
The website doesn't specify:
- What industries they serve
- What size companies they typically work with
- Whether they have experience with services companies like mine

Without this context, I don't know if they're the right fit. Do they work with 200-employee companies, or are they better suited to startups or enterprises?

### 6. Limited Trust Signals
Beyond WCAG compliance, there are no:
- Professional certifications (ITIL, PMP, Prince2, etc.)
- Industry awards or recognition
- Professional associations or memberships
- Client logos (even if anonymized)
- Media mentions or press coverage

For a consultancy asking for significant investment, this lack of credibility markers is concerning.

### 7. No Educational Resources
No blog, no whitepapers, no guides, no thought leadership content. Educational resources would:
- Demonstrate expertise and thought leadership
- Help me understand their methodology in depth
- Build trust through valuable content
- Show they're active and current in their field

### 8. No Competitive Differentiation
While the website mentions their "no-bloat philosophy," "direct collaboration," and "tailored solutions," it doesn't clearly explain how they differ from competitors. What makes them better or different from the dozen other consultancies I could hire?

### 9. Vague Service Management Description
The Service Management section is the weakest of the four services. It mentions "process optimization" and "working directly with teams" but lacks specificity about:
- Their methodology (do they use ITIL, Six Sigma, Lean, etc.?)
- What tools they use
- Typical engagement length
- Expected outcomes

For someone specifically looking for process optimization help, this is too vague.

## Specific Issues

### Issue 1: Mismatch Between Promise and Evidence
**Location:** Throughout site
**Problem:** The site repeatedly promises "tailored solutions," "proven results," and "trusted delivery," but provides zero evidence to back up these claims.
**Recommendation:** Add case studies showing tailored approaches, metrics demonstrating results, and testimonials establishing trust.

### Issue 2: "Get Started" Feels Premature
**Location:** Multiple prominent CTAs
**Problem:** I'm being asked to "Get Started" before I have enough information to make an informed decision. The CTA assumes I'm ready to commit, but I'm still in research mode.
**Recommendation:** Offer multiple CTAs at different stages: "View Case Studies," "Download Services Guide," "Schedule Brief Call," "Request Pricing," then "Get Started."

### Issue 3: Contact Form Lacks Context
**Location:** /get-started
**Problem:** The form asks for basic information but doesn't let me specify: service interest, budget range, urgency, company size, or specific challenges. This means the initial consultation will waste time on basic discovery.
**Recommendation:** Add optional fields to help them tailor their response and show understanding of my needs.

### Issue 4: No Risk Mitigation
**Location:** Missing throughout
**Problem:** I'm risk-averse about making the wrong choice. Nothing on the site addresses my concerns about commitment, contracts, or what happens if things don't work out.
**Recommendation:** Add a FAQ section addressing: engagement minimums, contract terms, pilot projects, exit clauses, and what happens if we're not satisfied.

### Issue 5: Generic "About Us" Content
**Location:** /about
**Problem:** The About page has nice mission/vision/values content, but it's generic. It could apply to any consultancy. No personality, no specific origin story, no unique point of view.
**Recommendation:** Tell the story of WHY Solutions Delivered exists, WHO is behind it, and WHAT makes your perspective unique.

### Issue 6: No Next Steps After Initial Contact
**Location:** /get-started
**Problem:** After I submit the form, what happens? The page mentions "24 hours" and "initial consultation" but doesn't explain the journey from inquiry to engagement.
**Recommendation:** Add a timeline showing: Inquiry → Review (24h) → Discovery Call (Week 1) → Proposal (Week 2) → Decision Point → Engagement Start.

## Recommendations

### Priority 1: Add Case Studies (CRITICAL)
**Why:** This is the single most important missing element. Without case studies, I cannot justify this decision.

**What to Include:**
- Client industry and size (can be anonymized: "200-employee financial services firm")
- The challenge/problem
- Your approach and solution
- Concrete results (metrics, outcomes, ROI)
- Timeline and scope
- Client quote (even if anonymous)

**Minimum:** 3-5 detailed case studies covering different services and industries.

### Priority 2: Add Client Testimonials (CRITICAL)
**Why:** Social proof is essential for building trust and confidence.

**What to Include:**
- At least 6-8 client testimonials
- Specific, detailed quotes (not generic "great work" statements)
- Client name, role, and company (or anonymous if necessary)
- Focus on outcomes, experience, and value delivered

### Priority 3: Provide Pricing Guidance (HIGH)
**Why:** Budget is a key decision factor. I need to know if you're in our range before investing time.

**What to Include:**
- Starting prices or typical project ranges
- Day rate if applicable
- Minimum engagement costs
- What affects pricing (scope, timeline, complexity)
- Example scenarios with ballpark costs

**Alternative:** If you won't publish exact pricing, at least say: "Projects typically range from £15,000-£100,000 depending on scope" or "Our day rate is competitive with market rates for senior consultants."

### Priority 4: Showcase Team Expertise (HIGH)
**Why:** I need to know who I'll be working with and whether they're qualified.

**What to Include:**
- Key team members with bios
- Relevant certifications and qualifications
- Years of experience and notable projects
- Areas of specialization
- LinkedIn profiles or contact options

### Priority 5: Add Industry & Company Size Context (MEDIUM)
**Why:** I need to know if you've worked with companies like mine.

**What to Include:**
- Industries served
- Typical company sizes (startups, SMEs, enterprises)
- Geographic focus
- Client types (B2B, B2C, public sector, etc.)

### Priority 6: Create Educational Resources (MEDIUM)
**Why:** Thought leadership builds trust and demonstrates expertise.

**What to Include:**
- Blog with regular posts on relevant topics
- Downloadable guides or whitepapers
- Methodology documentation
- Tools or templates
- Webinars or recorded presentations

### Priority 7: Add Trust Signals & Credentials (MEDIUM)
**Why:** Professional credibility matters when making significant investments.

**What to Include:**
- Professional certifications (ITIL, PMP, etc.)
- Industry awards or recognition
- Professional association memberships
- Client logos (if permission granted)
- Press mentions or publications

### Priority 8: Clarify Differentiation (LOW)
**Why:** I'm comparing multiple vendors and need clear reasons to choose you.

**What to Include:**
- Dedicated "Why Choose Us" or "What Makes Us Different" section
- Direct comparison to common alternatives
- Specific advantages of your approach
- Your unique methodology or process

### Priority 9: Add FAQ Section (LOW)
**Why:** Address common concerns and objections proactively.

**What to Include:**
- Engagement process and timeline
- Pricing structure
- Contract terms
- What happens if we're not satisfied
- Geographic limitations
- Availability and capacity

### Priority 10: Enhance Service Management Section (LOW)
**Why:** As someone specifically interested in process optimization, I need more detail.

**What to Include:**
- Specific methodologies used (ITIL, Lean, Six Sigma, etc.)
- Tools and frameworks
- Typical engagement structure
- Expected outcomes and metrics
- Industry-specific applications

## Overall Rating: 5/10

### Why This Rating?

**Foundation (3/5):** The website has solid bones. It's professional, accessible, well-designed, and clearly describes services. The value proposition is clear, and the process is transparent.

**Trust & Credibility (1/5):** This is where the website falls apart. Zero case studies, zero testimonials, no team credentials, no concrete evidence of past success. For a consultancy asking for significant investment, this is unacceptable. I need proof, not promises.

**Decision Support (1/5):** The website doesn't provide the information I need to make an informed decision. No pricing guidance, no industry examples, no comparison points, no clear differentiation. I'm left with more questions than answers.

### The Bottom Line

As Sarah Jenkins, I'm frustrated. This website looks professional and the services sound good, but I cannot justify moving forward without more information. In my comparison of three potential vendors, Solutions Delivered is currently in last place specifically because they haven't given me the evidence I need to build a business case.

**What would change my mind:**
1. 3-5 detailed case studies showing successful outcomes for companies similar to mine
2. At least 6-8 client testimonials with specific, measurable results
3. Ballpark pricing so I know we're in the right budget range
4. Team credentials showing relevant experience and qualifications
5. Some indication they've worked with services companies in the 100-300 employee range

**Until then:** I'll probably reach out to gather this information verbally, but they've lost the opportunity to make a strong first impression. If the other vendors on my shortlist provide better evidence on their websites, Solutions Delivered may not make it to the consultation phase.

### Key Quote from My Perspective

> "I want to believe in Solutions Delivered. The website is professional, the services align with my needs, and I like their collaborative approach. But I'm not spending six figures based on marketing copy. Show me the proof. Show me your work. Show me happy clients. Then we can talk."

## Conclusion

Solutions Delivered has built a solid foundation with a professional website and clear service offerings. However, for a potential customer like me who needs to justify a significant investment to executives, the website falls short in providing the critical decision-making information required.

The lack of case studies, testimonials, pricing guidance, and team credentials creates unnecessary risk and makes it difficult to move forward with confidence. Adding these elements should be the highest priority for improving conversion rates among potential customers in the consideration stage.

I'm leaving this website with the impression that Solutions Delivered might be good at what they do, but they haven't proven it to me yet. And in a competitive market where I have options, proof matters.

**Will I reach out?** Maybe. But they're not my first choice based on this website alone.

**Would I recommend them to a colleague?** Not yet. Not without more evidence.

**Would my CEO approve them?** Not without case studies and client references.
